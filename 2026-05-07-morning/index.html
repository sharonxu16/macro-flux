    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 6 6PM to May 7 8AM HKT Good morning.

    Overview

    Optimism that Washington and Tehran are moving toward a one-page memorandum to end their two-month war ignited a global risk-on rotation, sending oil nearly 8% lower and propelling equities from Tokyo to New York to new highs. The AI investment cycle accelerated in parallel: AMD’s blowout data-center forecast drove its stock up 19%, Nvidia committed up to $3.2 billion to Corning’s optical-fiber buildout, and Anthropic struck a compute deal for SpaceX’s Colossus‑1 facility while revealing an 80‑fold first‑quarter growth rate, reinforcing chip‑capex euphoria. The yen surged abruptly to a ten‑week high on intervention chatter, adding a dramatic dollar‑weakness leg to the cross‑asset move, though the broad dollar sell‑off is equally attributable to peace‑driven demand for risk currencies. A fragile truce remains far from finalised while the Hormuz blockade is still being enforced, with a US fighter jet disabling an Iranian tanker even as China publicly urged a prompt reopening of the strait ahead of the Trump‑Xi summit next week.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 US‑Iran Peace Hopes Trigger Oil Collapse and Global Equity Surge¶

    Fact Oil steadied after losing nearly 8% in the previous session, as the US and Iran weighed a fresh push to end the war in the Middle East (BBG 1). President Trump said the US had “very good talks” with Iran in the last 24 hours and that the war could end if Iran agrees to the proposal (Reuters 1, 2). Iran’s Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi is in Beijing seeking support on a deal with the US and said the Islamic Republic is still reviewing Washington’s proposal (Al Jazeera). Emerging‑market stocks rallied to an all‑time high and a currency index rose to pre‑war levels as traders grew optimistic the two sides are moving closer to a peace deal (BBG 2). The S&P 500 and Nasdaq each notched record closes, with AMD’s results sparking an AI‑stock rally (Reuters). Japan’s Nikkei 225 hit an intraday record as markets reopened to catch up with the global equities rally (BBG 3). Brent crude briefly traded below $100 before steadying, while gold held a hefty 2.77% gain from its biggest daily advance since late March as truce hopes eased inflation fears (BBG 4). A US fighter jet disabled an Iranian oil tanker that was attempting to breach the American naval blockade of Iranian ports, US Central Command confirmed, underscoring that the blockade is still very much in force (SCMP, US_CENTCOM). Iran is simultaneously pushing ahead with a Hormuz toll “authority” despite reports of a deal (Lloyds_List). A one-page memorandum is now said to be close, but Iran has cautioned that it remains a US “wish list” until it is reality (CNN).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: A framework agreement, even a non‑binding memorandum, would pull the tail‑risk of an open‑ended supply disruption, compressing energy‑cost fears globally and lowering the barrier for EM central banks to begin easing cycles.
    • Tactical Trade: Long EM equities and Short Brent; however, PIVOT to flat/neutral if Iran publicly rejects the memo this week, which would put the $100 floor back at risk.

    📌 AI Capex Euphoria Intensifies: AMD, Nvidia/Corning, Anthropic Deals¶

    Fact AMD shares hit a record high and surged 19% after the company forecast data‑center revenue well above estimates, with CEO Lisa Su explaining a massive forecast change driven by “unprecedented” AI infrastructure demand (CNBC 1, Reuters). Nvidia will invest up to $3.2 billion in Corning and partner on three new optical‑fiber factories in North Carolina and Texas focused on AI data‑center connectivity (CNBC 2). Anthropic has signed a deal to use all of the compute capacity at SpaceX’s Colossus‑1 data center in Memphis, Tennessee, and CEO Dario Amodei said the company’s first‑quarter growth was “80‑fold,” explaining its “difficulties with compute” (CNBC 3, WSJ). In China, DeepSeek is in talks to raise funds at a $50 billion valuation, and domestic chipmakers are racing to adopt its V4 large language model (WSJ 1, SCMP). The global chip rally also lifted Taiwan’s stock market, though the Hormuz crisis has exposed gaps in the island’s “silicon shield” because of its heavy dependence on seaborne energy supplies (BBG 5).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The simultaneous acceleration of hyperscale capex across CPUs, GPUs, optical interconnects, and AI‑cloud capacity signals that the AI investment super‑cycle is broadening, not peaking, supporting semi‑manufacturers and energy‑infrastructure plays.
    • Tactical Trade: Long KOSPI and Long TWD; PIVOT to flat if US restrictions on chip exports to China tighten abruptly after the Trump‑Xi summit, undercutting the DeepSeek‑fueled domestic AI narrative.

    📌 Yen Surges Amid Intervention Speculation; Dollar Sinks on Peace Hopes¶

    Fact The yen jumped abruptly to a ten‑week high, fueling fresh speculation that Japanese authorities may be intervening to stem dollar weakness, while the broader dollar slipped amid US‑Iran peace hopes (Investing_Forex 1, Reuters 2). Asian currencies strengthened, with the South Korean won outperforming after hot CPI data, as peace‑deal optimism accelerated the shift out of the dollar (Investing_Forex 2). The pound also jumped as the dollar sold off, with cable rising sharply on the combination of improved risk sentiment and speculation around Japanese intervention (Investing_Forex 3).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: Yen volatility is being amplified by thin liquidity around Japan’s reopening, but the macro backdrop — falling oil, softer US yields, and peace hopes — independently supports a lower‑dollar regime that could sustain yen strength even without active intervention.
    • Tactical Trade: Long JPY vs USD; PIVOT to neutral if the MOF explicitly dismisses intervention this week, allowing the yen to retrace some of the speculative gains.

    📌 China Presses Iran to Reopen Hormuz While US Trade Tensions Loom¶

    Fact During a meeting between Foreign Minister Wang Yi and Iranian counterpart Abbas Araghchi in Beijing, China called for a “prompt resumption of shipping traffic through the Strait of Hormuz,” though that point was absent from Iran’s statement (CNBC 4). The meeting preceded next week’s Trump‑Xi summit, which will almost certainly include proclamations of cooperation but beneath the surface “major trade conflict is building,” according to trade experts (SCMP 2). Separately, the US trial of a man accused of operating a secret Chinese police station in New York has opened, adding to the bilateral friction (WSJ 2). The EU’s plan to phase out Chinese tech could cost the bloc over $400 billion, a Chinese study has found, illustrating the global decoupling headwind (Reuters).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: Beijing’s commercial urgency to get tankers moving again gives it a strong incentive to press Iran toward a deal, but the deeper trade rift with Washington suggests that any post‑peace risk‑on rally in CNH could be capped by tariff uncertainty.
    • Tactical Trade: Short CNH on a relative basis, expecting underperformance vs KRW and TWD if the summit fails to produce a tangible trade truce.

    🌍 Global Radar¶

    📊 Economic Indicators¶

    • US companies boosted payrolls by 109,000 in April, the most in over a year, though below the 120,000 median estimate in a Bloomberg survey (BBG, SCMP).
    • Sweden’s finance ministry expects the Nordic country’s economic recovery to be slowed more severely by the Iran war than previously thought, potentially denting the government’s re‑election prospects (BBG).
    • Egypt’s inflation unexpectedly slowed in April despite the Iran war spurring higher fuel costs and a weaker currency (BBG).

    🏦 Central Banks¶

    • Federal Reserve — Chicago Fed President Goolsbee warned that consumer behavior is showing signs of stress and that the central bank should not front‑run anticipated productivity gains by cutting rates prematurely (BBG 1, 2).
    • Bank of Canada — The Bank of Canada is weighing whether to spell out clearer guidance on how it responds to supply shocks in its monetary policy framework (BBG).
    • National Bank of Poland — Poland kept interest rates unchanged for a second month as the Iran war fallout on energy prices revives domestic inflation (BBG).
    • Bank Negara Malaysia — Malaysia is expected to keep its benchmark rate unchanged on Thursday as inflation remains benign despite higher global oil prices (BBG).

    🌐 Geopolitics & Policy¶

    • Russia warned diplomatic missions to evacuate staff from Kyiv if Moscow launches a mass retaliatory strike, as the Russian military staged a huge attack on Ukraine on the eve of a proposed cease‑fire (Reuters, WSJ).
    • Israel struck Beirut for the first time since agreeing to a cease‑fire with Hezbollah last month, targeting a commander of the militant group’s elite Radwan force (Reuters).
    • US lawmakers in a rare push demanded transparency on Israel’s nuclear capability, arguing that the policy of ambiguity heightens risks amid the US‑Israel war on Iran (Al Jazeera).
    • France is moving its aircraft carrier to the Red Sea with an eye on a future Hormuz mission (Reuters).
    • The US is considering tapping oil under military bases to refill the Strategic Petroleum Reserve (BT).

    🛢️ Commodities¶

    • US crude and fuel inventories fell as the Iran war continues to roil energy markets, the EIA said, adding that the supply shock is expected to worsen even if the conflict ends (Reuters).
    • Fujairah oil product stocks fell nearly 7% to a new record low, underscoring acute product tightness at the Gulf bunkering hub (SP_Global).
    • Russian crude exports jumped 8% on sanctions relief, boosting global supply even as Hormuz‑related anxiety raises availability fears (SP_Global).
    • The Hormuz crisis slashed VLCC volumes by 36% but voyage lengths have lengthened as vessels reroute, raising freight costs (Lloyds_List).

    📈 Equities¶

    • The S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite each closed at record highs, with strong US corporate earnings and AI optimism driving the advances (Reuters).
    • Japan’s Nikkei 225 surged to an intraday record high as markets reopened to catch up with the global equities rally (BBG).
    • European stocks rose more than 2% on the Stoxx 600, buoyed by both Iran deal optimism and positive earnings surprises (BT).
    • Emerging‑market stocks hit an all‑time high as the EM currency index returned to pre‑Iran‑war levels, reflecting broad risk appetite (BBG).
