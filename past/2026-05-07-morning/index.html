    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 6 6PM to May 7 8AM HKT Good morning.

    Overview

    Hopes that the US and Iran could strike a peace deal to end the two‑month war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz sent oil prices tumbling below $100 a barrel and sparked a global equity rally, though a US airstrike disabling an Iranian tanker, Iran’s push for a strait toll authority, and a lack of concrete surrender terms injected caution, leaving risk assets swinging on diplomatic headlines. The S&P 500 touched record territory on the back of stunning corporate earnings, but Fed officials warned that mounting supply‑chain disruptions could entrench inflation, complicating the rate outlook. With a Trump‑Xi summit next week, simmering US‑China trade tensions added a second front of geopolitical uncertainty for investors, cementing oil volatility as the dominant cross‑market thread.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 US‑Iran Peace Negotiations & Strait of Hormuz Dynamics¶

    Fact Iran is reviewing a US proposal to end the war, though key demands remain unaddressed (CNA). President Donald Trump warned that Iran will be bombed at a ‘much higher level’ if it doesn’t agree to the peace deal (CNBC). China called for a ‘prompt resumption of shipping traffic through the Strait of Hormuz’ ahead of the Trump‑Xi summit (CNBC). Iran said it will ensure the safe passage of ships through Hormuz (CCTV). However, Iran is pressing ahead with a toll ‘authority’ for the strait (Lloyd's List). A US fighter jet disabled an Iranian oil tanker in the Gulf of Oman that tried to breach the American naval blockade (US_CENTCOM, SCMP). France moved its aircraft carrier to the Red Sea with an eye on a potential Hormuz mission (Reuters). Trump also said the United States will get uranium from Iran (Reuters).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The peace push is genuine but fragile, with conflicting signals—official review, continued blockade enforcement, Iranian toll authority, and allied military posturing—suggesting that even a formal ceasefire won’t quickly reopen Hormuz, keeping energy risk premiums elevated.
    • Tactical Trade: Short Brent on near‑term de‑escalation momentum; PIVOT to long if a deal collapses or fails to include immediate strait reopening.

    📌 Oil Price Plunge & Supply Shock¶

    Fact Brent crude futures fell to $97 a barrel before rebounding to above $101 after reports of a potential US‑Iran deal (BBC). US crude and fuel inventories fell again last week, and further declines are expected even if the conflict ends, according to EIA data (Reuters). Oil product stocks at Fujairah fell nearly 7% to a new record low (S&P Global). US fuel exports hit an all‑time record as the Hormuz closure spurred demand from Asia and Europe (FT). The US is considering tapping oil under military bases to refill its strategic petroleum reserve (Business Times). Russian crude exports jumped 8% on sanctions relief, adding supply pressure (S&P Global).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: De‑escalation hopes temporarily collapsed the war premium, but the physical supply chain is so degraded that even a political solution won’t quickly refill inventories, capping downside for crude.
    • Tactical Trade: Short Brent to harvest a peace premium fade, but PIVOT to long if the ceasefire does not include a clear timeline for commercial Hormuz transit.

    📌 Equity Rally Wavers on Deal Uncertainty¶

    Fact A rally that drove global stocks to all‑time highs wavered as traders awaited updates on the US‑Iran peace deal (BBG). Europe’s Stoxx 600 closed up over 2% on Iran deal optimism (Business Times). The S&P 500 and Nasdaq edged higher as crude oil fell ahead of the update (Yahoo Finance). US corporate profit strength ignited the charge to record peaks (Reuters). Apollo CEO Rowan warned of elevated risk of unexpected shocks and said he is positioning defensively for an expected correction (CNBC).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: Peace‑driven disinflation and strong earnings are fueling a risk‑on melt‑up, but the rally’s pause on deal uncertainty signals limited upside unless a verifiable ceasefire is completed.
    • Tactical Trade: Long US equities into a deal announcement, take profits if negotiations stall; layer in hedges if oil spikes back above $110.

    📌 Fed Officials Warn on Supply‑Chain Inflation¶

    Fact Fed officials say rising supply chain risks fuel concern that inflation may prove more persistent than expected (Reuters). The San Francisco Fed’s advisory council reported that businesses are increasingly uncertain and the economic outlook is deteriorating, though consumer spending is holding up (SF_Fed). Kraft Heinz’s CEO said consumers are ‘literally running out of money’ (WSJ).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: Supply‑driven inflation risks, even if the war ends, will keep the Fed cautious, supporting a higher‑for‑longer rate environment and tempering dovish bets.
    • Tactical Trade: Short front‑end Treasuries (bear flattening) on sticky inflation; long USD/JPY as the Fed remains firm while BOJ intervention may prove limited.
