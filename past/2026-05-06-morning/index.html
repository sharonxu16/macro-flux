    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 5 6PM to May 6 8AM HKT Good morning.

    Overview

    Oil prices slumped after Trump paused the US “Project Freedom” Hormuz escort mission, citing “great progress” toward a final Iran deal. The announcement injected fresh de-escalation hopes even as the UAE reported its second consecutive day of missile and drone interceptions over Fujairah port. Markets seized on the diplomatic opening, sending the S&P 500 and Nasdaq to fresh records led by AI chip stocks, while US long bonds rebounded as yields fell back below 5%. South Korea’s inflation hit its fastest since July 2024 on surging energy costs, keeping pressure on the BOK to follow the RBA’s hawkish lead. The cross‑current remains the tug‑of‑war between the oil‑supply risk premium and the fragile ceasefire, with the Fed’s policy path now tilting toward a pre‑emptive rate hike if energy inflation proves sticky.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 Trump pauses Hormuz operation, cites Iran deal progress — oil drops but UAE attacked again¶

    Fact US President Donald Trump said he is pausing “Project Freedom,” the US military’s bid to guide commercial ships out of the Strait of Hormuz, telling reporters, “Great Progress has been made on a final agreement to end the war with Iran” (Reuters 1, 2). US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said the ceasefire with Iran remains in place despite growing tensions in the Strait (Al Jazeera). Hegseth later urged Iran “to be prudent” in its actions during the ceasefire (CNN). Secretary of State Marco Rubio said the US has achieved the objectives of the Iran operation and described resolving the Hormuz situation as a test for the UN, urging against any Security Council veto (Reuters 3, 4). Meanwhile, the UAE said it came under Iranian missile and drone attacks for a second consecutive day, with air defence systems engaging the projectiles; Iran’s foreign ministry stated its forces “have not carried out any missile or drone operations against the UAE in recent days” (Reuters 5). The US Navy’s USS George H.W. Bush aircraft carrier was sailing in the Arabian Sea (US_CENTCOM). Oil fell more than $2 after the pause announcement (Reuters 6).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The pause reduces immediate military escalation risk, but the residual supply-risk premium persists as long as Iran and the UAE trade fire. A final deal could bring the Hormuz narrative to an end, crushing oil’s geopolitical bid and easing EM Asia’s energy‑import pain. Failure would harden the blockade and force a broad reassessment of global energy‑security architecture.
    • Tactical Trade: Short Brent on de‑escalation; PIVOT to long Brent if attacks on Fujairah escalate into a sustained disruption of UAE oil‑export capacity and the ceasefire collapses.

    📌 Traders ramp up bets Warsh’s Fed could hike rates before cutting; job market stable¶

    Fact Traders ramped up wagers that the Fed under incoming Chair Kevin Warsh could raise rates before cutting them (BBG). US job openings were little changed in March while hiring rebounded, showing a continued stabilisation of the labour market (BBG 2, Reuters). Fed Governor Michelle Bowman flagged consumer fraud could pose risks to the financial system (BBG 3). The US trade deficit widened in March as imports outpaced exports, signalling solid demand (BBG 4). The 30‑year Treasury yield fell back below 5% as buyers stepped in to lock in multi‑year highs (BBG 5).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: A stable labour market and persistent inflation from the oil shock give the incoming Fed chair cover to hike, narrowing the window for Asian EM central banks to ease. That underpins a higher‑for‑longer global yield environment and a stronger dollar.
    • Tactical Trade: Long USD/Asia FX; Short KRW on the combination of rate‑hike expectations and accelerating domestic inflation.

    📌 South Korea inflation hits fastest since July 2024, pressuring BOK¶

    Fact South Korea’s consumer inflation picked up as surging energy costs began to feed more forcefully into the domestic economy, with price gains matching the fastest pace since July 2024 (BBG). The acceleration adds to headwinds for Asia EM currencies already under strain from the oil shock (Reuters).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The BOK can no longer look through energy‑driven price pressure; a rate hike is becoming a tail‑risk scenario that would further widen the DXY‑KRW yield gap and accelerate capital outflows.
    • Tactical Trade: Short KRW; PIVOT to flat if the BOK explicitly pushes back against tightening and the May‑6 inflation print surprises to the downside.
