    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 3 6PM to May 4 8AM HKT Good morning.

    Overview

    The US confirmed Monday's "Project Freedom" military escort in the Strait of Hormuz, Iran called it a ceasefire violation, and two vessels were attacked – a bulk carrier off Sirik and a tanker off Fujairah – cementing a binary risk scenario for Brent and global shipping. Meanwhile, China activated its new "Blocking Rules" for the first time, ordering non-compliance with US sanctions on five oil refiners, a direct financial decoupling escalation that could test the Xi-Trump summit backdrop. The RBA is set to deliver a third straight rate hike (to 4.35%), cementing its hawkish outlier status against a backdrop of ECB recession warnings and a Fed constrained by Iran war uncertainty. Across markets, Asian stocks outside Japan are grinding toward all-time highs on AI/tech earnings optimism, while gold edges higher and oil steadies with a tanker-attack floor.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 Hormuz Escalation: "Project Freedom" launched, Iran warns of strikes, two vessels hit¶

    Fact The United States will launch an effort on Monday to "guide" stranded ships from the Iran-gripped Strait of Hormuz, President Donald Trump said, as two ships around the strait reported attacks. Trump gave few details about what could be a sweeping attempt to help hundreds of vessels and some 20,000 seafarers. Iran quickly denounced the move as a ceasefire violation (SCMP). U.S. Central Command confirmed it will support the launch of Project Freedom in the Strait of Hormuz (US_CENTCOM). A tanker was hit by unknown projectiles off UAE's Fujairah, UKMTO says (Reuters 1). A bulk carrier was attacked by multiple small craft 11 nautical miles west of Sirik, Iran, according to UKMTO (Al Jazeera 1). An Iran lawmaker said the Strait of Hormuz will never return to the status quo that existed before the US-Israel war on Tehran (Al Jazeera 2). The Iranian military warned the US of attacks over the Hormuz mission (Al Jazeera 3). Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said the US is "suffocating" Iran with economic and financial pressure (BBG 1). OPEC+ agreed a third oil output quota hike since the Hormuz closure (Reuters 2). Oil steadied as traders questioned the workability of Trump's plan, with a tanker reportedly hit in the waterway (BBG 2).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: "Project Freedom" triggers limited skirmishes but no full blockade breach, keeping Brent elevated near $85 on persistent risk premium.
    • Tactical Trade: Long Brent front-month calendar spread; PIVOT to flat if the first 24 hours conclude without any engagement, implying risk premium overdone.

    📌 China Activates "Blocking Rules" for First Time Against US Sanctions on Oil Refiners¶

    Fact China has ordered companies throughout the country not to comply with US sanctions on five Chinese oil refiners accused of trading in Iranian fuel, a move observers say could mark a new stage in Beijing's pushback against American long-arm jurisdiction. The order is the first application of a measure designed to block "improper" foreign actions, and could be a potential headache for US sanctions enforcement, according to analysts (SCMP 1). Beijing's decision to block Meta's $2 billion acquisition of Chinese AI start-up Manus was also described as a show of strength ahead of the Xi-Trump summit (SCMP 2). Separately, China's exports of telecoms, computer and information services reached 808 billion yuan ($118 billion) in 2025, up 13% YoY, far outpacing traditional goods (SCMP 3).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The "Blocking Rules" signal a new normal of selective financial decoupling, putting upward pressure on USD/CNH as capital flow risks rise.
    • Tactical Trade: Long USD/CNH via forwards or options; PIVOT to neutral if US refrains from immediate secondary sanctions, suggesting diplomatic space ahead of the summit.

    📌 Central Bank Divergence: RBA Hawkish Outlier, ECB Recession Fears, Fed Hemmed In¶

    Fact Australia's central bank is set to entrench its status as a hawkish outlier with a third straight interest-rate hike, diverging from peers that are mainly sitting tight to observe fallout from the US-Iran conflict (BBG 1). The RBA is expected to hike to 4.35% (BBG 2). ECB policymaker Stournaras said euro zone recession concerns are "real and justified" (Reuters 1). Minneapolis Fed President Kashkari said the Iran war limits the Fed's ability to provide rate guidance (Reuters 2). Federal Reserve Vice Chair for Supervision Michael Barr warned that private credit could spark "psychological contagion" (BBG 3).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: RBA hike widens AUD/USD carry advantage short-term, but global risk-off from Hormuz caps upside; ECB recession and Fed pause reinforce USD strength vs EUR and JPY.
    • Tactical Trade: Short EUR/AUD on policy divergence; PIVOT to flat if Tuesday's RBA guidance sounds a markedly dovish note, signaling a peak.
