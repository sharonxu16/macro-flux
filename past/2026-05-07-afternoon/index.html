    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 7 8AM to May 7 6PM HKT Good afternoon.

    Overview

    Asian equities surged to fresh records and the dollar weakened as optimism over a potential US‑Iran peace deal pushed oil prices sharply lower, though the rally was tempered by the EU’s failure to finalize a US trade pact—risking new tariffs on cars—and China’s decision to invoke its anti‑sanctions law while asking banks to halt loans to US‑sanctioned refiners. The Nikkei 225 soared past 62,000 for the first time and Hong Kong’s Hang Seng Index jumped more than 400 points, with chip names leading the charge, as traders looked past President Trump’s warning that Iran would be bombed at a “much higher level” if it rejects the peace proposal. Oil’s retreat below $100 a barrel and the broad risk‑on rotation are strengthening the case for a near‑term peace dividend, but the twin headwinds of transatlantic trade friction and US‑China sanctions escalation prevent a clean break. The cross‑market thread remains oil‑price volatility, with peace hopes simultaneously lifting risk assets and easing inflation fears while fresh trade and sanction tensions inject geopolitical uncertainty.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 Risk‑On Rally Extends as Oil Drops on Peace Hopes¶

    Fact Asian stocks hit a record high and the dollar fell on Thursday as oil prices continued to pull back on optimism over a US‑Iran peace deal (Reuters) Japan’s Nikkei 225 surged over 5% to a record high, topping 62,000 for the first time, after markets reopened from Golden Week holidays (CNBC) Hong Kong’s Hang Seng Index climbed more than 400 points, with chip stocks leading the advance (HKEJ) Brent crude fell further as investors bet on a potential reopening of the Strait of Hormuz (WSJ) President Donald Trump said the conflict in the Middle East would be “over quickly” if Iran agreed to the peace proposal, while Iran was reviewing a one‑page peace plan drawn up by the United States (SCMP)

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The peace narrative acts as the dominant macro force, fueling a classic risk‑on rotation, but the absence of a formal Iranian response keeps oil volatility elevated and prevents a decisive break in sentiment.
    • Tactical Trade: Long Asia ex‑Japan equities favored on peace meme; PIVOT to flat if Iran’s official response rejects the proposal, sending oil back above $105.

    📌 EU Fails to Finalize US Trade Deal, Risks Fresh Tariffs¶

    Fact The European Union failed to finalize a long‑delayed US trade deal during overnight talks, despite warnings from President Donald Trump that he would soon impose fresh tariffs on EU cars (BBG) The impasse raises the risk of a new trade front that could dent European exports and amplify the war‑induced supply‑chain strains

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The collapse of US‑EU trade talks injects a fresh layer of uncertainty for European manufacturing, complicating the ECB’s policy outlook and accelerating a pivot toward defensive trade measures.
    • Tactical Trade: Short EUR/USD on tariff risk premium; PIVOT to long if a last‑minute deal materializes ahead of the May 9 deadline.

    📌 China Invokes Anti‑Sanctions Law, Halts Loans to Sanctioned Refiners¶

    Fact Beijing ordered the blocking of US sanctions on five oil refineries, marking the first invocation of its 2021 anti‑sanctions law (Al Jazeera) Separately, China asked its banks to pause new loans to US‑sanctioned Chinese refiners, according to Bloomberg News (Reuters, BusinessTimes) The moves signal a hardening of China’s response to US pressure ahead of next week’s Trump‑Xi summit

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: China’s invocation of its anti‑sanctions law represents an escalation in the economic cold war, raising the probability of a rift at the summit and encouraging a de‑risking of supply chains linked to Chinese refiners.
    • Tactical Trade: Short CNY proxy (KRW, TWD) on sanctions contagion fear; PIVOT to neutral if the Trump‑Xi meeting yields a sanctions relief agreement.

    📌 Yen Intervention Follow‑Up Falls Flat as Markets Test Resolve¶

    Fact Japanese authorities likely used another $30 billion in follow‑up intervention in the currency market just days after an earlier round of action, according to a Bloomberg analysis of central bank accounts (BBG) The yen weakened on Thursday as traders questioned the effectiveness of the interventions, with the Nikkei 225 surging over 5% to a record, partly on a softer yen (CNBC) The Ministry of Finance’s intervention during the Golden Week holiday has left the market questioning the durability of the support amid a wide rate gap with the US (CNBC)

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The yen intervention is proving ineffective as the dovish BOJ stance and wide interest‑rate differential encourage carry trades, forcing Tokyo to consider additional measures, including possible coordination with the US Treasury during Bessent’s visit.
    • Tactical Trade: Short JPY favored if the BOJ does not shift policy; PIVOT to long JPY if Bessent’s Japan visit yields a joint intervention commitment.
