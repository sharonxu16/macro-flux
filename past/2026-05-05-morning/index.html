    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 4 6PM to May 5 8AM HKT Good morning.

    Overview

    The US and Iran exchanged direct fire in the Strait of Hormuz, with Washington sinking Iranian fast boats and Tehran striking a UAE oil port and a South Korean-operated vessel, driving Brent above $115 and shattering the four‑week ceasefire. Shipping remains at a near‑standstill, and Kalshi prediction markets now see normal traffic resuming only in August or later. China invoked its anti‑sanctions law to defy US blacklisting of refiners, while Treasury Secretary Bessent pressed Beijing to help open the strait, raising the stakes ahead of the Xi‑Trump summit. ECB hawk Nagel reinforced the case for a June rate hike, and the EU braced for a threatened 25% US auto tariff. The cross‑market thread: risk‑off flight into oil and the dollar, while equities and risk‑sensitive Asian FX came under pressure.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 Strait of Hormuz: US‑Iran Live Fire Escalates, Shipping at Standstill¶

    Fact The US military said it “sank Iranian small boats, shot down missiles and drones” as it began efforts to open the Strait of Hormuz, while Iran claimed it “prevented entry of US warships” into the waterway (Reuters 1, 2). Iran said its navy “forced a US warship to turn back” – a claim Washington denied (Al Jazeera). “An explosion and fire” struck a South Korean‑operated vessel anchored near the UAE inside the strait; Seoul reported no casualties among the 24 crew (SCMP). A fire broke out at the Fujairah petroleum complex in the UAE after an Iranian drone attack (Reuters). A US‑flagged Maersk vessel transited the strait accompanied by the US military, but most shipping remains at a standstill (Reuters, 2). Traders on Kalshi said traffic will not return to normal until August or later (CNBC). Brent crude jumped to $115 intraday on the flareup and held the bulk of the gain (BBG). S&P 500 slipped 0.1% from its all‑time high, the dollar strengthened, and bond yields rose (BBG).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The direct exchanges raise the probability of a sustained military standoff in the Strait, hardening the supply‑risk premium and reducing the credibility of the ceasefire.
    • Tactical Trade: Long Brent; PIVOT to neutral if the next 48 hours produce no further kinetic engagement, signaling the risk premium was overshot.

    📌 China Invokes Anti‑Sanctions Law, Bessent Pressures Beijing on Iran¶

    Fact China invoked its anti‑sanctions law to counter US blacklisting of its refiners, ordering non‑compliance with US sanctions (Reuters). US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent urged China to “step up with some diplomacy and get the Iranians to open the strait,” noting that China buys “90 per cent of their energy” (SCMP). Bessent stressed the US has “absolute control” over the waterway even as he called on Beijing to pressure Tehran (Reuters). The dispute raises the temperature ahead of the Xi‑Trump summit, with US experts saying the Iran war could give Beijing the upper hand in those talks (SCMP).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The legal showdown over sanctions increases pre‑summit tension and signals China will not yield on Iran ties, limiting US leverage.
    • Tactical Trade: Long USD/CNH on escalated bilateral risk; PIVOT to flat if the summit proceeds without additional punitive measures.

    🌍 Global Radar¶

    📊 Economic Indicators¶

    • IMF Managing Director Kristalina Georgieva warned of a “much worse outcome” if the Middle East war drags into 2027 (BusinessTimes).
    • EU Economy Commissioner Valdis Dombrovskis warned of a stagflationary shock for the bloc from the Iran war (BBG).
    • US restaurant sales dropped as the Iran war pushed gasoline prices higher, a consumption‑led indicator (Reuters).
    • South African gasoline prices jumped to a near‑four‑year high as the government failed to fully offset higher oil prices (BBG).
    • Asian Development Bank’s chief economist urged countries to limit fuel subsidies to the most vulnerable, citing prolonged Middle East tensions straining public finances (BBG).

    🏦 Central Banks¶

    • Fed – New York Fed President John Williams said rates are “well positioned” amid war uncertainty and will need to be lower “at some point,” while former New York Fed President Bill Dudley backed Jerome Powell staying on after “merciless” attacks on the central bank (BBG).
    • ECB – Bundesbank President Joachim Nagel sees a case for a June rate hike without marked inflation progress (BBG). The ECB warned that weaker bank capital rules could fuel payouts, not lending (BBG).
    • South Africa – Central bank Governor Lesetja Kganyago said policymakers will “very carefully” monitor incoming data as the Iran war clouds the inflation outlook (BBG).
    • India – The government is exploring steps to mobilise US dollar inflows as the rupee slides, sources said (BusinessTimes).

    🌐 Geopolitics & Policy¶

    • US President Donald Trump’s administration will move forward with plans to hike EU car tariffs, putting German carmakers in the crosshairs (Reuters). The EU vowed action but urged restraint (BBG).
    • The US, Gulf Arab nations drafted a new UN resolution on the Strait of Hormuz (Reuters).
    • The EU blocked funds for key Chinese solar energy parts, citing security concerns (FT).
    • India’s ruling BJP under Narendra Modi won a historic landslide in West Bengal, toppling the leftwing chief minister after 15 years (FT).

    🛢️ Commodities¶

    • Oil held gains after the US‑Iran exchange, with Brent surging to $115 intraday (BBG).
    • Diamondback Energy, one of the biggest US shale producers, said it is boosting crude output immediately in response to rising prices (BBG).
    • Goldman Sachs warned that global oil stocks are approaching an eight‑year low and depletion speed is a concern (Reuters).
    • Chevron CEO warned that shortages in oil supply will begin appearing (Reuters).
    • Comex gold settled 2.38% lower at $4519.50, pressured by the stronger dollar (WSJ).

    📈 Equities¶

    • South Korean stocks hit a fresh record, building on a historic monthly rally in April, despite regional risk‑off tone (CNBC).
    • US equity indexes (S&P 500, Dow) slipped from all‑time highs, driven by Middle East worries (Reuters).
    • European shares dropped 0.99%, with auto stocks under pressure from the tariff threat (HKEJ).
