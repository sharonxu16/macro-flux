    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 5 8AM to May 5 7PM HKT Good afternoon.

    Overview

    The US said its ceasefire with Iran remains intact despite Monday’s direct‑fire exchange, but the operational reality is a stand‑off that keeps shipping clustered away from the Strait of Hormuz and oil supply risks elevated. The RBA delivered a third straight rate hike to 4.35% and signalled a pause, while Philippine inflation surged to 7.2% and the Indonesian rupiah and Indian rupee hit record lows, intensifying Asian EM stress. South Korea rebuffed Trump’s call to join the Hormuz mission, and China escalated its sanctions defiance as Washington threatened visa sanctions over illegal migration. HSBC shares tumbled 6% after a fraud‑linked charge dragged first‑quarter profit below estimates. The cross‑market thread is a risk‑off flight into the dollar, under pressure on EM FX, while oil holds elevated and global equities struggle for direction.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 Ceasefire Holds but Hormuz Stand‑Off Persists; Ships Cluster Further from Strait¶

    Fact The US played down the prospect of a return to active war with Iran after a day of clashes in the Strait of Hormuz, stating that the ceasefire is still in place and the strikes were “all below the threshold” of restarting full combat (BBG, FT). Hundreds of vessels were seen clustering near Dubai as more ships moved away from the still‑empty strait, with Iran widening its area of control (BBG). Nine ships used the strait on Tuesday but “renewed tensions” are expected to slow traffic (SP_Global_Commodities). Shipping prepared for a potential Hormuz exit amid a confused US plan and renewed Iranian threats (Lloyds_List_Shipping). Brent eased on signs the US operation is loosening Iran’s blockade but held above $115 intraday (BT).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: Washington’s down‑play of the clashes keeps the diplomatic path open but does not reverse the physical supply risk; shipping avoidance entrenches a persistent energy insurance premium.
    • Tactical Trade: Long Brent remains favoured, but PIVOT to flat if tanker traffic through the strait returns to pre‑clash levels for more than one day.

    📌 South Korea Rebuffs Trump’s Project Freedom After Ship Fire¶

    Fact Seoul indicated that a UN mandate and a global coalition are required before joining the US mission to reopen the strait, delivering a polite but firm no to Trump’s call that invoked a South Korean‑operated vessel hit by an explosion and fire (SCMP). Seoul is reviewing Trump’s navigation plan following the explosion on the Korean‑operated ship, with officials maintaining the need for a multinational framework (Reuters).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: South Korea’s refusal signals alliance friction and raises the risk that other US allies hesitate, complicating any rapid reopening of the strait and prolonging supply disruptions.
    • Tactical Trade: Short KRW gains conviction; PIVOT neutral if Seoul signals a compromise on the UN mandate within the week.

    📌 RBA Delivers Third Straight Hike, Signals Pause as Spending Surges¶

    Fact Australia’s central bank raised its key interest rate to 4.35% from 4.1%, marking a third consecutive hike, with Governor Michele Bullock signalling policymakers would now pause to assess next steps (BBG). Australian household spending rose at its fastest pace in two years in March, driven by a spike in gasoline prices following the outbreak of the Iran war, complicating the RBA’s efforts to cool the economy (BBG). The RBA cited higher fuel prices from the war as a reason for the hike, cementing its outlier status among global peers (FT).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The surge in oil‑fuelled spending while the RBA pauses leaves Australian demand‑side inflation unchecked, risking a later forced hiking cycle if energy costs stay high.
    • Tactical Trade: Long AUD/NZD on policy divergence, but PIVOT flat if next month’s spending data show a rapid cooling that validates the pause.

    📌 Asia EM FX Under Siege: PHP Inflation at 7.2%, IDR and INR Hit Record Lows¶

    Fact Philippine inflation accelerated to 7.2% in April, the highest since March 2023, breaching the central bank’s forecast range and raising the chance of more aggressive rate hikes (BBG, SCMP). Indonesia’s central bank intervened as the rupiah set a new record low, with the governor previously warning the war in the Middle East would keep the currency under pressure (BBG). The Indian rupee fell to a record low, with analysts dusting off the playbook from the 2013 taper tantrum, as renewed US‑Iran tensions intensified economic worries (Reuters).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: Energy‑driven inflation forces more EM tightening, curbing growth just as external demand flags, increasing the risk of a broad EM sell‑off and forcing further FX intervention.
    • Tactical Trade: Short IDR and short INR, but PIVOT to long if oil retreats sustainably below $100 and BI/BSP/RBI clearly shift to easing mode.

    📌 HSBC Shares Tumble 6% After Fraud‑Linked Charge Misses Profit¶

    Fact HSBC reported first‑quarter pre‑tax profit of $9.4 billion, missing analysts’ estimates, as a surprise $400 million hit linked to a fraud‑related credit loss dragged on earnings, alongside provisions for Iran war risks (Reuters, SCMP). Shares slumped 6% in Hong Kong, dragging the Hang Seng Index back below the 26,000 level (Reuters, HKEJ).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The fraud charge adds idiosyncratic risk to an already stretched Asia banking complex, potentially triggering a broader earnings downgrade cycle for lenders exposed to HK CRE and Iran‑war provisions.
    • Tactical Trade: Short HSI near‑term, but PIVOT long if HSBC’s CFO re‑states confidence and China trade data next week surprises to the upside.
