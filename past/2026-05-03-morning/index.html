    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 2 6PM to May 3 8AM HKT

    Overview

    The Iran war remains the single dominant macro driver, with the Hormuz blockade continuing, oil prices elevated, and Trump reviewing a new 14-point Iranian proposal while threatening to restart strikes. The first major corporate casualty of the war emerged as Spirit Airlines shut down after a failed government bailout, a direct consequence of jet fuel prices doubling. OPEC+ provisionally agreed to another modest quota increase for June, while Beijing ordered Chinese firms to ignore US sanctions on refiners linked to Iranian oil. The week ahead opens with South Korea manufacturing PMI and a KTB auction, with markets focused on whether the Iran diplomatic track can deliver a breakthrough.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 Iran War: Trump Reviews New Proposal, Hormuz Blockade Continues¶

    Fact | Source: (SCMP), (SCMP), (Reuters), (Reuters) President Trump said he was reviewing a new 14-point Iranian proposal delivered via Pakistan, responding to a 9-point US proposal. Trump expressed displeasure with the current state of negotiations and said the US could "restart strikes if they misbehave." A senior Iranian military officer said renewed fighting was "likely." The Hormuz blockade continues, with Iraq saying oil output and exports can recover within a week once the crisis ends. Separately, Senator Lindsey Graham (R-SC) said the US should "finish the job" if Iran does not yield (FT).

    [AI Reasoning] Macro Lens — The diplomatic track remains fragile with both sides signaling willingness to continue fighting. The market is pricing a ceasefire premium in equities (S&P 500 extending weekly winning streak) but the underlying oil supply disruption remains unresolved.

    [AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact — Favors long oil and long defense/energy equities until there is a verified Hormuz reopening. Supports short duration on any headline suggesting diplomatic progress, as a ceasefire would release pent-up supply and steepen the curve.

    📌 OPEC+ Agrees June Quota Increase¶

    Fact | Source: (BBG) Major OPEC+ nations provisionally agreed to another modest, symbolic increase to supply targets for June, the group's first move since the UAE's surprise exit. The increase is modest and symbolic in scale.

    [AI Reasoning] Macro Lens — The quota increase is a political signal that OPEC+ still has spare capacity and is willing to use it, even as the Hormuz closure limits actual physical supply from the Gulf. The market impact is likely limited until the blockade lifts.

    [AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact — Neutral to slightly negative for oil prices near-term, but the real supply story remains the Hormuz chokepoint.

    📌 Spirit Airlines Shuts Down — First Iran War Corporate Casualty¶

    Fact | Source: (BBC), (SCMP), (Reuters) Spirit Airlines ceased operations after failing to secure a $500M government bailout from the Trump administration. The budget carrier was the first industry casualty of the Iran war, with jet fuel prices doubling over the two-month conflict. All flights were canceled, passengers stranded, and rival carriers scrambled to manage the disruption. Transportation Secretary Duffy said there is no need for a US budget airline bailout.

    [AI Reasoning] Macro Lens — Spirit's failure is a canary in the coal mine for sectors with high fuel cost exposure and thin margins. The transmission to broader markets is limited (Spirit was ~2% of US airline capacity), but the signal for credit markets is negative — the government declined to backstop a distressed company despite direct war-related cost shock.

    [AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact — Negative for high-yield credit in fuel-sensitive sectors. Supports long volatility in airline and transport credit.

    📌 Beijing Orders Firms to Ignore US Sanctions on Iranian Oil Refiners¶

    Fact | Source: (BBG) China ordered companies not to comply with US sanctions on five domestic refiners linked to the Iranian oil trade, deploying a blocking measure introduced in 2021 aimed at protecting firms from foreign laws deemed unjustified.

    [AI Reasoning] Macro Lens — This escalates the US-China dimension of the Iran conflict. The blocking statute creates legal ambiguity for Chinese firms operating in USD settlement systems, potentially accelerating de-dollarization efforts in oil trade. Direct risk of secondary sanctions on Chinese banks.

    [AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact — Bearish for CNH on sanctions escalation risk. Supports long gold as a reserve diversification trade. Favors long USD/CNH.

    📌 S&P 500 Extends Longest Weekly Winning Streak Since Late 2024¶

    Fact | Source: (WSJ) The S&P 500 extended its longest weekly winning streak since late 2024, with speculation that the worst of the Iran war is over driving a melt-up in AI and semiconductor stocks (BBG). Semiconductors rose in 21 of the last 23 sessions.

    [AI Reasoning] Macro Lens — This is a classic "sell the rumor, buy the fact" reversal of the war-risk discount. The equity market is pricing a ceasefire that has not yet materialized, creating asymmetry. If diplomacy fails, the unwind could be violent.

    [AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact — Favors long VIX or tail hedges on US equities. The risk/reward for chasing the melt-up is poor given unresolved Iran risk.
