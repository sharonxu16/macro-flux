    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 4 8AM to May 4 7PM HKT Good afternoon.

    Overview

    The Hormuz crisis escalated further after Iran claimed a missile strike on a US warship entering the Strait — a claim denied by Washington — sending oil and defense stocks higher while European equities slid. China's unprecedented defiance of US sanctions was formalized, ordering non-compliance on five refiners, threatening a banking-sector showdown weeks before the Xi-Trump summit. The Bank of Korea signaled it's time to consider a rate hike, the first hawkish pivot from an Asian central bank since the war began, while ECB hawks doubled down on a June hike as eurozone inflation expectations ticked up. Across markets, Asian stocks outside Japan hit a record high on AI/tech earnings momentum, the euro held firm on rate expectations, and the yen spiked intraday on intervention fears, linking elevated geopolitical risk with divergent monetary policy paths.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 Hormuz Live Fire: Iran Claims Missile Strike on US Warship; Denied¶

    Fact Iranian state media says two missiles have struck a US navy destroyer to prevent it entering the Strait of Hormuz (Al Jazeera). The US denied a report by Iranian media on Monday that the country had struck an American naval vessel with missiles, Axios reported citing a senior official (BBG). Oil prices and stocks pared back some of their earlier moves after the denial (BBG). Iran's Revolutionary Guards unveiled a new map of the Strait of Hormuz under their control, state media says (Reuters). Oil jumped after Iran's navy said it halted a US warship (Reuters). Japan's PM said the Hormuz closure is "inflicting an enormous impact" on Asia Pacific (Al Jazeera). A tanker was hit by unknown projectiles off UAE's Fujairah (Reuters).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The unverified missile claim injects a binary headline risk premium; whether true or false, the threat of direct US-Iran naval engagement is now priced into oil and shipping.
    • Tactical Trade: Long Brent; PIVOT to neutral if US provides conclusive evidence the claim is fabricated and Project Freedom proceeds without incident.

    📌 China Defies US Sanctions on Oil Refiners Ahead of Trump Summit¶

    Fact China has ordered its companies to ignore US sanctions, an unprecedented act of defiance that threatens to trap a vast banking sector in the crossfire as tension rises between the world's largest economies (BBG). The US action "violates international law and the basic norms of international relations", China's Ministry of Commerce said on Saturday (SCMP). The sanctions cover a refinery in Dalian owned by Shanghai-listed Hengli Petrochemical, along with four independent "teapot" refineries (SCMP).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: China's formal defiance elevates financial decoupling from a tariff dispute to a direct sanctions-busting conflict, testing the Xi-Trump summit framework and risking secondary sanctions on Chinese banks.
    • Tactical Trade: Long USD/CNH on widening risk premium; PIVOT to flat if US Treasury provides a waiver or postponement before the summit.

    📌 Bank of Korea Flags Readiness to Hike Rates¶

    Fact Top Bank of Korea official says it's time to consider rate hike (BusinessTimes).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The BOK's hawkish signal breaks the dovish Asian central bank consensus, reflecting imported inflation pressure from energy costs and a strong dollar that is squeezing the current account.
    • Tactical Trade: Short USD/KRW on rate differential narrowing; PIVOT to long if the BOK delays beyond Q3 due to growth concerns.

    🌍 Global Radar¶

    📊 Economic Indicators¶

    • Euro-zone inflation will jump to 2.7% on average this year but return close to 2% next year, according to the ECB's quarterly survey of professional forecasters (BBG). Turkish annual inflation rose more than expected in April to 32.4%, as price pressures from the Iran war continued to ripple through the economy (BBG). Vietnam's inflation hit a six-year high and its trade gap reached a 30-year peak, squeezing the country's growth push (BusinessTimes). Indonesia's April inflation eased to 2.42% (BusinessTimes). A gauge measuring South African manufacturer sentiment rose in April to its best level in two years, potentially reflecting front-loading ahead of price increases due to the Iran war (BBG).

    🏦 Central Banks¶

    • ECB — It's "highly likely" that the ECB will have to raise interest rates at its next meeting in June, Governing Council member Peter Kazimir said (BBG). The ECB must be both cautious and ready to act on rates should inflation spread beyond a surge in oil prices, outgoing Governing Council member Francois Villeroy de Galhau said (BBG). Eurozone companies expect a new inflation surge if the war lasts months, an ECB poll showed (BusinessTimes).
    • Bank of Korea — Top Bank of Korea official says it's time to consider rate hike (BusinessTimes).
    • BOJ/MOF — A sudden surge in the yen ensured traders remained on edge over the potential for Japanese authorities to step back into the market after last week's intervention to curb declines (BBG). Goldman Sachs estimates Japan spent 5 trillion yen intervening last week and has capacity to intervene another 30 times (HKEJ).

    🌐 Geopolitics & Policy¶

    • Europe is "ready to respond" should Trump follow through on his threat to raise tariffs on cars and trucks from the EU to 25%, according to Eurogroup President Kyriakos Pierrakakis (BBG). America's small businesses are being left out of Trump tariff refunds (FT). Singapore and New Zealand signed the world's first legally binding bilateral agreement to keep essential supplies flowing even during crises (SCMP, CNA). The US called Taiwan a "trusted and capable partner" and praised its ties with Eswatini (Reuters). An unfinished Iran war could give Xi the upper hand in Trump talks, sources told CNN (CNN).

    🛢️ Commodities¶

    • Oil steadied as traders questioned the workability of Trump's Plan to guide ships through Hormuz, with a tanker reportedly hit in the waterway (BBG). Oil then jumped after Iran's navy said it halted a US warship (Reuters). OPEC+ agreed a third oil output quota hike since the Hormuz closure, of 188,000 barrels per day (CNBC). Abu Dhabi's ADNOC plans to award $55 billion in projects through 2028 (WSJ). The UAE fertiliser giant Fertiglobe is resorting to trucks to shift product out of the Gulf, avoiding the Strait of Hormuz (FT). India resumed wheat exports after four years but high prices are likely to limit demand (Reuters).

    📈 Equities¶

    • A rally in shares tied to AI helped Asia's stock benchmark outside Japan wipe out losses sparked by the Iran war and climb back to an all-time high (BBG). South Korea's KOSPI closed up 5.1% and Taiwan's TAIEX also hit a record high (HKEJ). European stocks tumbled as intensifying rhetoric between the US and Iran over the Strait of Hormuz pushed up oil prices (BBG). Hong Kong's Hang Seng Index rose 319 points to close above 26,000 in its May open (HKEJ). Bitcoin topped $80,000 for a three-month high as stocks rose (BusinessTimes).
