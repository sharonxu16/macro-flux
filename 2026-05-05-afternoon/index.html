    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 5 8AM to May 5 6PM HKT Good afternoon.

    Overview

    The afternoon session saw a clear risk-off tilt in Asia, led by a retreat in Hong Kong equities as HSBC missed profit estimates on a fraud-related charge and provisions for the Iran war, with the Hang Seng Index shedding over 300 points. Oil prices eased from earlier highs as a Maersk vessel successfully transited the Strait of Hormuz under US escort, but the ceasefire remains fragile after Iran struck an Emirati oil port and a South Korean-operated ship. South Korea rebuffed Trump’s call to join “Project Freedom,” while the EU sounded out industry on a new trade weapon targeting Chinese overcapacity. The cross-market thread: the dollar held firm on haven demand, Brent stayed above $110, and Asian FX—particularly the rupiah and rupee—came under renewed pressure.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 Hong Kong: HSBC Misses, HSI Slides on Risk-Off¶

    Fact Hong Kong’s benchmark Hang Seng Index fell as much as 1.6% in afternoon trading, on track for its biggest drop in three weeks, dragged by HSBC Holdings Plc after the lender missed first-quarter earnings estimates, booking a fraud-related charge and provisions for the Iran war (BBG 1, 2). HSBC reported a first-quarter pre-tax profit of $9.4 billion, marginally missing analysts' estimates, with higher expected credit losses (CNBC). The bank’s net profit was broadly flat at $6.94 billion, missing the $7.07 billion consensus, as “lower interest rates and provisions linked to the Middle East conflict offset solid growth in its wealth management business” (SCMP). HSBC’s CFO said there is still confidence in achieving a full-year RoTE of 17% or higher, but noted that the outlook for Hong Kong commercial real estate is “weak” due to “uncertain inflation and interest rate trends” (HKEJ 1, 2).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: HSBC’s miss reinforces the stagflationary tone of this macro regime — margin compression from lower rates and war-related credit losses hitting a bellwether for Asian trade and wealth flows.
    • Tactical Trade: Short HSI; PIVOT to neutral if ISM Services PMI prints above 54.0, signaling resilient US demand that could lift risk appetite.

    📌 Strait of Hormuz: Fire on South Korean Ship, Seoul rebuffs Trump¶

    Fact South Korea will investigate a fire on one of its vessels in the Strait of Hormuz, with President Trump blaming the incident on an Iranian attack (CNA). Seoul has rebuffed Trump’s call to join “Project Freedom,” indicating that “a UN mandate and a global coalition are required before Seoul joins Trump’s mission to reopen the strait” (SCMP). A US-flagged Maersk commercial vessel transited the strait under US military protection (BBC), which contributed to oil prices easing from their highs, though the IEA warned of a “dangerous escalation” (Al Jazeera).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: Iran’s strikes on the UAE and a South Korean vessel harden the supply-risk premium, complicating the risk-on trade for Asian FX and supporting the case for elevated BOK hawkishness.
    • Tactical Trade: Long USD/KRW; PIVOT to short USD/KRW if South Korea announces it will join Project Freedom, signaling a de-escalation of bilateral risk.
