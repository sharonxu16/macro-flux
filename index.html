🔬 Narrative Watch

📌 Fed: Warsh In, Powell Out — But the Real Fight Is Over Rate Cuts vs. Oil Shock

Fact | Source: (FT), (CNBC), (Bloomberg), (CNN) The Fed subtly signaled that only rate cuts are on the table in its latest statement, triggering dissents from three officials (Kashkari, Hammack, and Bowman) who argued the Iran war oil shock has fundamentally changed the rate outlook (Bloomberg). Kevin Warsh faces an immediate dilemma in his first meeting as chair: either stare down White House demands for immediate cuts or confront hawkish colleagues who see inflation risks rising. Powell has vowed he won't be a "shadow chair," but the clash is unavoidable. Kalshi bettors predict Powell will stay on as Fed governor through August, attending two more FOMC meetings (CNBC).

[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens — The Fed is institutionally fractured. The dissenters are correct that the oil supply shock is stagflationary (higher inflation + lower growth), making a cut premature. Warsh's political pressure to ease conflicts with the data.

[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact — Favors flattening of the US 2s10s curve as front-end hikes are repriced and long-end term premium rises on fiscal concerns. Supports long USD vs. EUR and JPY on rate differential, but the trade is crowded.

📌 Global Central Bank Divergence: ECB/BOE/RBA Hike While Fed Cuts

Fact | Source: (FT), (Bloomberg), (Reuters) The ECB is leaning toward a June rate hike, with Bundesbank's Nagel saying one is needed "unless the outlook improves markedly" (Bloomberg). The BOE cannot shield households from the Iran energy crisis, Bailey said, and its modeling points to higher rates this year (FT). The RBA is expected to raise its cash rate to 4.35% on May 5 (Reuters). The BOJ intervened with an estimated $34.5B to support the yen, but oil prices threaten to overwhelm that effort (MarketWatch).

[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens — This is the most significant G-7 central bank divergence since 2022. A Fed cutting while ECB/BOE hike would put structural downward pressure on EUR/USD and GBP/USD, but also create a carry trade opportunity into USD-funded EM FX.

[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact — Supports a long USD position vs. EUR and GBP. The yen is a special case — intervention provides a floor but oil import costs create structural selling pressure. Favors short EUR/JPY.

📌 Trump Escalates Trade Wars: EU Auto Tariffs to 25%, Expanded Cuba Sanctions

Fact | Source: (CNBC), (FT), (SCMP) Trump announced he will raise tariffs on EU cars and trucks to 25% "next week," accusing the bloc of not complying with the trade deal (FT). The move could cost Germany nearly $18 billion in output (Reuters). Separately, Trump signed an executive order expanding Cuba sanctions with global reach, targeting foreign banks and firms that do business with the island — potentially implicating Chinese entities (SCMP). EU Parliament Trade Chief called the auto tariff threat "unacceptable" (Bloomberg).

[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens — The tariff escalation is a negative supply shock for European auto exporters and a positive for US domestic producers. The Cuba sanctions signal a broader secondary sanctions regime that could ensnare Chinese banks, adding to RMB depreciation pressure.

[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact — Supports short EUR/USD and short European auto sector equities (e.g., via Euro Stoxx 50). Favors long USD vs. CNH on secondary sanctions risk.

📌 Iran War: Trump Declares Hostilities "Terminated" to Sidestep War Powers Deadline

Fact | Source: (Reuters), (SCMP), (CNN) Trump told Congress the Iran conflict "has terminated" ahead of the 60-day war powers deadline, claiming there has been no exchange of fire since April 7 (Reuters). However, Iran sent a new peace proposal via Pakistan that Trump said he is "dissatisfied with" (CNBC). A GOP senator said Trump doesn't need Congress for war powers (CNN). The Strait of Hormuz blockade continues, with oil briefly hitting $126/bbl before falling back on the peace proposal news (BBC). OPEC+ agreed in principle on a small output hike of 188,000 bpd in June, excluding the UAE (Bloomberg).

[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens — The "terminated" declaration is semantic — the blockade remains in place, oil prices stay elevated, and the economic damage (Spirit Airlines bankruptcy, fertilizer shortages, food price risks) continues. The small OPEC+ hike is insufficient to offset the supply loss from Hormuz.

[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact — Favors long oil (Brent) on structural supply deficit. The $126/bbl spike shows the market is pricing in a prolonged blockade. Supports long USD vs. Asia FX (CNH, KRW, TWD) on energy import cost channel.
