📌 Trump Declares Iran War "Terminated" — Sidesteps Congress

Fact | Source: (Reuters, WSJ, SCMP) Trump sent letters to the House and Senate stating hostilities "have terminated" as of April 7 ceasefire, preempting the 60-day War Powers Resolution deadline that would have required congressional authorization. However, Trump expressed dissatisfaction with the current state of negotiations (SCMP). Separately, the US Treasury warned shippers not to pay Hormuz tolls, even in the form of "charity" (Reuters). US withdrew 5,000 troops from Germany, fulfilling a Trump threat (Reuters, SCMP).

[AI Reasoning] Macro Logic — Trump's unilateral termination declaration is a legal/political maneuver, not a strategic reset. The Strait of Hormuz remains blocked. [AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Short oil on any diplomatic breakthrough, but only if Hormuz reopening is explicitly discussed. Long USD/JPY on Japan intervention exhaustion. Watch for a spike in European defense stocks on the Germany withdrawal signal.

📌 Trump Hikes EU Auto Tariffs to 25% — Trade War Escalates

Fact | Source: (BBC, FT, SCMP) Trump announced on Truth Social that he will increase tariffs on EU cars and trucks to 25% next week, accusing the EU of "not complying with our fully agreed to Trade Deal" from last July's Turnberry agreement (which set most EU goods at 15%). The European Commission responded it will "keep our options open to protect EU interests" and is seeking "clarity" from the US (BBC). The EU Parliament's trade chief called the threat "unacceptable" (Bloomberg). UK exports to the US have already plunged 25% after Trump's "Liberation Day" tariffs (CNBC).

[AI Reasoning] Macro Logic — This is a direct violation of the Turnberry deal framework. The EU will retaliate, likely targeting US tech and agricultural exports. The transatlantic trade relationship just went from fragile to adversarial. This compounds the Iran-driven supply shock with a demand-side trade shock for European automakers (VW, BMW, Mercedes — all heavily exposed to US exports).

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Short European auto sector (EUSX: SXAP). Long European defense (since the US is also withdrawing troops from Germany — see above). Short EUR/USD on widening trade tensions. Monitor for EU retaliation list — that will determine which US sectors get hit.

The market is pricing cuts into 2027, but the Fed is signaling hikes are back on the table if oil stays elevated. This is a regime change for rate-sensitive assets.

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Long GBP (BOE hawkishness vs. ECB/BOJ dovishness). Short UK gilts (long end). Monitor UK CPI prints for confirmation.
