📌 The Iran War: Oil Shock, Peace Hopes, and the War Powers Deadline

Fact | Source: (BBC, FT, CNBC, Reuters, WSJ, SCMP) Brent crude touched $126.31/bbl — highest since 2022 — after Axios reported the US military is preparing a plan for "short and powerful" strikes on Iran. The price then fell back sharply after Iran delivered an updated peace proposal via Pakistan. Trump told Congress that hostilities "have terminated" ahead of the War Powers deadline, but also said he is "dissatisfied" with the Iranian proposal. The Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed, with ~20% of global oil and LNG flows still blocked. US regular gasoline hit $4.42/gallon, up nearly 50% since the war began (MarketWatch).

[AI Reasoning] Macro Logic — The oil market is pricing a binary outcome: a negotiated reopening of Hormuz (deflationary) vs. a sustained blockade or escalation (inflationary). The wild intraday volatility reflects maximum uncertainty. The real risk is that the 'terminated' ceasefire is a legal fiction — the blockade persists, and energy supply continues to bleed.

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Long Brent volatility via options; short airline and consumer discretionary equities exposed to fuel costs. The Spirit Airlines bankruptcy is the canary. Monitor the War Powers deadline — if Congress challenges Trump's 'termination' claim, it's a tail risk for risk assets.

📌 The Fed: Powell's Defiant Curtain Call, Warsh's First Vote, and a 3-Dissent Shock

Fact | Source: (CNBC, Bloomberg, Bloomberg, CNBC, FT) Three Fed officials — Kashkari, Hammack, and Bowman — dissented at the April FOMC meeting, arguing the statement's hint that the next move would be a cut was inappropriate given the Iran war-driven inflation risks. Incoming Chair Kevin Warsh faces an immediate dilemma: stare down White House demands for rate cuts or side with hawkish colleagues. Powell said he will stay on as Fed governor after his chair term ends (CNBC). The dissent ratio of 3/12 is well above the >= 2 threshold that triggers a market shock alert.

[AI Reasoning] Macro Logic — The Fed is now openly divided on the policy path. Warsh's first vote will be a signal of his independence. If he dissents from the hold (voting for a cut), it validates the market's dovish pricing. If he joins the hawks, it's a repricing risk for rates. The dissenters' core argument — that the war has changed the outlook — is correct: this is a supply shock, not a demand shock.

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Short 2-year Treasuries on any dovish Warsh signal; the market is pricing cuts that a supply-shock environment does not justify. A 3-dissent FOMC is historically a volatility regime shift for rates and FX.

📌 Japan's $34.5B Yen Intervention

Fact | Source: (Bloomberg, Investing.com, CNBC, FT) Japan likely spent $34.5B on May 1 — its first intervention since July 2024 — to prop up the yen after it weakened past 160/USD on Iran war and hawkish Fed fears. The yen surged sharply after the intervention. Citigroup exited its yen trade, citing oil risks (Investing.com). Japan warned it is ready to intervene again (Reuters).

[AI Reasoning] Macro Logic — Japan is fighting a losing battle against a macro force — the oil shock — that is structurally weakening the yen. Intervention can slow the pace but not reverse the trend. The $34.5B is large, but the BOJ's firepower is finite. The risk of a "sniper attack" on the yen by speculative accounts is high.

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Short USD/JPY on any further rallies above 155; the intervention provides a floor but not a durable trend. The oil shock is the dominant driver — watch Brent for the next leg in yen.

📌 ECB/BOE: Hawkish Pivot on the Iran War

Fact | Source: (FT, Bloomberg, Bloomberg, CNBC) The ECB and BOE both warned of potential rate rises as the Iran war drives up energy prices. ECB's Nagel favors a June hike unless the outlook improves markedly. BOE's Bailey said the central bank cannot shield households from the energy crisis, and its sole dissenter called for "quick rate hikes" (Bloomberg). The G-7 is now penciling in possible rate hikes as soon as June (Bloomberg).

[AI Reasoning] Macro Logic — The global rate cycle is bifurcating: the Fed is on hold with a dovish lean (but hawkish dissenters), while the ECB and BOE are openly discussing hikes. This is a regime change from the 2024-2025 easing narrative. The common driver is the Iran war — a supply shock that is stagflationary for Europe and the UK, which are more energy-import dependent.

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Long EUR/USD on rate differentials; the ECB is now the most hawkish major central bank. Short UK Gilts on the BOE's hiking bias. The divergence between the Fed (on hold) and ECB (hiking) favors a stronger euro.

The whisky tariff removal is a cosmetic gesture — the real damage is in the auto sector.

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Short European auto sector equities (Stellantis, Volkswagen, BMW). Long USD vs. EUR on trade war uncertainty. The 25% tariff is a material earnings headwind for the sector.
