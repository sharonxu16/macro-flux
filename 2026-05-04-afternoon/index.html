    # 🌊 Macro Flux May 4 8AM to May 4 6PM HKT Good afternoon.

    Overview

    Intraday price action was dominated by a violent two-way swing in crude and Asian currencies after Iran claimed to have hit a US warship with missiles in the Strait of Hormuz—a claim swiftly denied by Washington—while a tanker attack off Fujairah kept the physical supply risk firmly on the table. Brent spiked intraday before paring gains on the US denial, while KRW and KOSPI rallied to new records on AI optimism, decoupling from the geopolitical noise as the BOK signalled an eventual rate hike. China's unprecedented activation of "Blocking Rules" against US sanctions on oil refiners is now the dominant macro overhang for CNH and the broader decoupling narrative. Across markets, tech earnings momentum is grinding Asian equities ex-Japan to all-time highs, while the ECB confirmed a June rate hike is "all but inevitable," cementing a hawkish global central bank pivot.

    🔬 Narrative Watch¶

    📌 Hormuz Live Fire: Iran Claims Missile Strike on US Warship; US Denies, Tanker Hit¶

    Fact Iranian news agency says two missiles hit a US warship trying to enter the Strait of Hormuz (Reuters). The US denied an Iranian report that the country had struck an American naval vessel with missiles, Axios reported citing a senior official (BBG 1). A tanker was hit by unknown projectiles off UAE's Fujairah, UKMTO says (Reuters). The US military supports the launch of Project Freedom in the Strait of Hormuz beginning Monday (US_CENTCOM). The Iranian military warned the US of attacks over the Hormuz mission (Al Jazeera 1). Iran's Revolutionary Guards unveiled a new map of the Strait of Hormuz under their control (Reuters). The US Hormuz plan leaves shipowners guessing as attacks persist (BBG 2). Oil prices were flat as Trump's plan for the Strait of Hormuz failed to calm the market (Al Jazeera 2). OPEC+ agreed a third oil output quota hike since the Hormuz closure, announcing a 188,000 barrels-per-day output increase in its first meeting without the UAE (CNBC). Some 32 volcanic earthquakes, including 25 volcanic tremors, were reported as more than 300 families were evacuated after massive ash from Mayon volcano blanketed towns in the Philippines (SCMP).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The live-fire denial cycle keeps Brent in a $5-7 risk premium range, with the market pricing a high probability of further attacks but no full closure.
    • Tactical Trade: Long Brent calendar spreads vs flat price; PIVOT to neutral if Project Freedom transits one vessel without incident within 48 hours.

    📌 China Activates "Blocking Rules" vs US Sanctions: Showdown Over Banks Looms¶

    Fact China ordered companies to ignore US sanctions against five oil refiners accused of buying Iranian crude, an unprecedented pushback against American financial measures less than two weeks before a presidential summit (SCMP). China’s Ministry of Commerce said the US action "violates international law and the basic norms of international relations," covering a refinery in Dalian owned by Hengli Petrochemical and four independent "teapot" refineries (SCMP). China’s rare defiance of US sanctions sparks a showdown over banks (BBG 1). An unfinished Iran war could give Xi the upper hand in Trump talks, sources say (CNN 1). China and Indonesia launched a new cross-border QR payment system, marking the latest step in Beijing’s drive to build a regional digital payments network (SCMP).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The blocking rules are a high-stakes bargaining chip ahead of the Xi-Trump summit, with real implementation uncertainty keeping UST yield bid and CNH capped.
    • Tactical Trade: Short CNH/CNY via 1m NDF; PIVOT to flat if US Treasury Secretary Bessent signals a waiver or delay in secondary sanctions enforcement.

    📌 BOK Signals Hawkish Turn; KOSPI & Asian Tech Record Rally Decouples¶

    Fact Top Bank of Korea official says it's time to consider a rate hike (BusinessTimes). South Korean stocks hit a fresh record, building on a historic monthly rally in April (CNBC). Asian stocks outside of Japan hit a record high as tech rallied, with AI chipmakers in Korea and Taiwan driving the move (BBG 1). SK Hynix shares rallied 13% after US tech firms signaled strong AI spending plans (Reuters). Hong Kong's Exchange Fund gained HK$34.5 billion (US$4.4 billion) in the first quarter, its smallest gain in five quarters (SCMP).

    [AI Reasoning]

    • Base Case: The BOK hawkish signal will cap KRW downside but reinforce the tech-liquidity tightening tension, favoring a funded short KRW vs long KOSPI trade.
    • Tactical Trade: Long KOSPI / Short KRW via futures; PIVOT to flat KRW if BOK delivers a hold with explicit hawkish guidance at the next meeting.
