### 📌 The Iran War: Oil Shock, Peace Hopes, and the War Powers Deadline

**Fact** | Source: ([BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx21m88rd14o), [FT](https://www.ft.com/content/ce575380-eccc-447c-a64e-493957d9b037), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/oil-prices-today-brent-wti-us-iran-war-trump-war-powers-deadline.html), [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/breaking/crude-futures-fall-on-new-iran-proposal-for-peace-talks-2026-05-01/), [WSJ](https://www.wsj.com/articles/iran-softens-conditions-for-resuming-peace-talks-with-the-u-s-), [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/us/article/3352193/trump-not-satisfied-irans-latest-peace-proposal-talks-stall))

The price then fell back sharply after Iran delivered an updated peace proposal via Pakistan. The Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed, with `~20%` of global oil and LNG flows still blocked. US regular gasoline hit `$4.42/gallon`, up nearly 50% since the war began ([MarketWatch](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/it-now-costs-160-to-fill-up-americas-top-selling-vehicle-as-the-iran-war-nears-its-10th-week-803077d2)).

> **[AI Reasoning] Macro Logic** — *The oil market is pricing a binary outcome: a negotiated reopening of Hormuz (deflationary) vs. a sustained blockade or escalation (inflationary). The wild intraday volatility reflects maximum uncertainty. The real risk is that the 'terminated' ceasefire is a legal fiction — the blockade persists, and energy supply continues to bleed.*

> **[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications** — *Long Brent volatility via options; short airline and consumer discretionary equities exposed to fuel costs. The Spirit Airlines bankruptcy is the canary. Monitor the War Powers deadline — if Congress challenges Trump's 'termination' claim, it's a tail risk for risk assets.*

### 📌 The Fed: Powell's Defiant Curtain Call, Warsh's First Vote, and a 3-Dissent Shock

**Fact** | Source: ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/fed-dissenters-explain-no-votes-saying-they-disagreed-with-hinting-next-move-would-be-a-cut.html), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/fed-s-kashkari-and-hammack-say-war-has-changed-rate-outlook), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-30/warsh-must-confront-risks-of-dissenting-in-his-first-fed-vote), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/inside-the-fed-powell-and-warsh-set-to-clash.html), [FT](https://www.ft.com/content/184f1dce-7ee5-4796-ae82-e18250737815))
Three Fed officials — Kashkari, Hammack, and Bowman — dissented at the April FOMC meeting, arguing the statement's hint that the next move would be a cut was inappropriate given the Iran war-driven inflation risks. Incoming Chair Kevin Warsh faces an immediate dilemma: stare down White House demands for rate cuts or side with hawkish colleagues. Powell said he will stay on as Fed governor after his chair term ends ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/kalshi-bettors-prediction-powell-to-stay-as-fed-governor.html)). The dissent ratio of `3/12` is well above the `>= 2` threshold that triggers a market shock alert.

> **[AI Reasoning] Macro Logic** — *The Fed is now openly divided on the policy path. Warsh's first vote will be a signal of his independence. If he dissents from the hold (voting for a cut), it validates the market's dovish pricing. If he joins the hawks, it's a repricing risk for rates. The dissenters' core argument — that the war has changed the outlook — is correct: this is a supply shock, not a demand shock.*

> **[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications** — *Short 2-year Treasuries on any dovish Warsh signal; the market is pricing cuts that a supply-shock environment does not justify. A 3-dissent FOMC is historically a volatility regime shift for rates and FX.*
