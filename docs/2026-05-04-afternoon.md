### 📌 China Orders Companies to Defy US Sanctions on Oil Refiners

**Fact**
China ordered companies to ignore US sanctions against five oil refiners accused of buying Iranian crude, an unprecedented pushback against American financial measures less than two weeks before a presidential summit ([SCMP](URL)). The US action “violates international law and the basic norms of international relations,” China’s Ministry of Commerce said ([BBG](URL)). The sanctions cover a refinery in Dalian owned by Shanghai‑listed Hengli Petrochemical, along with four independent “teapot” refineries ([SCMP](URL)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Base Case**: The standoff pressures USD/CNH higher toward `7.30` as markets price in a risk of US secondary sanctions on Chinese banks before the Xi‑Trump meeting.
> * **Tactical Trade**: **Long USD/CNH**; **PIVOT to flat** if China’s Commerce Ministry issues a clarifying statement showing willingness to negotiate.

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]

> * **Tactical Trade**: **Short EUR/USD**; **PIVOT to flat/neutral** if the EU announces concrete retaliation measures that risk a full‑blown transatlantic trade war.
