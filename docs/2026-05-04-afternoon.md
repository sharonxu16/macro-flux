> [!abstract] Overview
> The macro picture deteriorated sharply this afternoon as the first kinetic exchange of the "Project Freedom" era unfolded — **Iranian missiles struck a US warship** attempting to enter the Strait of Hormuz, and subsequent reports of another tanker hit off Fujairah cemented a new, more dangerous phase of direct military confrontation between the US and Iran. **Asian FX** weakened broadly, with **KRW** leading losses after the BOK signaled a potential rate hike and **CNH** under pressure from the escalation, while **USD/Asia** tilted higher across the board. **European stocks** fell on Trump’s 25% auto tariff threat, and **Brent** crude edged higher as the market priced a failed ceasefire. **Korean and Taiwanese stocks** continued their record-breaking AI-driven rally, a striking divergence from the risk-off tone in FX and rates. The singular thread across asset classes today is the market's inability to price a resolution in Hormuz, with the probability of a sustained military operation rising.
