> [!abstract] Overview
> A tentative US-Iran deal to extend the Hormuz ceasefire and reopen the Strait sent oil to a six-week low and lifted gold above `$4,500/oz` as inflation risk premia deflated, though the accord remains subject to Trump’s approval and fresh US sanctions on Iranian military oil sales underscore the fragility of the diplomatic breakthrough. The Fed’s preferred inflation gauge met `3.3%` consensus while the ECB warned that waiting on rates was “increasingly inappropriate,” reinforcing a hawkish global rate environment that is testing Chair Warsh’s ability to contain internal Fed hawks. Another front opened against China as major EU economies backed the Industrial Accelerator Act, a sweeping investment-conditioning framework designed to blunt a feared “China shock 2.0,” adding a structural trade headwind to the CNH alongside Beijing’s decision to cut the reserve requirement ratio by a full percentage point. Today’s session pivots on whether the ceasefire holds long enough for the oil risk premium to stay compressed, allowing the PBOC’s forceful easing to dominate Asia FX flows or whether geopolitical uncertainty snaps back and restores the stagflationary anchor that has defined the past two weeks.
