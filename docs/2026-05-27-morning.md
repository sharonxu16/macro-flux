> [!abstract] Overview
> The dominant macro development overnight was twin ECB Governing Council heavyweights explicitly preparing markets for a June rate rise, with chief economist Philip Lane and board member Isabel Schnabel both warning that higher energy prices from the Iran conflict are spilling into the broader economy — a signal that the global hawkish impulse is now fully synchronized across the Fed and ECB. On trade, USTR Greer announced the formal process for public comment on China tariff cuts would begin shortly while acknowledging Washington has "come to terms" with China's political-economic model, a pragmatic de-escalation signal. China's industrial profits surged 18.2% year-to-date through April, with high-tech manufacturing soaring 44.8%, underscoring domestic earnings momentum even as Hong Kong equities retreated. The cross-market thread connecting these events is a tug-of-war between synchronous G3 hawkishness anchoring front-end rates and the dollar, countered by two simultaneous de-escalation channels — Iran peace talks and US-China trade normalization — compressing risk premia that had supported energy and weighed on risk assets.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 ECB Lays Groundwork for June Rate Hike as Energy Spillover Fears Mount

**Fact**
Chief economist Philip Lane said higher energy prices are starting to spill over into broader price pressures across the eurozone economy, joining board member Isabel Schnabel in warning that the ECB must act to contain second-round effects ahead of the central bank's June meeting ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/e9f01829-1c6a-4200-a7fd-c1c8ceae8304)). Bank of France governor François Villeroy de Galhau told CNBC the ECB "will do what is necessary" to tame inflation, explicitly linking the Iran conflict's energy price impact to the policy outlook ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/26/ecb-villeroy-inflation-iran-war.html)). In a separate interview with Nikkei, Lane reinforced the message that the ECB's assessment of underlying inflation dynamics had shifted, warranting a policy response ([ECB_Press](https://www.ecb.europa.eu//press/inter/date/2026/html/ecb.in260526_1~71caa51b14.en.html)). The ECB also warned in its forthcoming Financial Stability Review that the private-credit-fueled AI investment boom poses risks to the financial system, with investors potentially exposed to losses if the technology does not meet its potential ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/7ecdff9f-4f3a-40dd-b984-9860097dd083)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — the prior Macro State flagged Schnabel's June-hike call, but Lane joining her and Villeroy explicitly endorsing necessity transforms this from a single hawkish voice into Governing Council consensus.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Rate-differential channel favors **long EUR/USD** and **short EUR rates** front-end; global spillover supports **higher UST yields** and **broad USD** strength against EM Asia FX.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: ECB June meeting minutes leak or pre-meeting media guidance in the next 48h confirming the size and unanimity of the hawkish shift; Confidence: High given three named Governing Council members on record.

### 📌 US-China Trade De-escalation Accelerates as Tariff Review Process Begins

**Fact**
USTR Jamieson Greer said the US government will shortly issue a formal call for public comment on which Chinese products should qualify for lower tariffs, adding that the administration has "come to terms" with the fact that China will not fundamentally change its political-economic model ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3354957/us-seek-public-comment-which-chinese-goods-get-tariff-cuts)). Greer also confirmed the process to form a China Board of Trade — a mechanism to adjudicate tariff classifications — will start shortly ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-26/greer-says-process-to-form-china-board-of-trade-to-start-shortly)). Separately, Greer said the US will maintain tariffs on USMCA countries, describing issues with Canada as "significant," indicating that trade normalization with China does not extend to all partners ([Reuters](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiogFBVV95cUxNZEJVOXkzN0twSExVQ29ud1ZmckM0Y3VjNmUzOW1ubllsQUNweWFhNkxYYXBlRXhFM1FBLUZoT2xLVFh3MFRtcmxZSXU3NER1UkU5VG5zTnlTYUhHZU5EeWkzdjZ0YUtoMHZ1Z3BZYzZ6Tm5CcWdvT1FqRHgwZEVMY3ZZWEdWYmVlYnpsRG5qWkVjMVVaQk5XRTN3bW5VWVFjTWc)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New — the Macro State had no active trade de-escalation narrative; Greer's formal launch of the public-comment process and the "come to terms" language represent a genuine policy shift from prior maximum-pressure posture.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Capital-flow channel supports **long CNH** and **long China equities** via reduced tariff uncertainty; the "come to terms" language reduces tail risk of renewed escalation, favoring compression of China equity risk premium.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Publication of the Federal Register notice with specific product categories and timeline; also Greer's testimony or speech elaborating on the scope; Confidence: Medium — process announced but actual tariff cuts remain undefined.
