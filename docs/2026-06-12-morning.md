> [!abstract] Overview
> A dramatic collapse in geopolitical risk premia drove a powerful cross-asset rally after President Trump cancelled military strikes on Iran and signalled a peace deal could be signed within days. Oil prices plunged to their lowest since April, the dollar fell by the most in a month, and global equities surged as haven demand evaporated. The European Central Bank delivered its first rate hike since 2023, explicitly citing the Iran war as an inflation accelerant, but the hawkish signal was completely overshadowed by the peace narrative. US producer prices surged at the fastest annual pace in three-and-a-half years, and an appeals court ruling locking in Trump’s 10% global tariffs reinforced persistent inflationary pressure. The dominant cross-market thread is the rapid repricing of the entire “Iran war premium” that has anchored long energy and short risk positioning for over 100 days.
