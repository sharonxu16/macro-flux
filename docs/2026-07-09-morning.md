> [!abstract] Overview
> A second day of US airstrikes on Iran and President Trump’s declaration that the ceasefire is “over” sent oil prices surging, reigniting global inflation fears and reshaping the rate outlook. The Fed’s June minutes compounded the hawkish tilt, revealing a divided committee where “a few” officials saw a case for hiking rates and where AI investment was flagged as a new inflation risk. The dollar reversed course as these inflation fears offset safe-haven flows, while oil-importing Asian equities and currencies came under pressure, and gold held its decline on the rates repricing. The dominant cross-market thread is an oil-supply-shock-driven inflation repricing that is raising the odds of a more protracted Fed tightening cycle, pressuring duration assets and Asia FX simultaneously.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Fed minutes reveal divided committee, with AI flagged as an inflation risk

**Fact**
A few Federal Reserve officials in their most recent policy meeting said there was a case for raising interest rates, though they ultimately supported the decision to leave rates on hold, according to the minutes of the Federal Open Market Committee’s June 16-17 meeting ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-08/fed-minutes-show-a-few-officials-saw-case-for-june-rate-hike)). “A few” FOMC members supported a `25 bps` hike, while “several others” noted the economy’s resilience could justify tighter policy ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/b1eda604-0593-4f56-af1c-ccaf3619efd5)). The minutes also showed Fed staff flagged artificial intelligence investment as one of three main upside risks to inflation going forward, alongside geopolitical developments and stronger-than-expected consumer spending ([WSJCN](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiU0FVX3lxTE53c2ZrSksxOU5lRXRhSmFIX3kyZVM1QnEwOTFYLXhld0pnXzVtUTZhZC00dmpKaUJkemJDclFQNWRkNnh1b3lIRUQwa0RQUF9vV2lj)). US real yields rose to a more than one-year high, with AI investment supporting economic resilience and the oil spike rekindling rate-hike bets ([WSJCN](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiU0FVX3lxTE1FTFBFZTN5THgwczMwYTJWbmxOYTZEQUJRS2d0VUR6YXFBRHg5bi0xWk80X3pkbFVDbDlzbTNSNEJwUHB0Ti1MRTZrTEpNMThvX2Fj)). The `10`-year Treasury yield climbed to `4.57%` as higher oil prices reignited inflation fears ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/08/treasury-yields-trump-iran-ceasefire.html)).

### 📌 Samsung, SK Hynix and leveraged ETFs dominate Korea trading, amplifying downside fragility

**Fact**
Retail fervor over leveraged ETFs in South Korea has grown so intense that the products, together with the two chipmaker stocks they track, now make up more than `70%` of trading value in the `$4.3 trillion` market ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-08/samsung-sk-hynix-and-leveraged-etfs-drive-70-of-korea-trading)). The concentration reflects extreme retail positioning in AI-exposed names and instruments, amplifying swings as the Kospi entered bear-market territory in prior sessions.

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]

> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk-premium channel supports **short KRW** and **short Kospi**; volatility amplification likely on any further AI rotation or geopolitical shock.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Korean regulator announcement on stabilisation measures or margin-call data; Confidence: Medium — structural data is clear but timing of intervention is uncertain.
