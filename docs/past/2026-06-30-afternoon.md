> [!abstract] Overview
> The EU ratcheted up trade pressure on China, setting an October deadline for “tangible results” on its deficit and slashing tariff-free steel quotas by a third, a hardening that dragged CNH and European cyclicals lower while boosting haven demand. ECB officials remained split on the case for another rate hike in September after French inflation unexpectedly fell back to the 2% target, with hawks like Nagel warning of lingering upside risks and doves such as Wunsch calling the prior hike less justified now, leaving EUR/USD pinned in a narrow range ahead of Sintra speeches. South Korea tightened property curbs to cool a chip‑boom‑driven housing market, and US retailers front‑loaded China holiday orders, hinting at pre‑tariff pull‑forward. The day’s thread: a trade‑war escalation and robust dollar, but with tentative Middle East de‑escalation capping oil, leaving Asia FX under divergent pressures — CNH vulnerable to tariffs, JPY to Fed‑BOJ divergence, and KRW to domestic macro‑prudential tightening.

### 📌 EU Hardens Trade Stance on China with Quota Cut and October Deadline

**Fact**
The European Union set an October deadline for “tangible results” in tackling its growing trade deficit with China after the two sides held talks in Brussels in an attempt to avoid a trade war ([SCMP 1](https://www.scmp.com/plus/news/china/diplomacy/article/3358895/eu-seeks-tangible-results-china-trade-deficit-october)). China and the EU released a joint statement agreeing to establish a joint platform to monitor trade flows and to set up working groups to ease bilateral economic tensions ([SCMP 2](https://www.scmp.com/plus/news/china/diplomacy/article/3358867/china-eu-joint-statement-trade)). The EU will reserve half of its steel quotas for countries with free‑trade deals and cut tariff‑free steel quotas by 33% for close trade partners under new measures meant to protect local industry from mostly Chinese imports ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-30/eu-cuts-tariff-free-steel-quota-33-for-close-trade-partners)). EU trade commissioner Maros Sefcovic hosted Chinese Commerce Minister Wang Wentao, and the bloc is getting tough on China as trade imbalance stokes deindustrialisation fears ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/6/30/eu-gets-tough-on-china-as-trade-imbalance-stokes-deindustrialisation-fears)). ### 📌 ECB Divided as French Inflation Eases, Officials Split on September Hike

**Fact**
French inflation unexpectedly slowed for the first time since the Iran war broke out, falling back to the ECB’s 2% target as energy costs fell ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-30/french-inflation-eases-to-lowest-since-march-as-oil-prices-drop)). ECB Chief Economist Philip Lane said second‑round inflation effects may take some time to appear, signaling the central bank remains concerned about price risks ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-30/ecb-s-lane-sees-second-round-effects-taking-some-time-to-appear)). Bundesbank President Joachim Nagel warned that inflation is likely to stay “significantly above target” even if the Iran war ends ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/30/high-inflation-could-linger-despite-iran-war-ending-ecbs-nagel-says.html)). Governing Council member Pierre Wunsch said the case for another rate hike is not as strong now as it was ([BBG 3](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-30/ecb-s-wunsch-says-case-for-another-rate-hike-not-as-strong-now)). Apollo’s Torsten Slok sees an ECB hike in September as possible despite the energy drop ([BBG 4](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-30/slok-says-ecb-may-hike-again-in-september-despite-energy-drop)), while council member Olaf Sleijpen cautioned the full extent of the inflation shock is not yet clear ([BBG 5](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-30/ecb-s-sleijpen-says-extent-of-inflation-shock-remains-to-be-seen)).
