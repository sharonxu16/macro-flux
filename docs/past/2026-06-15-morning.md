> [!abstract] Overview
> Risk assets surged and oil collapsed after the US and Iran said they reached a peace deal, with President Trump declaring the Strait of Hormuz would reopen upon a signing ceremony penciled for Friday. Brent crude plunged more than `4%` toward `$86`, while Asian equities jumped and the dollar weakened as safe‑haven demand unwound. The deal’s reported contours — oil‑sanctions waivers, nuclear limits, and a gradual asset release — were muddied by Tehran’s last‑minute hedging and Israeli airstrikes on Beirut, keeping execution risk alive. The abrupt repricing of geopolitical supply risk sets the stage for a momentous week that includes Kevin Warsh’s first FOMC meeting, a likely BOJ rate hike, and China’s May activity data. The cross‑market thread is a massive unwind of the Iran‑war risk premium, favoring a short‑term rotation out of energy and havens into Asian FX and cyclicals, tempered by fragility in Chinese domestic demand.
