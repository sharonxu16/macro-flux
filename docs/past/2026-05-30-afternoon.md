> [!abstract] Overview
> The afternoon leaned risk-on as Tehran peace talks approached a decisive moment, lifting European equities to monthly gains on hopes of a reopened Strait of Hormuz. That tailwind was partly offset by US Defense Secretary Hegseth’s Shangri-La address, which combined praise for Asian allies and a swipe at Europe with pointed criticism of China’s role and a reference to the Taiwan arms sale, injecting fresh alliance friction into Asia FX. Away from the headlines, Singapore’s accelerating business migration to Malaysia highlighted structural cost pressures that weigh on the city-state’s medium-term growth trajectory. Twin forces of a nearing Iran deal and elevated US-China alliance unease are pulling Brent and regional FX in opposite directions, leaving markets fragile to any breakdown in either narrative.
