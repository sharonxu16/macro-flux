> [!abstract] Overview
> The Strait of Hormuz crisis reignited, sending Brent crude up nearly 10% in its biggest daily surge since 2020 after President Trump reimposed a blockade on Iranian ships and demanded a 20% toll on all other cargo. Treasury yields climbed and equities tumbled as Fed Governor Waller warned a hot inflation print this week could force a rate hike as soon as the July meeting. ### 📌 US-Iran Blockade Reignites, Sends Oil Surging; Fed's Waller Opens Door to July Rate Hike

**Fact**
President Donald Trump reinstated the US blockade of Iranian ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz and demanded a 20% reimbursement on all other cargo shipped through the waterway ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-july-14)). Brent crude surged almost 10% on Monday, its biggest one-day gain since April 2020, settling at `$83.25/bbl`, up 9.5% ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/12/oil-price-strait-hormuz-iran-trump-tanker.html), [WSJ](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMilAFBVV95cUxOZVRBVFg5a0hrbVoybUtRQlB3RVNDS2RZUUIzU29POVFyVmJvdXBCczVUbDFaTVdYX0lDR05yNWdZZ3FOUnRPVTlnY1JObEIzVVo2Yjd5aGRLa2pRd09EN2FFM1FFRmYyQ3VvM1JRb1VBbEhqMC0zQTRJSW5xeUI0eFg3UW9UZlJ6OVlPSzgxak1UY3JQ)). The US launched air strikes as Iran targeted tankers with cruise missiles, and Trump said Washington would hit Iran “hard” on Monday and Tuesday nights ([FT 1](https://www.ft.com/content/34f93b7e-b1a7-40c3-8e6e-a5cac7dbaa9c)). Federal Reserve Governor Christopher Waller said the central bank may need to raise interest rates if core inflation remains elevated, adding a move could come as soon as the July 28-29 meeting ([Reuters](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMipAFBVV95cUxNaW0tUldZeEtOTXVXNFlST05jYjZUMzcxcVZQNl9MVXh3ZXltWVVUcUlMelA2OENsVmdtSDNqTUVBSTBNdEVpbzg3Q1E2N28wMVBlYlZCVS1SYU5XUkVuMUdtUzUwcEJrWmRDYVp2ajg3cHdXMnpHbHhfSHc4Z2VaNmpVZUNrLTFKY2hBdmFwNWt3b2xJdzdRYzFpbUMxSERsdmtTZQ)). Market-implied odds for a quarter-point July hike rose to about 35%, up from near zero last week ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/-a-july-rate-hike-from-the-fed-the-odds-are-rising.html)).

> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Oil-supply shock feeds into inflation expectations, forcing rate-hike repricing — favors **long Brent**, **short US equities**, **long USD** versus Asia FX, and widens EM risk premia.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: US CPI release on Tuesday; a core print above `0.2% MoM` cements the hike narrative; Confidence: High given cross-source confirmation of both the blockade and Waller’s explicit warning.

### 📌 Singapore GDP Growth Slows to 5.7% as Geopolitical Disruptions Bite

**Fact**
Singapore’s gross domestic product grew `5.7%` in the April-June period from a year earlier, down from `6.3%` in the previous quarter, according to advance estimates from the Ministry of Trade and Industry ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/singapore-s-economic-growth-slows-to-5-7-in-second-quarter)). Growth was tempered by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East that damped trade in services and goods ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/singapore-s-economic-growth-slows-to-5-7-in-second-quarter)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Deceleration from a strong AI-driven base; the Hormuz spillover is now showing in hard data.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Growth channel tilts risks toward a less hawkish MAS, keeping SGD in a narrow range but not yet triggering easing.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: MAS policy communication in coming weeks; Confidence: Medium.
