> [!abstract] Overview
> China’s central bank unveiled a sweeping overhaul of its monetary framework, creating a new repo tool for foreign central banks and narrowing the overnight rate corridor to `50bp` in a bid to anchor short‑term rates and internationalize the yuan. The reform signals Beijing is moving toward a Fed‑style floor system, a structural shift for onshore liquidity and cross‑border CNY funding. In Europe, ECB officials pushed back against oil‑driven disinflation, with one rate‑setter explicitly forecasting at least one more hike, while the ECB’s own wage tracker points to re‑accelerating pay growth in the second half. Meanwhile, physical crude markets showed oil tankers already re‑routing toward the Middle East ahead of the Strait of Hormuz’s expected reopening, amplifying the downside pressure on Brent. The common thread is a re‑calibration of policy and supply risk just hours before the Federal Reserve’s first decision under Chair Warsh.

**Fact**
中国人民银行行长潘功胜在2026陆家嘴论坛上宣布，央行将创设境外央行类机构回购工具，允许境外央行类机构使用持有的在岸市场人民币债券作为抵押品，通过央行公开市场业务一级交易商融入人民币资金，以此进一步推进人民币国际化和金融市场开放 ([Caixin](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiYkFVX3lxTE9jc3U3bmtXQ1NvQWxPUDBmYXI4SmtEc3ZvY1FyN3laUkZRMGlmM2hKSkhPSUFpSGRnMUpnNWszbnVXM3JxazIwczRTd3dqVWstSXRsdDZUamtxTTAyMTJHeG1n))。同时，潘功胜表示央行将进一步完善以7天期逆回购操作利率为短期政策利率的调控框架，将临时隔夜回购利率走廊的宽度从100个基点收窄至50个基点，以增强对短期市场利率的引导作用，促进市场形成稳定预期 ([Caixin](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiYkFVX3lxTE9NQ3dsS3BTenpOZTk2X1FYbTkyZWpJMnN6WUJYdnpRZkFBTEZJZHBWa1JPMGgtUU02cnVueHhqVE1tRzFXN2dYdXM5ZmRhd25Jamx2VTY1bGljQ09SZm1MMjZB))。The People’s Bank of China said it would create a new tool to allow foreign central banks to borrow yuan using onshore bonds as collateral, a move aimed at easing access to the currency and boosting its international use ([BBG](http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-17/pboc-announces-tool-to-boost-yuan-usage-by-foreign-central-banks))。PBOC Governor Pan Gongsheng said the central bank will narrow the width of the overnight repo interest-rate corridor to `50` basis points from `100` basis points, a move that’s part of efforts to strengthen the benchmark role of short-term policy rates ([BBG](http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-17/pboc-hints-at-new-policy-framework-focused-on-overnight-rate))。Pan also stressed that the central bank will steadily promote the application of the digital yuan in offshore business ([Caixin](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiYkFVX3lxTFBBdGV1Rnc2VFFud0RKUGY1TnVvYnVRUDR4WUFDRzBNM2U4YnM0VU9rVTVxYkZEZVZxaGpoNzI2S3VncW84NTlTaEFYWlBBaG1FYlZLX3llRlBGRU5zRDY3MWNB))。

### 📌 ECB Officials Push Back, Warning Iran Peace Won’t End Energy Shock

**Fact**
European Central Bank officials warned that the recent peace deal between the US and Iran is unlikely to fully resolve the energy supply shock that has kept inflation above target ([BBG](http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-17/ecb-officials-say-peace-in-iran-isn-t-enough-to-fix-energy-shock))。Gediminas Simkus, a member of the ECB’s Governing Council, said in an interview that he sees “at least one more” increase in borrowing costs ([BBG](http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-17/ecb-s-simkus-sees-at-least-one-more-interest-rate-increase))。Euro‑area pay growth is set to accelerate in the second half of this year, while remaining far below previous peaks ([BBG](http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-17/euro-zone-wage-growth-to-quicken-in-second-half-of-this-year))。The ECB’s experimental wage tracker points to stable negotiated wage pressures in 2026 ([ECB_Press](https://www.ecb.europa.eu//press/pr/date/2026/html/ecb.pr260617~79dfc49802.en.html))。

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — Multiple ECB officials explicitly link hawkish guidance to persistent energy risk beyond the Hormuz deal, reinforcing the bank’s rate‑hike bias even as oil slides.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Inflation‑expectations channel tilts toward **higher EUR/USD** and **steeper front‑end Euro rates**, limiting the disinflation optimism from the crude sell‑off.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Any softening of hawkish language after the Fed decision tonight or if oil breaks below recent lows; Confidence: Medium given clear official comments but potential for data‑dependent flip.

### 📌 Oil Tankers Rush to Middle East, Signaling Physical Markets Price in Hormuz Reopening

**Fact**
Two oil tankers heading toward Africa have U‑turned in the Indian Ocean this week, switching their destinations to the Middle East as shipowners race to re‑position vessels ahead of the possible reopening of the Strait of Hormuz ([BBG](http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-17/oil-tankers-u-turn-rush-to-middle-east-before-hormuz-reopening))。

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — Physical fleet repositioning translates diplomatic reopening into concrete supply normalization, accelerating the crude disinflation narrative.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Commodity‑supply channel supports **short Brent** and **long energy‑sensitive equities**, further compressing the energy risk premium.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: First commercial convoys transiting without incident; Confidence: High because ship‑tracking data is a lead indicator for actual barrel flows.
