> [!abstract] Overview
> The BOJ’s hike to `1%` and Deputy Governor Uchida’s signal of further tightening send the policy rate to a 31‑year high and drag the yen off its recent lows, reinforcing a hawkish turn that ripples through Asian rate markets. China’s May activity data plunge — retail sales contracted for the first time since Covid and property prices accelerated their fall — douses the risk‑on mood stoked by the Iran peace deal, pulling CNH and Chinese equities lower. The deal itself loses immediate punch as shipowners demand “material” proof before re‑entering the Strait of Hormuz and Iran insists on a Lebanon ceasefire, while Trump pivots from the war to fresh tariff threats that revive EU trade‑war premium. The day’s dominant thread is a cross‑current between a hawkish BOJ and fresh China domestic weakness, with the Hormuz reopening now a slower‑burn supply normalization rather than an overnight disinflationary stimulus.

### 📌 BOJ hikes to 1%, Uchida says rates will keep rising; bond‑purchase taper to end in 2027

**Fact**
The Bank of Japan raised its policy rate to `1%` from `0.75%`, the highest level since 1995, and signaled it would continue to raise the rate depending on economic activity and prices ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-16/boj-hikes-rate-to-31-year-high-at-meeting-with-governor-absent)). ### 📌 China retail sales contract for first time since Covid, property slide deepens

**Fact**
China’s retail sales fell `1.1%` year‑on‑year in May, the first decline since the pandemic era, while fixed‑asset investment growth slumped to `1.2%` in the first five months from `1.8%` in January‑April ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-16/china-s-first-consumer-spending-drop-since-covid-imperils-growth)). Industrial output rose `2.8%` from a year earlier, down from `3.3%` in April ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/f0238d1d-8b35-4686-84f7-cdf90a628ebf)). New‑home prices in 70 cities, excluding state‑subsidised housing, fell `0.2%` month‑on‑month in May, accelerating from a `0.1%` decline in April and halting the green shoots seen at the start of the year ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-16/china-home-prices-fall-at-faster-pace-in-setback-to-revival)). The weakness in domestic demand contrasts with booming exports and the potential relief from a US‑Iran accord, raising downside risks to Beijing’s full‑year growth target ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-16/china-s-first-consumer-spending-drop-since-covid-imperils-growth)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — domestic demand deterioration has surprised to the downside, adding pressure to the fragile recovery.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Growth and capital‑flow channels favor **short CNH** and **short China equities** on rising policy‑easing expectations.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Any PBOC LPR cut or fiscal stimulus announcement in the next 48‑72h; Confidence: High.
