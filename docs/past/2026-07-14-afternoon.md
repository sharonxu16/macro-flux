## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Hormuz Crisis Spirals: Oil at $87, Air Strikes Extend, Iran Uses Dark Fleet

**Fact**
Oil hit `$87` a barrel for the first time since 2023 as the battle for the Strait of Hormuz alarmed energy markets, triggering a fall in stocks and bonds as fears of a renewed inflation shock intensified ([FT 1](https://www.ft.com/content/2d899824-5f94-4f0c-81fc-c54a0750e498)). The US completed a third consecutive night of strikes against Iranian military assets while Tehran targeted tankers with cruise missiles, and President Trump demanded a `20%` fee on all cargoes passing through the waterway ([CNBC 1](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/us-iran-hormuz-strikes-oil-toll.html), [FT 2](https://www.ft.com/content/34f93b7e-b1a7-40c3-8e6e-a5cac7dbaa9c)). The UAE condemned Iran’s “brazen” attack on tankers as the US launched fresh strikes ([BBC 1](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c8d2vn38dy1o)). Iran has reverted to going dark to move oil tankers through the Strait of Hormuz even before the US reimposed a blockade ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/iran-sneaking-out-tankers-via-hormuz-as-trump-amps-up-threats)). Gulf producers are increasingly relying on alternative routes to keep crude moving, exposing the risks of depending on the strait ([CNBC 2](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/strait-hormuz-uae-saudi-gulf-trump-tolls.html)). Asian oil buyers are looking to US crude as the Iran war intensifies ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/asian-oil-buyers-look-to-us-crude-again-as-iran-war-intensifies)). Traders also boosted wagers on faster Bank of England and European Central Bank interest-rate hikes due to the oil surge ([BBG 3](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/traders-boost-boe-ecb-rate-hike-bets-after-oil-price-surge)).

### 📌 China’s Exports Shatter Records as AI Chip Boom Supercharges Trade

**Fact**
China exported a record `$412 billion` worth of goods in June, blowing past all forecasts as factories gain a competitive edge in high-end industries ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/china-s-exports-imports-soar-faster-than-forecast-amid-ai-rush)). Exports rose at the fastest pace since 2021, lifted by the AI boom and a tariff-rush effect ([CNBC 1](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/china-june-trade-data-exports-imports.html)). In the first six months, China exported `179.44 billion` integrated circuits worth `$177.28 billion`, more than `96%` higher year-on-year ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/tech/article/3360503/global-ai-boom-sees-chinas-chip-exports-nearly-double-first-half-year)). Monthly car exports surged past `1 million` for the first time, and Beijing stressed it is also the world’s second-largest importer ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/37324f66-7167-4083-a28e-6ffc505c4cda)). China’s trade surplus with the European Union climbed to a fresh high, keeping the issue of growing imbalances high on the agenda ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/china-s-trade-surplus-with-eu-hits-record-as-tensions-intensify)). At the same time, China’s June oil imports hit a near 10-year low amid the Iran war ([Reuters](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMirwFBVV95cUxOWkxRblJwR3pkdkRBLW9BQTJwT3htaWpDNXBaTi1LQ1ptQ1VlLW5VellsQ3JTWUluQ2oyOVlGLVBvSmFsM0FmTWh3eE1OV3AtN1RKRUJEQVJaQmdXTXNnNEVyUHFfanFDMjVBRGZqcmVJNWhLLWNNd0IxVGtYSUpJZnUxQmJwSFZBcjU4YVFhYy1ic2RZZXhDZnJhaHhzbG8zTkppelFCcHZoNWJhSGFN)).
