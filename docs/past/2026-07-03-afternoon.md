## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Kospi leaps 6%, won jumps as SK Hynix ADR flows converge with AI sentiment swing

**Fact**
South Korean stocks rebounded after a near `10%` drop in the previous two sessions, highlighting skittish investor sentiment as scrutiny grows over the sustainability of the global AI boom ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-03/south-korean-stocks-jump-5-after-turbulent-week-on-ai-swings)). South Korea’s won strengthened after a person familiar with the matter said the nation’s officials were preparing for currency flows related to SK Hynix Inc.’s offering of American depositary receipts ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-03/korea-said-to-prepare-for-currency-flow-from-sk-hynix-us-listing)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Reversal — the AI‑led selloff that dominated the prior two sessions flips sharply higher with a 6% Kospi bounce driven by a technical rebound and flow anticipation.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk‑premium re‑rating favors **long KRW** and **short USD/KRW** near‑term, while lifting regional tech‑heavy equities.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Sustaining the reversal requires positive SK Hynix ADR pricing and no fresh AI‑capex downgrades over the next 48‑h; Confidence: Medium as the move is fragile amid crowded positioning.

### 📌 Hormuz de‑escalation deepens as tankers return, Citi eyes $60 oil, but Iran warns on routes

**Fact**
The number of ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz has quadrupled over the past week as tankers cautiously return, with the ceasefire between the US and Iran holding ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/55195e9a-09ab-4eb8-878a-516a087a90b7)). Qatar appears to be sending a liquefied natural gas tanker through Hormuz, the first visible passage in about a week, indicating a rapid restart to exports ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-03/qatar-lng-ship-traffic-through-hormuz-resumes-after-brief-pause)). TotalEnergies SE is offering millions of barrels of Iraqi crude that can be promptly delivered to Asia, adding to a market already brimming with supply ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-03/totalenergies-offers-iraqi-oil-to-asian-buyers-as-supply-swells)). Citigroup analysts say oil may slump to `$60` a barrel by Christmas as the Hormuz shock fades, noting both the US and Iran have good reason to maintain the ceasefire ([BBG 3](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-03/citi-says-oil-may-slump-to-60-as-the-hormuz-shock-fades-away), [FT](https://www.ft.com/content/30f0195c-6283-4a70-aab0-929b026c9b70)). Separately, Iran’s military command warned ships against using unapproved routes in the Strait of Hormuz, issuing a threat a day after Qatari mediators hailed “positive progress” in talks ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/3/iran-warns-ships-against-using-unapproved-routes-in-strait-of-hormuz)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration of de‑escalation — physical flows are normalising and analysts slash price targets, yet Iran’s routing warning introduces a fresh, though still rhetorical, risk premium.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Supply‑glut channel favours **short Brent** and **long Asia ex‑JPY equities** on lower energy costs, while keeping a tail‑risk bid in **long gold**.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: De‑escalation remains intact unless any tanker is diverted or attacked; Confidence: High on physical normalisation, Medium given the funeral‑week escalation rhetoric.

### 📌 PBOC trims bond purchases to nine‑month low, signalling wariness over falling yields

**Fact**
China’s central bank trimmed its government bond purchases to the smallest in nine months in June, suggesting policymakers are turning cautious about potential further yield declines or excessive liquidity ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-03/pboc-cuts-bond-purchases-in-sign-of-wariness-over-yield-declines)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New — the PBOC is actively leaning against bond‑market easing, a shift that tightens domestic financial conditions on the margin.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Liquidity‑restraint channel supports **higher CNH** and **flatter onshore yield curves**, while weighing on rate‑sensitive Chinese equities.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Confirmation would come from a further reduction in July bond purchases or an MLF rate hold against market expectations; Confidence: Medium, based on a single month’s data.
