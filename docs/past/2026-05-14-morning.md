> [!abstract] Overview
> Kevin Warsh was confirmed as Fed chair on a historically narrow vote just hours after US wholesale inflation surged to `6%` year-on-year — the fastest since 2022 — and the Treasury sold 30-year bonds at `5%` for the first time since 2007, cementing a structural repricing of long-end yields. The S&P 500 nevertheless closed at a record high as AI enthusiasm overwhelmed the inflation signal, while President Trump landed in Beijing flanked by tech CEOs and his defense secretary for a summit with Xi Jinping that traders expect to yield tariff concessions and Boeing orders. Oil steadied ahead of the talks as a Chinese supertanker that had been attempting a rare Hormuz transit successfully exited the strait and stopped in the Gulf of Oman. The cross-market tension is between a bond market pricing stagflationary reality and risk assets betting on a diplomatic breakthrough — a divergence that the Trump-Xi summit and incoming Fed chair will resolve over the next 48 hours.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Warsh Confirmed as Fed Chair as PPI Blows Past Expectations and 30-Year Auction Hits 5%

**Fact**
The US Senate on Wednesday confirmed Kevin Warsh as the new Federal Reserve chairman to lead a central bank whose independence is under attack and with inflation at multi-year highs ([SCMP 1](https://www.scmp.com/news/world/united-states-canada/article/3353480/warsh-confirmed-federal-reserve-chair-us-central-bank-faces-trump-assault)). Warsh received the fewest votes of any Fed chair in history ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/warsh-trump-congress-senate-analysis.html)). The vote brings to an end one of the most fraught processes of selecting a central bank chief in decades ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/2f1a9453-d062-45a4-9991-9a9552542e25)). Hours before the vote, US wholesale inflation accelerated in April to the fastest pace since 2022 on a war-driven increase in energy prices that is feeding into higher freight transportation costs ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-13/us-producer-prices-rise-most-since-2022-on-higher-energy-costs)). The producer price index jumped `6%` on an annual basis, the biggest increase since 2022, well above the Dow Jones consensus estimate of `0.5%` for the monthly gain ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/ppi-inflation-report-april-2026-.html)). The US then sold `$25` billion of 30-year bonds at a `5%` yield for the first time since 2007, with the auction coming hours after data showing the sharpest rise in producer prices since Russia's invasion of Ukraine ([FT 1](https://www.ft.com/content/11233902-2054-4ed5-b647-26402e7b58bd)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration. Warsh confirmation removes leadership uncertainty but compounds the hawkish pressure from PPI and the `5%` long-bond yield, hardening the stagflationary policy bind beyond the prior state.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Policy and term-premium channels favor **short UST long-end** and **long USD**; the `5%` 30Y yield structurally reprices rate expectations in a way that a single CPI print cannot.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: US retail sales tonight and Warsh's first public remarks will confirm whether the bond sell-off extends further; Confidence: High due to cross-source confirmation of both PPI and auction results.

**Fact**
US President Donald Trump arrived in China flanked by his top aides and American tech leaders on Wednesday night as Beijing rolled out a lavish welcome ceremony at the airport, received by Chinese Vice-President Han Zheng at Beijing Capital International Airport ([SCMP 1](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3353471/trump-arrives-china-tech-titans-and-top-aides-high-stakes-xi-summit)). A Chinese oil tanker has exited the Strait of Hormuz and stopped in the Gulf of Oman, ship tracking data shows, marking a rare successful transit amid the ongoing Iran conflict ([Reuters](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiqAFBVV95cUxPcGdhZmtfdThVeUJHVTBTU1NDNnNxc3VNVVRvcWw0aDFrNmEwT3psOG5VSUhmTWl1QnJhWkJRM0FjeTVRd0JpWTI1VmVEMkZIT21SSVRJUUdtdVVzYzJYNXZkY0UzRWdVc2JfbU02dUR6dVJOVU5ldDVETkQyM3p5X09raW0ybEZvU196M2ZKVmtmRS0xZHVhcExtRGZqSUlicTUwaDdOVUs)). Oil steadied ahead of the Trump-Xi meeting as the Iran war shows no signs of a near-term resolution ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-13/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-may-14)). Saudi Arabia launched strikes against Iran, with Riyadh signaling to Tehran that it was defending itself but not joining the broader US-Israeli assault ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/bbab4ea7-bb54-47fd-98c9-bb66fb52c78f)). OPEC cut its oil demand forecast as the Hormuz shock pushed output lower ([WSJ](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMitAFBVV95cUxNaHExNWhXZE5GSjk3cXd4SVhfVHNtTngyb2d6QU0xWGE3TkIxdEkxWTRUODd5YnBLek5kSHdVSUZyOGhleng0czBkb1d1Qm1YeFhfeXptZ2EwX2NnQWJvcWRmSjNfRGhnekJISjhBeXJ6YzVvWHAyd184dVljWkNJaFY0bHdOMlRyUy1mWGlCX2Vlb0ZFd3ZHWXNWcVIzWUpTWDUta0tVNUJXTG1FX0ZrWElSczY)). The IEA noted that global oil supply declined by a further `1.8` million barrels per day in April and that inventories are being depleted at a "record pace" ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/oil-price-spike-turmoil-iea-iran-war.html)). Depleted US weapons stockpiles from the Iran war risk eroding Trump's bargaining power and may give Beijing extra leverage because of its control of critical mineral supply chains ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/military/article/3353435/how-us-missile-shortages-after-iran-war-could-strengthen-chinas-hand-during-trump-visit)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Confirmation with a de-escalatory signal. The Chinese tanker's successful Hormuz exit provides the first concrete evidence that commercial transit may be possible, but Saudi strikes introduce a new escalation vector.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Physical supply channel — successful tanker transit favors **lower Brent risk premium**, but the Saudi-Iran strike and record IEA inventory draw cap the downside; oil remains bid on any disruption.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Whether additional tankers follow the Chinese vessel's path through Hormuz in the next 48 hours will confirm or invalidate the de-escalation signal. Confidence: Medium — a single transit is suggestive but not conclusive.
