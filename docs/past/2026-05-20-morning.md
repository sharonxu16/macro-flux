> [!abstract] Overview
> The global bond selloff accelerated overnight as the 30-year US Treasury yield surged to a near two-decade high of `5.18%`, dragging Asian equities lower and pushing the Indonesian rupiah to a record low of `17,720` per dollar. The pressure on fixed income was compounded by a fragile Iran ceasefire, with President Trump stating strikes were imminent before another last-minute delay, while fresh sanctions on an Iranian exchange house and shadow fleet tankers kept oil markets on edge and inflation expectations elevated. Simultaneously, President Putin arrived in Beijing to finalize a blockbuster energy deal with Xi Jinping, a move that could reshape the global oil and gas order but also reflects China’s strategic push to settle more trade in renminbi, adding a currency dimension to the geopolitical flux. The single thread connecting the day is a stagflationary impulse that is breaking long-duration bonds, crushing emerging-market currencies, and forcing central banks from Jakarta to Frankfurt into a policy vise.

### 📌 Putin-Xi Summit Targets Blockbuster Energy Deal with Yuan Settlement

**Fact**
Russian President Vladimir Putin arrived in Beijing on Tuesday night for a visit aimed at deepening the two sides’ comprehensive partnership, four days after the Chinese capital hosted US President Donald Trump ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3354142/russian-leader-vladimir-putin-arrives-china-just-days-after-donald-trumps-visit)). The two leaders are set to discuss a “serious, very substantial” oil and gas deal, with talks on the Power of Siberia 2 pipeline coming just days after Trump’s visit ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/7612a618-fbaf-4e3e-8e55-0cf5cc88d132)). Analysts warn a host of factors could complicate a potential agreement, including Beijing’s considerations about avoiding overreliance on a single energy supplier and reducing its dependence on the US dollar by settling transactions in yuan ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3354114/putin-pushing-blockbuster-oil-and-gas-deal-china-will-he-get-it)). The Russian rouble surged to its strongest level against the yuan since February 2023, signaling market anticipation of a major bilateral framework ([Investing](https://www.investing.com/news/forex-news/russian-rouble-hits-strongest-level-against-yuan-since-february-2023-93CH-4698981)). Russia’s goal is simple: shore up its domestic economy by selling more oil and gas to China, but Beijing has other considerations, including yuan-denominated settlements ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3354114/putin-pushing-blockbuster-oil-and-gas-deal-china-will-he-get-it)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New; the juxtaposition of back-to-back Trump and Putin visits creates a strategic triangulation that directly involves CNH internationalization and Asia’s energy security architecture.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: A large yuan-settled energy deal would structurally increase demand for **long CNH** as a trade-invoicing currency, while potentially insulating China from dollar-denominated oil shocks.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: A joint statement announcing a framework for Power of Siberia 2 with yuan-pricing clauses would be a major catalyst; Confidence: Medium as Beijing’s caution on over-reliance on Russia is a known friction point.

### 📌 Asia EM Currency Stress Intensifies as Indonesian Rupiah Sinks to Record Low

**Fact**
The Indonesian rupiah fell to `17,720` per US dollar on Tuesday, cementing its status as one of Asia’s worst-performing currencies this year, while the Jakarta Composite Index tumbled `3.46%` ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/week-asia/economics/article/3354111/indonesias-rupiah-sinks-record-low-prabowo-shrugs-rout-fears)). Indonesia may see its first interest-rate hike in two years as the rupiah’s slide to record lows intensifies pressure for the central bank to mount a stronger defense of the currency ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/bank-indonesia-rate-hike-in-play-as-rupiah-plumbs-new-lows)). President Prabowo Subianto’s attempts to play down the currency’s slide attracted criticism from economists, highlighting a policy disconnect that could further undermine market confidence ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/week-asia/economics/article/3354111/indonesias-rupiah-sinks-record-low-prabowo-shrugs-rout-fears)). The selloff is part of a broader retreat from EM assets driven by the surge in global bond yields and the flight-to-safety bid for the US dollar.

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration; the IDR rout moves from a headline to a potential policy crisis point with the central bank forced to hike amid government complacency.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: The capital-flow and policy channel favors **short IDR and short ASEAN equities**; Bank Indonesia’s rate decision today is a knife-edge event that could trigger a broader EM devaluation round if not hawkish enough.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Bank Indonesia’s rate decision at 3:30 PM HKT on May 20; a hold or insufficient hike would accelerate EM outflows; Confidence: High due to the immediate timeline.
