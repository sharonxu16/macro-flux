### 📌 Tech Rout Deepens as AI Infrastructure Costs and Memory Crunch Hit
**Fact**
Technology stocks closed out a volatile week sharply lower as investors reassessed the sustainability of the AI trade, with concerns over rising semiconductor costs, memory pricing, and capital spending ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-06-27/tech-slump-deepens-video)). SoftBank Group plunged, leading a broad sell‑off in Asian technology stocks amid mounting concerns over the rising cost of AI infrastructure ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/26/global-tech-stocks-ai-infrastructure-costs-selloff-softbank-apple.html)). The memory shortage shaking Apple and Microsoft is described as an “existential crisis” for smaller consumer electronics players ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/27/memory-crunch-shaking-apple-and-microsoft-existential-for-small-guys.html)). Jeremy Grantham said soaring valuations around AI make the U.S. stock market “the most expensive market in American history” ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/26/jeremy-grantham-says-this-is-the-most-expensive-market-in-american-history.html)). SpaceX to join the Nasdaq‑100 in a fast‑tracked process that will drive huge ETF buying demand ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/26/spacex-added-to-nasdaq-100.html)). Tech equity sales are renewing AI debt‑binge worries ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-27/tech-equity-sales-renew-ai-debt-binge-worries-credit-weekly)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — the AI unwind intensifies from valuation concerns to a physical memory‑supply crunch, deepening the pullback in Asia tech exporters.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk‑premium and capital‑flow channel supports **short KRW** and **short TWD** as AI capex fears and memory shortages hit Korea‑Taiwan’s export complex.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: KOSPI holding above recent levels without further cascading retail liquidation; Confidence: High given consistent price action and memory‑shortage headlines.

### 📌 ECB’s Schnabel Flags Upside Inflation Risks Despite Peace Deal
**Fact**
ECB Executive Board member Isabel Schnabel said upside risks to inflation persist even after a peace deal, noting that the output gap could be smaller than assumed and wage growth remains strong ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-27/ecb-s-schnabel-sees-upside-inflation-risks-despite-peace-deal)), and in a speech she examined the question “Is inflation back?” ([ECB](https://www.ecb.europa.eu//press/key/date/2026/html/ecb.sp260627~3807c2766e.en.pdf)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]

> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Rate‑differential channel tilts in favour of **EUR/USD** support, limiting downside in EUR‑denominated bonds.
