> [!abstract] Overview
> The swearing-in of Kevin Warsh as Federal Reserve chair, alongside Governor Waller’s explicit statement that the next rate move is as likely a hike as a cut, triggered a wholesale repricing of the front-end, with bond traders now betting the Fed will raise rates this year. This hawkish pivot collided with a record-low University of Michigan consumer sentiment print driven by cost-of-living angst, hardening the stagflation narrative and sending long-bond yields to the highest in almost two decades. Concurrently, China’s securities regulator is moving to completely shut down the onshore operations of Futu, Tiger Brokers, and Longbridge, ordering existing mainland clients to only sell down positions, a dramatic escalation in capital-account enforcement that may support CNH in the near term but signals further regulatory risk for cross-border platforms. The global macro thread remains the collision of supply-driven energy inflation with central banks that are increasingly unwilling to look through it, tightening financial conditions just as consumer demand buckles.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Warsh Takes Fed Helm as Traders Price December Rate Hike

**Fact**
Kevin Warsh was sworn in as the 17th chair of the Federal Reserve in a White House ceremony on Friday, vowing to lead a “reform-oriented Federal Reserve, learning from past successes and mistakes, both escaping static frameworks” ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-05-22/kevin-warsh-sworn-in-as-new-federal-reserve-chair-video)). President Donald Trump urged Warsh to be “totally independent” but also said he should recognize that “growth does not mean inflation” ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/world/united-states-canada/article/3354589/warsh-takes-us-feds-helm-inflation-dilemma-already-unfolding)). The Federal Open Market Committee unanimously selected Warsh as its chairman ([Fed_Press](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/other20260522a.htm)). Bond traders are now betting the Federal Reserve under Warsh will raise interest rates by the end of this year, with markets pricing a shift to tightening as the Iran war intensifies inflation ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/traders-bet-fed-under-warsh-will-hike-rates-by-end-of-this-year), [FT](https://www.ft.com/content/7a0b2b54-c62b-4498-8b8d-512ef7f2cb5d)). Fed Governor Christopher Waller said the next policy move is as likely to be an interest rate hike as a cut ([BBG 3](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/fed-s-waller-says-next-move-as-likely-to-be-rate-hike-as-cut)). Franklin Templeton Fixed Income CIO Sonal Desai described Warsh as the “most hawkish Fed nominee in 20 years” ([BBG 4](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-05-22/warsh-most-hawkish-fed-nominee-in-20-yrs-desai-video)). Global long bond yields have pushed up to levels last seen during the global financial crisis, with market participants warning the move has room to run ([BBG 5](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-05-22/global-long-bond-yields-hit-highest-in-two-decades-video)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — Waller’s `50/50` hike/cut odds and the market’s full repricing to a 2026 hike represent a sharp hawkish leap beyond prior state expectations of a hold or cuts.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Policy channel — **long USD**, **short USTs** (higher front-end yields), and pressure on **Asia FX** and **EM carry trades** as the dollar strengthens on rate differential widening.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Warsh’s first public speech or press conference, and June FOMC dot plot; Confidence: High.

### 📌 China to Fully Ban Futu, Tiger Brokers, Longbridge Onshore Operations

**Fact**
China’s securities regulator is moving to completely ban the onshore operations of Futu, Tiger Brokers, and Longbridge, with existing mainland clients ordered to only sell down positions and no new purchases permitted ([Caixin](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiYkFVX3lxTE5KamVrRjlMMm5TcFYyZ3pTSG0tVjRYZjlCTGRRQlhqMmgzYmNxYm5HUnM0RHNSTUxkY2VfMnhCMzUwRFlQWE81MWRUdlhBMXU5SFdkb3UxY21pSFY3ZUxma3ZB)). The crackdown targets illegal cross-border securities trading, penalizing brokerages in an effort to close a loophole that allowed retail traders to circumvent capital controls ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/c6b15d3a-a4b6-42c4-8804-84d5efe4b81f)). The China Securities Regulatory Commission said the probe is part of a wider effort to “crack down on ‘illegal’ cross-border securities” activities ([Reuters](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMitAFBVV95cUxNeHpicXlfcW9NVGU3Wnl4c3lnNE9KekJ6TjRwZnFDT3MtOTJ3WWtHM2UzMHJ4TFpjLW5YaEEtYlVNX0V6Qm1laGFNcHdpcXNsY29IZ003SmJrVldSUmRQRG1YZVR3ZGtJdThFRE8yMktGXzJNZVI1U0wzRmFhOVBaQUdOQ3oyalRzYUlnQjlUVC1OUzJ3NDkxRnd4NDBBSVR0TmJUYzc4dXo4NUJRZzZERkROQVU)). Hong Kong’s Securities and Futures Commission is separately raising standards for securities account opening, with additional measures for mainland investor accounts ([Caixin 2](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiYkFVX3lxTFBXbXl1eVZBSncycDI1WkVxSFA5b2hGWE5WR2pkVl9weG1HSzdUWUQxMWdDOG1NX2YtM0l4MTUwbV9sODNhbnVaRjZYTHRGU29Zd3FGUUZTcjgzcGxlMUV6VXpn)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — moves from the prior briefing’s initial crackdown report to an outright ban on existing onshore accounts, forcing full liquidation.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Capital-flow channel — supports **CNH** in the near term by closing outflow valves, but risks a sharp selloff in HK-listed fintech names and broader **Hang Seng Tech** sentiment.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Official penalty announcements with specific forced-liquidation deadlines; Confidence: High given multiple Chinese-language primary sources.

US consumer sentiment fell in May to a record low, with long-term inflation expectations worsening notably due to the Iran war ([BBG 6](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/us-consumer-sentiment-slumps-to-record-low-on-inflation-worries), [Reuters 2](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMivgFBVV95cUxOeEZJY1Y0Q3cxdWZSV0FXaVBpbTRld2Qydl9FZ0NaY2ozZ3RhVlRnb09wWnVqUHNwenFhZVZRcTN2aG9VaHloRmEwUDFxVmN1cDVKTXNPekVKMFdkN0lPcVY0OHJsZ0EyblQwcXBPc0Z6NzRwd0hwSkVWdExoaUUydzYtSWJ1TGtJZTVaVENVRm9CRVJoMHdDcWhIN3JwSkl5a0xPekwycUl2WmxPTDZxMzZVOFlBWllnZ0JCRWdR)). The inflation shock in the euro zone’s biggest economies probably either jumped in May or held at an already robust pace, fueling the case for an interest-rate hike ([BBG 8](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/big-euro-zone-economies-are-enduring-unfolding-inflation-shock)). ECB Governing Council member Madis Muller said there is a “good case” for a June rate hike given the energy surge ([BBG 9](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/ecb-s-muller-sees-good-case-for-june-hike-on-energy-surge)).

> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Inflation channel — favors **long EUR/USD** on ECB hike pricing, but **short global duration** as the coordinated hawkish shift pushes long-end yields higher.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: June ECB meeting and US PCE inflation data; Confidence: High given cross-source confirmation from BBG, WSJ, Reuters, and CNN.
