### 📌 Germany Demands “Plaza Accord” for the Undervalued Yuan, Raising CNH Policy Risk

**Fact**
German Chancellor Friedrich Merz said the Chinese currency is undervalued by `30%`, well above the IMF’s estimate of “about `16%`” ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3357742/germany-hews-eus-tough-china-line-call-plaza-accord-talks-yuan)). Merz pointed to the 1985 Plaza Accord as a model, arguing that China “subsidising overcapacities” along with a “currency that isn’t convertible freely … is not acceptable” ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3357742/germany-hews-eus-tough-china-line-call-plaza-accord-talks-yuan)). The European Commission is crafting tools to counter what it sees as China’s industrial excesses, and Merz’s remarks signal a potential coordinated push to force yuan appreciation.

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New; a direct call by a major EU economy to revalue the yuan introduces a novel policy‑pressure channel on the PBOC.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Trade‑policy channel; could support **CNH strength** if EU rhetoric leads to tariff or FX action, but near‑term uncertainty adds to **CNH volatility**.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Any Chinese official response or G7 communiqué language on yuan; Confidence: Medium, as policy reach is still uncertain.
