> [!abstract] Overview
> Oil prices fell after President Donald Trump stated he called off a military strike on Iran planned for Tuesday, following an appeal by Persian Gulf allies, while the US granted a 30-day waiver extension for Russian oil imports, easing near-term supply fears. Risk sentiment found limited relief as global bonds continued to sell off sharply, with Japan's 30-year yield hitting a record high and the US 10-year yield touching its highest level in a year, fueling concerns that inflation will force the incoming Federal Reserve Chair into a hawkish posture. The combination of a fragile geopolitical detente and a persistent sovereign bond rout is deepening the "stagflationary" anxiety gripping markets, pushing onshore Chinese chip stocks to a sharp rally disconnected from the broader macro gloom due to a blockbuster earnings outlook from memory champion CXMT.

### 📌 Global Bond Rout Intensifies; Japan 30Y Yield Hits Record

**Fact**
Global bond yields rose on Monday as fears of resurgent inflation grip financial markets, with the 10-year Treasury yield touching its highest level in a year and Japan's 30-year yield rising to a record ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/treasury-yields-inflation-bond-rout-oil.html)). An alarming debt-market selloff is intruding on a meeting of G-7 finance ministers in Paris ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-05-18/bond-selloff-latest-g7-finance-ministers-discuss-debt-market-moves)). The Federal Reserve will have to raise interest rates in July to appease 'bond vigilantes,' Yardeni Research's Ed Yardeni said ([CNBC 2](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/the-fed-will-have-to-raise-interest-rates-in-july-to-appease-bond-vigilantes-yardeni-says.html)). A senior Japanese Finance Ministry official sowed doubt over any prospects for the nation to sell US Treasuries to prop up the yen, suggesting the maneuver could be counterproductive ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/japan-official-sows-doubt-over-potential-sales-of-us-treasuries)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration; the bond selloff is widening from a UST-centric story to a global sovereign debt event, with the G-7 meeting and a "vigilante" call for Fed hikes adding a policy-response dimension.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Liquidity and policy channels favor a continued **short UST and Bund** posture; the incoming Fed Chair Warsh faces immediate pressure to address inflation, supporting a **long USD** vs. low-yielders like JPY and EUR.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: The G-7 communiqué and Kevin Warsh's swearing-in on Friday are immediate catalysts that could either validate the vigilante thesis or signal coordinated intervention, determining the next leg for yields; Confidence: High.
