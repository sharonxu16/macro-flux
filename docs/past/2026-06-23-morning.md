> [!abstract] Overview
> The dollar plowed to a fresh one‑year high as hawkish Fed commentary and growing conviction that the FOMC will raise rates in September overwhelmed most other macro currents. A second front opened on currencies as ECB President Lagarde explicitly called for global talks on what she labelled a 15‑16 % yuan undervaluation, injecting China‑specific tension into an already firm‑USD environment. ## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Lagarde urges G‑7 to tackle yuan undervaluation, citing 15‑16 % IMF estimate

**Fact**
European Central Bank President Christine Lagarde on Monday urged global leaders to discuss undervaluation of the Chinese currency as a facet of the imbalances endangering the global economy. China has consistently denied that it manipulates its currency for trade advantage but its surging trade surpluses are one of several macroeconomic mismatches worrying leaders of the Group of Seven (G7) nations who met last week in France ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/money-wealth/article/3357971/european-central-banks-president-lagarde-urges-talks-yuan-undervaluation)). Lagarde cited International Monetary Fund research indicating that the Chinese currency, also known as renminbi, was `15 per cent` to `16 per cent` undervalued when its nominal exchange rate was adjusted for international price differences ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/money-wealth/article/3357971/european-central-banks-president-lagarde-urges-talks-yuan-undervaluation)). Any Group of Seven talks on currency valuations need to include China, according to Lagarde ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-22/lagarde-says-china-should-be-part-of-any-talks-on-fx-imbalances)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New — Lagarde’s direct call to put yuan undervaluation on the G‑7 agenda marks an escalation from prior US tariff‑heavy focus, adding a multilateral currency‑tension layer not present in the previous state.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk‑premium and capital‑flow channels favor **long USD/Asia FX** and **short CNH** if rhetoric hardens into coordinated pressure.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: China’s official response within 24‑48h; Confidence: Medium — Lagarde’s statement is highly specific but requires follow‑through from G‑7 or IMF.

### 📌 Iran deal implementation sours with food‑purchase dispute, oil sanctions waivers

**Fact**
US President Donald Trump said on Monday “I will do what I have to do” if Iran does not stick to its agreement with Washington. Trump added that Iran was supposed to use the money being unfrozen to buy food exclusively from the United States, while Iran’s semi‑official Tasnim news agency cited Iranian central bank governor Abdolnaser Hemmati as saying Tehran was under no obligation to buy agricultural inputs from the US under the current memorandum of understanding ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/world/united-states-canada/article/3357978/i-will-do-what-i-have-do-if-iran-does-not-stick-deal-says-trump)). Trump separately hedged on guaranteeing Iran would not use oil profits to rebuild its military, telling reporters “We’ll see” ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/22/trump-iran-war-oil-profits-rebuild-military.html)). The US has waived oil sanctions, releasing `$12 bn` in frozen Iranian funds, and eased sanctions to allow US refiners a brief window to purchase Iranian crude ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/6/23/iran-war-live-tehran-to-access-billions-in-frozen-funds), [BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-22/easing-of-us-oil-sanctions-opens-brief-window-for-iran-imports)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — peace implementation is advancing with concrete sanction relief, but new friction over agricultural purchases and Trump’s conditional language inject uncertainty absent in the initial deal‑signing phase.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk‑premium and supply channels support **elevated Brent** near‑term; a rapid full normalization would tilt **bearish crude**, but the chaotic rollout keeps two‑sided risk.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: First confirmed commercial Iranian crude cargo under eased sanctions; Confidence: Medium due to contradictory official statements within 24h.
