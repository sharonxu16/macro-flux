> [!abstract] Overview
> Global risk appetite seesawed as a brutal contraction in eurozone business activity collided with an explosive rally in South Korean equities after Samsung’s union averted a strike, while oil rebounded from Wednesday’s rout on intensifying US-Iran diplomatic friction. The S&P Global PMI showed euro-area private-sector activity shrinking at the fastest pace in two-and-a-half years, buckling under the energy-cost surge from the Iran war, which sent the euro lower and European bonds rallying. The KOSPI surged more than `8%` after Samsung Electronics and its union reached a last-minute wage deal, removing a key supply-chain tail risk for the global AI boom and dragging Asian tech stocks higher. The relief from falling energy costs is being overtaken by hard evidence that the war is tipping major economies into contraction, forcing markets to price stagflationary outcomes that complicate the hawkish path signaled by the Federal Reserve.

### 📌 Eurozone Stagflation Shock Intensifies as PMIs Contract at Fastest Pace Since 2023

**Fact**
Business activity in the euro area shrank at the quickest pace in 2 1/2 years, adding to fears that the Iran war and accompanying surge in energy costs are dealing a severe blow to the economy ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/eurozone-pmi)). French business activity shrank at the quickest pace in five and a half years as higher energy prices hit consumers and firms ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/french-business-activity-slumps-at-fastest-pace-since-2020)). German private-sector activity shrank for a second month, raising the risk that Europe’s largest economy is succumbing to the knock-on effects of the Iran war ([BBG 3](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/german-private-sector-activity-contracts-for-second-month-on-war)). British businesses posted the first decline in output in over a year as the Iran shock and a mounting rebellion against Prime Minister Keir Starmer hit activity in the services sector ([BBG 4](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/uk-businesses-shrink-as-starmer-crisis-creates-perfect-storm)). The global economy is showing signs of waning momentum and mounting inflation pressures during its third month of a war-induced energy crunch ([BBG 5](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/war-weighs-on-global-growth-with-inflation-worries-intensifying)).
