### 📌 Iran Hormuz Deadlock Hardens as US Rejects Counter-Offer; Saudi Warns Fuel Stocks at “Critically Low Levels”

**Fact**
Oil pushed higher as US President Donald Trump cast doubt over the ceasefire with Iran after rejecting Tehran’s latest peace offer, prolonging the effective closure of the vital Strait of Hormuz ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-may-12)). Trump said Iran ceasefire is 'on life support' after rejecting Tehran's counterproposal, describing it as “unacceptable” ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/11/trump-iran-war-ceasefire-life-support.html)). Saudi Aramco’s chief executive Amin Nasser said depletion of gasoline and jet fuel inventories is “rapidly accelerating” due to the Hormuz closure, warning fuel stocks are heading for “critically low levels” ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/92e2b3bd-40e5-4a9e-a6c4-c8fa9de83386)). Iran said it will 'never bow' as Trump rejects the counterproposal, prolonging the conflict ([CNBC 2](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/11/iran-war-trump-negotiation-hormuz-nuclear-talks.html)). The US Strategic Petroleum Reserve awarded 53.3 million barrels to companies including oil trader Trafigura Group and US refiner Marathon Petroleum Corp, adding to a wave of oil released to help tame surging prices ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/us-unleashes-another-wave-of-emergency-oil-as-gas-prices-bite)). Investment bank JP Morgan expects oil to remain in the "low $100s" even if the Strait of Hormuz were to reopen next month ([BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckgp4ev4yg4o)). Gold was steady as traders assessed the deadlock in the Strait of Hormuz that has sent oil prices higher and added further fuel to inflation concerns ([BBG 3](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/gold-steady-as-traders-track-hormuz-stalemate-inflation-risks)).

> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Energy-shock channel tilts risks toward **higher Brent**, **wider Asia FX carry** via KRW/TWD, and **bullish gold** on risk-off hedging.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Tuesday's US CPI and Thursday's WASDE report will test whether stagflation pricing extends; Confidence: High.
