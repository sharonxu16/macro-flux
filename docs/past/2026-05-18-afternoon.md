> [!abstract] Overview
> The global bond rout deepened through the Asian afternoon as Japan’s prime minister abruptly called for an extra budget, catapulting the 10‑year JGB yield to a 30‑year high and dragging the 30‑year Treasury yield back toward `5%`. Oil held firm near `$110` after the IEA warned commercial inventories are shrinking at an accelerated pace. In a jarring turn, China’s April activity data missed comprehensively — retail sales growth slumped to `0.2%`, industrial output cooled to `4.1%`, and investment re‑entered contraction — reigniting stimulus talk and sending the Hang Seng Index below its 250‑day moving average. Korean equities staged a V‑shaped rebound from correction territory after a court ruling defused Samsung strike fears, offering brief respite to KRW, while the Indonesian rupiah sank to a record low. The cross‑market thread remains the violent clash between sticky energy‑driven inflation and crumbling global growth, a combination that gives sovereign bonds no bid and forces central banks into an accelerating policy vacuum.
