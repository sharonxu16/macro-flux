> [!abstract] Overview
> The dollar reasserted broad strength, with traders turning the most bullish since 2015 and hedge funds piling into the biggest short‑yen position in nearly two decades as markets priced a more hawkish Fed path despite the soft June payrolls. Oil hovered near pre‑Iran‑war levels, pinned by a deepening supply glut from Saudi discounts and record UAE output, though an overnight tanker strike in the Strait of Hormuz kept a risk floor under crude. China expanded its military footprint with a submarine‑launched ballistic‑missile test and a dual‑use maritime survey east of Taiwan, amplifying regional security premia. JPMorgan Asset trimmed its long‑yuan bets, wagering that resilient US rates and Taiwan‑central‑bank intervention favour a won rebound over continued CNH outperformance. The session’s cross‑market thread is a return of the “higher‑for‑longer” dollar narrative, pressuring gold, Asia FX and the yen, while a swelling oil surplus moderates inflation‑hedge demand.
