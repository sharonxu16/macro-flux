> [!abstract] Overview
> Asian equities pushed higher with AI‑driven chip demand lifting Korea’s early exports nearly `50%` and SK Hynix overtaking Samsung as the market‑cap leader, while most Asia FX softened against a firm dollar. The day’s dominant macro driver was a further acceleration of US‑Iran peace implementation — two South Korean‑operated vessels transited Hormuz and Iranian crude flows surged — but the relief was offset by a sharp escalation in the US‑China trade war after Beijing imposed export controls on ten American firms in retaliation for the Pentagon blacklist. Sterling slumped to its 2026 low as Prime Minister Starmer said he would resign, adding a fresh political risk premium to UK assets. Across markets, fragile peace momentum and intensifying strategic‑trade friction kept risk premiums elevated even as supply chains showed tentative healing.
