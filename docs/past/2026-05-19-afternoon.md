> [!abstract] Overview
> The global bond rout deepened intraday as Citi targeted a 30-year US Treasury yield of 5.5% and a BofA survey flagged "secondary inflation" as the top tail risk, sending gold to a 1.5-month low while the dollar edged higher. Singapore stocks reclaimed record highs on haven demand, Indonesian markets tumbled amid export-control speculation, and Asian currencies from India to Indonesia slid to historic lows, crystallizing the stagflationary vise tightening around emerging assets. The cross-market thread is the deepening tension between intensifying bond-market stress and fragile geopolitical relief, leaving risk premiums elevated even as headline supply fears ease.
