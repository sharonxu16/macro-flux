> [!abstract] Overview
> A sharp de-escalation in the Iran conflict sent Brent crude plunging nearly 6% after two Chinese supertankers transited the Strait of Hormuz and President Trump said the US was in the "final stages" of talks, triggering a broad relief rally in Treasuries and EM currencies. The risk-on glow collided with an unexpectedly hawkish FOMC minutes release that showed many officials wanted to drop the rate‑cut signal and open the door to hikes if inflation stays sticky. In China, a former Chongqing mayor’s call for a `15‑20%` gradual yuan appreciation to shrink the record trade surplus added a new dimension to FX policy, while Washington’s commitment to a tariff ceiling from last year’s truce was challenged by a planned Trump‑Taiwan call and a `$14bn` arms package for Taipei. Falling energy costs are easing global inflation fears, but the hawkish Fed and resurfacing cross‑strait risk keep the macro repricing from turning into a one‑way trade.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Oil Plunges on Hormuz Transit Breakthrough, US‑Iran Talks in Final Stages

**Fact**
Brent crude falls to about `$105` a barrel after two China‑bound supertankers crossed the Strait of Hormuz, raising hopes of a resumption of energy flows ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/110b9bb3-ec4f-460d-bfe6-4ffae9a2e945)). Oil inched higher after plummeting on Wednesday, as President Donald Trump said the US is in the “final stages” with Iran ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-may-21)). Treasuries surged on investor optimism that the US and Iran were working toward a deal that could ease a rise in global energy prices that has fanned fears around inflation ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/treasuries-rally-as-trump-cites-final-stages-of-us-iran-talks)). Emerging‑market currencies rallied Wednesday, paring losses the day before, as oil prices tumbled on optimism over the outlook for the conflict in the Middle East ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/emerging-stocks-at-two-week-low-currencies-stay-under-pressure)). Asian equities followed Wall Street higher as optimism over US‑Iran talks eased concerns about Middle East tensions, sending crude oil and bond yields lower ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/asian-stocks-to-rise-as-nvidia-whipsaws-on-results-markets-wrap)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Reversal of the Iran escalation narrative as tankers transit and the US signals negotiations are in the final stage, collapsing the geopolitical risk premium.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk‑premium channel favours **long USTs**, **long EM FX (KRW, INR)**, and **short Brent**; easing energy prices lower near‑term inflation expectations and reduce the urgency of Fed tightening.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Further tanker passages without attack; Confidence: High given cross‑source confirmation, but vulnerable to a single Iranian incident.

Minutes of the Federal Open Market Committee’s April 28‑29 meeting showed more policymakers open to a rate hike if inflation stays elevated ([Reuters](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiogFBVV95cUxPbzRaNVFyVGlDLTQ4X0NRZF9wSGRNRmstd3JyX2NwanZFUEYwVjVXbndQN29MeDB5ZVpEaUh6WkxSaHBLNU1OQmtGNkRqV0xLdkJXWU80a1o5YWM1UTV6ZG1zNUJwOUY5a1lmbU9KRGdsYUszeFRYcGY3c1d1UWQ4czQ1SUYweUNOd01sLW1GbXNTeWlkQjdadWtSRmx0dXJQenc)). ### 📌 Ex‑Official Urges 15‑20% Yuan Appreciation as China’s Record Surplus Strains Trade

**Fact**
China needs a coordinated policy package of currency appreciation, tariff cuts and elevated labour benefits to rebalance its massive trade surplus, former Chongqing mayor Huang Qifan said on Tuesday; he proposed a gradual appreciation of the yuan by `15` to `20` per cent over the next decade, but stressed that the appreciation must be steady and incremental ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3354248/china-poised-record-trade-surplus-former-chongqing-mayor-calls-action)). Huang described China’s 2025 record‑breaking `$1.2` trillion merchandise trade surplus as “shocking” and warned “We could see another `$1.2` trillion surplus this year” ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3354248/china-poised-record-trade-surplus-former-chongqing-mayor-calls-action)). China’s goods trade surplus stood at `US$347.7` billion in the first four months of this year ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3354248/china-poised-record-trade-surplus-former-chongqing-mayor-calls-action)). Mounting Exporter Strain Risks Complicating China’s Yuan Policy ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/mounting-exporter-strain-risks-complicating-china-s-yuan-policy)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New narrative from a prominent former official signals internal pressure for a stronger yuan as the surplus balloons, adding a new dimension to the FX policy debate.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Capital‑flow channel favours **long CNH** over the medium term if the debate shifts toward appreciation, but near‑term exporter pushback and PBOC management will cap upside.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Any PBOC reference to the surplus or yuan in official statements; Confidence: Low because the proposal is unofficial and faces exporter opposition.
