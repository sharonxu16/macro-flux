### 📌 Warsh’s Fed nomination spurs higher‑for‑longer repricing

**Fact**
Kevin Warsh’s appointment to lead the Fed has prompted a sharp flattening of the Treasury curve as investors bet on prolonged high US interest rates ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-25/treasury-curve-flashes-higher-for-longer-warning-under-warsh)). Markets see Warsh as a hawkish successor who will keep policy tight to cement the inflation fight, lifting the dollar broadly and putting emerging‑market assets under fresh pressure.

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New; this is the first market pricing signal linking the Warsh nomination to an extended tightening cycle, distinct from prior Iran‑driven rate dynamics.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Policy channel supports **long USD** and **long UST yields**, with negative spillovers for **high‑beta Asia FX** and EM credit.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Upcoming FOMC minutes or Warsh’s confirmation hearing remarks; Confidence: High, given cross‑market confirmation.
