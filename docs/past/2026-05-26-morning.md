> [!abstract] Overview
> The sprint toward a US-Iran interim deal accelerated overnight as tankers transited the Strait of Hormuz and negotiators reached an understanding on unfreezing Iranian assets, driving a `>7%` intraday plunge in Brent crude, wiping out all Iran-war losses on Europe’s Stoxx 600, and pushing the dollar broadly lower. Sentiment was jolted by reports of fresh US strikes near Bandar Abbas and explosions inside Iran, which pared the equity rally and lifted oil off session lows, underscoring that kinetic action persists even as diplomacy intensifies. The Treasury curve is flashing a higher-for-longer warning under incoming Fed Chair Warsh, adding a hawkish rates overlay to what would otherwise be a pure risk-on peace-trade moment. The dominant cross-market thread is a tug-of-war between accelerating peace optionality and the residual risk premium from ongoing strikes — an environment that favors fading oil rallies and buying Asia FX dips until a formal ceasefire is signed.
