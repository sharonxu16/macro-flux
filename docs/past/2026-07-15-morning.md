> [!abstract] Overview
> A sharp downside US CPI surprise slashed Federal Reserve tightening odds, sending the dollar and Treasury yields lower and equities sharply higher, while the Middle East conflict intensified with a new US naval blockade, Brent crude spiking to `$87/bbl`, and a commercial tanker struck off Oman. ### 📌 US CPI delivers downside shock — July Fed hike evaporates

**Fact**
US consumer inflation fell more than expected to `3.5%` in June as petrol prices tumbled, with the headline rate down from `4.0%` in May and well below the `3.7%` consensus; core CPI also disappointed to the downside ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/cb3b2887-d805-402b-9c85-55e3b44d867b)). The dollar slipped and Treasury yields fell as the cooling inflation data eased rate‑hike bets ([WSJ](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMilgFBVV95cUxQMjlRbU8zMDUtMHlkclNua0w5azJvNDV3WVEyWkpQSWJaVGFYZlV4WjAzX20yUEd0M1ZjTWs5VEphSUtDTUJZMWNUYVlkWllEWlFhZkxreVlFU3FJcUJRU3VDczVYUmg5Q0h5cXBoV0x3cWdFLWV0M3ZsaFctMS1ycHNJWlRkNGtmNERKbWJYS1RCS2JUcFE), [Reuters](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMinAFBVV95cUxPYW9Yb2N3aXZuNlJEWEtmbTZfd1Q3ZGtGWnFGRkdHSjdNelZQd0dWUktpMUhFdVM3UWxUNms0TC1YUFFZTU5HZkRxWmlpdk45QnlkWEItbmlRb19lQzl1OVV2Ml9UMkFJc2xoVUFtQU9jQk5lMmRhM2liUGJzNllvNV9VM1Q1c1R0NEpnZ05TXy1MWGJWcWN3Ukh5OUc)). **Fact**
Brussels is looking at “case‑by‑case” tariffs and quotas after China’s trade surplus with the EU expanded by almost a quarter in the first half of the year ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3360556/eu-signals-emergency-import-curbs-after-dramatic-surge-chinese-trade-gap)). The two developments, along with today’s Chinese GDP release, are piling pressure on Beijing’s export‑driven model.
