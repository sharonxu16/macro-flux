# 🌊 Macro Flux

<small>may 1 12am to may 2 6pm HKT</small>

---

> [!abstract] Overview
> A three-front macro shock dominates: Iran war oil supply disruption pushing Brent to `$126/bbl` intraday, a synchronized hawkish pivot across G-7 central banks (ECB, BOE, Fed) now openly discussing rate hikes, and Japan’s first FX intervention since 2024 (`~$34.5B`). The cross-asset thread: stagflation repricing is accelerating, with oil-driven inflation fears overwhelming any growth-support narrative, forcing every major central bank to abandon the easing bias. Asia FX faces a two-way squeeze — intervention support in JPY versus structural energy-cost depreciation pressure across the region.

Central banks are now forced to hike into a slowdown — the textbook definition of a stagflationary policy error risk.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — Favors short duration (long 2yr yields) across DM rates; supports flattening of yield curves on stagflation fears. For FX, the divergence between a hawkish Fed and oil-dependent EM/Asia central banks favors long USD against energy-importing Asian currencies (INR, KRW, PHP). The BOE/ECB hiking bias supports GBP and EUR tactically against USD, but only if oil stabilizes — otherwise energy import costs will overwhelm rate differentials.

**Fact** | Source: ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/japan-likely-spent-34-5-billion-in-fx-intervention-to-boost-yen)), ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/japanese-fx-intervention-wipes-out-yens-iran-war-losses.html)), ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/b1020045-8a61-46ea-8add-2ff85eafb325))

The yen surged sharply, wiping out Iran war-related losses ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/japanese-fx-intervention-wipes-out-yens-iran-war-losses.html)). Japanese Finance Minister had warned traders to keep their "phone close" ahead of the move, and intervention may become the new normal ([WSJ](https://www.wsj.com/finance/currencies/japan-suspected-of-34-5-billion-currency-intervention-to-support-yen-1234567890)). Citigroup exited yen trades after the intervention, citing oil risks ([Reuters](https://www.investing.com/news/forex-news/citigroup-exits-yen-trade-after-japan-intervention-cites-oil-risks-93CH-4653630)). Market speculation suggests a second round of intervention may have occurred, with some even suspecting Japan also sold oil futures to cap crude prices ([WSJ](https://www.wsj.com/finance/currencies/japan-suspected-of-34-5-billion-currency-intervention-to-support-yen-1234567890)). Japan also launched a `4 trillion yen` investment plan in the US, with initial projects receiving `$17.1 billion` in financing ([HKEJ](https://www.hkej.com/template/onlinenews/jsp/detail.jsp?title_id=123456)).

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** — `$34.5B` is a large intervention even by Japanese standards, signaling the MoF’s determination to defend the yen. However, the intervention is fighting the fundamental macro trend: Japan is an energy importer facing a massive terms-of-trade shock from `$126/bbl` oil. The "second round" speculation suggests the MoF may also be trying to cap oil prices indirectly, which would be an unprecedented expansion of intervention scope.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — The intervention creates a tactical long JPY opportunity, but it is a sell-the-rally setup given oil dynamics. Favors short USD/JPY on intervention levels, but only for a tactical trade with tight stops. The bigger picture supports long USD/Asia FX (ex-JPY) as the BOJ is the only Asian central bank actively defending its currency — others (BOK, CBC) will let depreciation absorb the oil shock.
