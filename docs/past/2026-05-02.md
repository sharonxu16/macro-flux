> [!abstract] Overview
> The Iran war’s oil shock is now forcing a synchronized global central bank pivot toward rate hikes, with the ECB, BOE, and Fed all signaling a shift away from cuts. The PBOC remains the notable holdout, but faces a worsening external squeeze as the US sanctions a China-based oil terminal operator and the Strait of Hormuz blockade pushes Brent crude to a volatile `$126/bbl`. Japan intervened with an estimated `$34.5B` to support the yen, while US equities hit new records on an AI-driven tech rally, creating a sharp divergence between risk assets and the stagflationary macro reality. The single thread connecting everything: the energy war is transmuting into a global tightening cycle, with Asia caught between capital flight and import cost inflation.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Global Central Banks Pivot: The Era of Rate Cuts Is Over — Hikes Now on the Table

**Fact** | Source: ([FT](URL)), ([BBG](URL)), ([CNBC](URL)), ([WSJ](URL)), ([CNN](URL))
The post-Iran war oil shock has triggered a synchronized hawkish repricing across G-7 central banks. The **ECB** is now leaning toward a June rate hike, with President Lagarde signaling the move and Bundesbank President Nagel saying the ECB "should raise rates in June unless the outlook improves markedly" ([BBG](URL)). ECB’s Rehn warned that "fast ECB action needed if second-round inflation seen" ([BBG](URL)), and Makhlouf said "upside risks to inflation have intensified" ([BBG](URL)). WallstreetCN reported: 欧央行行长拉加德暗示6月或考虑加息，交易员预计欧洲年内三次25基点加息 ([WallstreetCN](URL)). Eurozone April inflation surged to `3%`, well above the ECB’s `2%` target, adding pressure ([WallstreetCN](URL), [BBG](URL)). The **Bank of England** is also facing a hawkish turn: Governor Bailey said the central bank cannot shield households from the Iran energy crisis, and the BOE’s chief economist dissented, arguing for "rates to rise more promptly" ([BBG](URL), [FT](URL)). The **Fed** is now debating the conditions for a rate hike, not a cut. The Wall Street Journal reported the shift: "After Months of Debating Rate Cuts, Fed Shifts Toward Mapping Out Hikes" ([WSJ](URL)). The "new Fed communications" blog noted: 从“降不降息”到“加息条件”，美联储内部讨论重心转向 ([WallstreetCN](URL)). Three FOMC dissenters — Kashkari, Hammack, and Goolsbee — explicitly said they disagreed with the statement's "next move is a cut" language because rising uncertainty from the war changed the rate outlook ([BBG](URL), [CNBC](URL), [WallstreetCN](URL)). The incoming Fed chair, Kevin Warsh, faces the risk of dissenting at his first FOMC vote as he confronts a more hawkish committee ([BBG](URL), [CNBC](URL)). CNN reported: "The Fed subtly signaled that only rate cuts are on the table. Some Fed officials are crying foul" ([CNN](URL)). G-7 officials are now penciling in possible rate hikes as soon as June ([BBG](URL)).

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** — This is a regime change. The global monetary policy cycle has flipped from easing to tightening in under 60 days, driven entirely by an energy supply shock. The ECB and BOE are leading the hawkish repricing because their inflation is more directly exposed to oil and gas prices. The Fed is slower to pivot but the internal debate has already shifted — the FOMC statement's asymmetric bias (only cuts) is now contested. The risk is that central banks hike into a growth slowdown, creating a stagflationary policy error.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — Favors long USD vs EUR and GBP on rate differentials. Supports short duration in European and UK government bonds. The repricing of Fed hike risk supports flattening of the US yield curve (2s10s). For Asia, the hawkish G-7 backdrop creates headwinds for EM FX and local bonds — the carry trade is reversing.

### 📌 Japan Intervenes: `$34.5B` Yen Support as Oil Shock Breaks Currency

**Fact** | Source: ([BBG](URL)), ([FT](URL)), ([CNBC](URL)), ([Investing](URL)), ([WallstreetCN](URL))
Japan conducted its first currency intervention since July 2024, spending an estimated `$34.5B` (5.4万亿日元) to support the yen after USD/JPY breached `160` ([BBG](URL), [Investing](URL), [WallstreetCN](URL)). The intervention caused a sharp spike in the yen before it partially retraced. Finance Minister earlier warned traders to keep their "phones close" during Golden Week ([WallstreetCN](URL)). Citi exited its yen trade after the intervention, citing oil price risks ([Investing](URL)). The FT reported Japan put the market on alert for "golden week action" ([FT](URL)). WallstreetCN also reported speculation of a "second intervention" and even suggested Japan may have coordinated with oil market moves: 赶在假期前“第二次干预汇市”？市场甚至猜测“日本不止拉高日元，还砸了油价” ([WallstreetCN](URL)). The intervention came as the Iran war-driven oil surge pushed the yen to multi-decade lows, with the BOJ facing a dilemma: raising rates to support the currency would slow growth, while letting the yen fall worsens import inflation.

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** — Japan’s intervention is a defensive move, not a structural fix. The `$34.5B` is large but represents only ~5% of Japan’s FX reserves — the BOJ cannot fight the oil shock with reserves alone. The real solution is either a BOJ rate hike (which would crush the JGB market) or an end to the Iran war. Intervention buys time, not a trend reversal.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — Supports a tactical short USD/JPY position near `160` but only for a scalp — structural yen weakness persists as long as the energy shock continues. Favors long JPY vol. The intervention also creates a floor for USD/JPY at `155` in the near term.

### 📌 Oil Shock Deepens: Brent Hits `$126/bbl` as Hormuz Blockade Continues

**Fact** | Source: ([FT](URL)), ([BBC](URL)), ([CNBC](URL)), ([BBG](URL)), ([CNN](URL)), ([WSJ](URL))
Brent crude surged to `$126/bbl` — the highest since 2022 — before volatile trading pulled it back, after a report that the US military is set to brief President Trump on new plans for potential action in the Iran war ([BBC](URL), [CNBC](URL)). The Strait of Hormuz blockade continues to disrupt global oil flows. Exxon Mobil CEO warned "the market hasn’t seen the full impact" of the Iran war on oil prices ([CNBC](URL)). Barclays lifted its 2026 Brent forecast to `$100/bbl` on prolonged Hormuz disruption ([Reuters](URL)). The FT reported oil markets are "one month from crunch point as global reserves dwindle," with traders warning of a "further jump in prices and huge pain" as economies cut consumption ([FT](URL)). Saudi Arabia is set for an oil windfall after the Hormuz blockade boosts prices, while other Gulf producers suffer ([BBG](URL)). US gasoline prices are rising faster than in any other G7 country ([FT](URL)). Kalshi prediction market traders see US oil prices exceeding the `$125/bbl` wartime high ([CNBC](URL)). Trump said the US Navy is acting "like pirates" to carry out the naval blockade of Iranian ports ([Reuters](URL)). The US Treasury warned shippers not to pay Hormuz tolls, even in the form of charity ([Reuters](URL)).

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** — The oil market is pricing a supply disruption that has no clear end date. The blockade of Hormuz is unprecedented in duration and scale. The risk is a self-reinforcing cycle: higher oil → higher inflation → tighter monetary policy → slower growth → demand destruction, but demand destruction takes months to materialize. In the interim, the supply shock dominates.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — Favors long crude oil and energy equities. Supports long USD vs oil-importing Asian FX (KRW, TWD, INR, PHP). The oil shock also supports a long position in commodity currencies (CAD, NOK, AUD) vs EUR and JPY. The widening US gasoline price gap vs Europe tilts risks toward a consumer slowdown in the US.

### 📌 US Sanctions China-Based Oil Terminal Operator Over Iran Trade

**Fact** | Source: ([SCMP](URL)), ([WSJ](URL))
The US Treasury sanctioned Qingdao Haiye Oil Terminal Co., Ltd and Chinese national Li Xinchun for importing "tens of millions of barrels of sanctioned Iranian crude oil" ([SCMP](URL), [WSJ](URL)). The WSJ separately reported on China’s "teapot" refiners providing a financial lifeline to Iran ([WSJ](URL)). China’s UN envoy blasted US "bullying" as sanctions widened before Trump-Xi talks ([SCMP](URL)). The sanctions come as the US escalates its "maximum pressure" campaign on Iran, targeting the financial infrastructure that enables Iranian oil exports.

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** — This is a direct escalation targeting the China-Iran oil trade. The designation of a specific Chinese terminal operator signals Washington is moving from general warnings to enforcement. This creates a compliance risk for Chinese banks and refiners, potentially reducing Chinese crude imports from Iran and tightening the global oil market further.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — Supports a short CNH/CNY position as the geopolitical risk premium rises. The risk of secondary sanctions on Chinese banks could disrupt trade financing and capital flows. Favors long oil vol.

### 📌 US Equities Hit Records on AI Rally, Diverging From Macro Reality

**Fact** | Source: ([Yahoo Finance](URL)), ([Reuters](URL)), ([FT](URL))
The S&P 500 and Nasdaq posted record highs, with the Nasdaq topping `25,000` for the first time, driven by a tech/AI rally on strong earnings from Apple, Sandisk, and other AI-linked names ([Yahoo Finance](URL), [Reuters](URL)). Apple reported 17% revenue growth and issued strong guidance ([CNBC](URL), [WallstreetCN](URL)). Sandisk reported record highs on AI storage demand ([Yahoo Finance](URL), [WallstreetCN](URL)). The FT asked: "Why markets are surging in spite of war," noting that even amid a supply shock, tech stocks continue to drive indices higher ([FT](URL)). Spirit Airlines shut down after a failed White House bailout, becoming the first Iran war casualty in the airline industry ([BBG](URL), [FT](URL), [Reuters](URL)).

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** — The divergence between the AI-driven equity rally and the stagflationary macro backdrop is unsustainable. Tech stocks are pricing a world where AI capex drives productivity gains that offset the oil shock. This narrative works as long as the oil shock doesn't trigger a recession that destroys earnings. The Spirit Airlines bankruptcy is a canary — energy-intensive sectors are already breaking.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — The AI/tech rally is a crowded trade. Favors hedging long equity positions with oil futures or energy sector shorts. The divergence between tech and energy/consumer sectors supports a long-short strategy: long AI-exposed names, short consumer discretionary and airlines.
