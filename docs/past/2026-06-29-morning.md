> [!abstract] Overview
> The U.S.-Iran ceasefire unraveled over the weekend as mutual strikes across the Gulf and Iranian drone attacks on American bases in Kuwait and Bahrain pushed Hormuz back to the brink, reigniting the full oil supply disruption premium. AI and semiconductor names extended their rout after SoftBank cratered on a delayed OpenAI IPO and the BIS warned that trillion-dollar AI capex risks a prolonged crash, dragging Asian tech exporters lower. China pushed back on EU trade freeze threats while VW’s deepening job cuts fuelled calls for European trade barriers, and the U.S. banned imports of more Chinese technology goods, adding a fresh trade layer to the risk-off tone. Beijing reported a `18.8%` y/y surge in Jan-May industrial profits, but government advisers warned of a widening two-speed divergence between AI-linked sectors and weak consumer spending, leaving Chinese asset sentiment fragile ahead of Tuesday’s PMI. Across markets, the twin blows of Mid-East escalation and an accelerating AI unwind are driving a simultaneous flight to oil and safe-haven currencies while punishing growth-sensitive and export-linked equities.
