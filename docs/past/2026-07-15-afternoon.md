## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 China Q2 GDP disappoints sharply — growth fears return

**Fact**
China’s economy grew at one of the lowest rates in decades, expanding `4.3%` year on year in the second quarter, down from `4.5%` in the first quarter and below a `4.5%` forecast ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/5b12e491-dcd0-4e0c-a464-96ec37b737ab)). Industrial production rose `3.8%` in June, missing a `4.3%` estimate; retail sales increased `2.1%`, below a `3%` prediction; and fixed asset investment for the first half climbed `2.7%`, short of a `3.2%` consensus ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/5b12e491-dcd0-4e0c-a464-96ec37b737ab)). The data marked the slowest quarterly expansion since 2022, with investment slumping and reigniting calls for policy easing ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/15/china-gdp-retail-sales-investment-june-.html)). Nominal GDP growth outstripped real growth for the first time in 13 quarters as deflationary pressure persisted ([Caixin](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiYkFVX3lxTE5weTF2WUwycmMycjdUa05fejFtQ0ZVT1hoSXJuZGZNcUhoM0FhcHUtMi05ZHlJVVBjWVVyN3AzVTNpNXhtcnVjTFcyNXdxRDZ6UUxLOGYxYWNhSHFfakNUVzJR)). China missed its growth target for the first time since the pandemic as the Iran‑driven global trade disruption compounded domestic weakness ([CNN](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiggFBVV95cUxPTmt2N1R1dDNhR3NLaC04THBCZGd3Q1JqRGt0VEQ2WHpTZ3hzZ0JwcU1RR2FhLVVXSGNpYk5WYmw4MGd4TlpJU3FQT1JZQUhUbmQxcXBfWll4ajZ4bWRxaTJ0MVVMSkFQYXFGcjFBVk9HejQ5NXlJQjVZTTBwQXlJY0hB)). The slowdown was underscored by a steep `40%` y/y plunge in China’s crude oil imports in June, the lowest in nearly a decade, reflecting both price sensitivity and the Hormuz blockade’s impact on physical supply ([Caixin](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiZEFVX3lxTE5laGhIVG1yZG5MOS1QUC1XNlpkaVJWV3FIb0s0b2ltTVJYQ0J0TDQtOThoOW9YbVZDbDI0X3VSRmt2WU81TngwOXRsTjZGOGEyTDZ5T0hIVzJaNmF0a2hzLWxBMVo)).

**Fact**
The United States launched another wave of strikes on Iranian missile sites and defense systems and reimposed a naval blockade of Iranian ports, while Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps threatened to close “all other export corridors that benefit the US and its allies” ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/15/us-attacks-iran-as-irgc-claims-strikes-on-us-military-sites-in-gulf)).
