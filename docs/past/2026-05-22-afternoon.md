### 📌 US Navy Pauses `$14 Billion` Taiwan Arms Sale, Beijing and Taipei React

**Fact**
Acting US Navy Secretary Hung Cao told a congressional hearing that “right now we’re doing a pause in order to make sure we have the munitions” for the war with Iran, signalling a hold on the `$14 billion` weapons package ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3354539/us-navy-signals-pause-taiwan-arms-sale-drawing-swift-reaction-beijing)). Beijing’s foreign ministry spokesman Guo Jiakun said on Friday: “China’s firm opposition to US arms sales to China’s Taiwan region is consistent, clear and unwavering” ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3354539/us-navy-signals-pause-taiwan-arms-sale-drawing-swift-reaction-beijing)). The pause came as President Donald Trump gave mixed signals on the sale, according to Al Jazeera ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/5/22/us-pausing-14bn-arms-sale-to-taiwan-due-to-iran-war-navy-chief-says)).

### 📌 ECB Set for June Hike as Recession Fears Deepen Growth Divergence

**Fact**
An ECB rate increase is likely in June to signal a continued focus on returning inflation to `2%`, according to ECB policymaker Demarco ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/ecb-hike-likely-in-june-to-signal-commitment-to-2-demarco-says)). President Christine Lagarde said long‑term inflation expectations remain on target ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/ecb-chief-says-long-term-inflation-expectations-remain-on-target)). EU trade commissioner Valdis Dombrovskis separately stated he sees an ECB rate response to the inflation shock hitting the continent ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/eu-s-dombrovskis-sees-ecb-rate-response-to-inflation-shock)). French business confidence remained weak in May, languishing below its long‑term average and adding to evidence that war fallout is weighing on demand in the eurozone’s second‑largest economy ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/french-business-confidence-languishes-below-long-term-average)). German business outlook improved for the first time since fighting broke out, but the overall picture is one of divergence, with Barclays warning that “growth divergence and ECB concerns” will drive further euro weakness ([Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/forex-news/barclays-sees-euro-weakness-on-growth-divergence-and-ecb-concerns-93CH-4705911)).

**Fact**
China’s securities regulator plans to penalise Futu Holdings, Tiger Brokers and Longbridge Securities for operating on the mainland without a licence, marking a sharp escalation in a years‑long campaign against illicit cross‑border brokerages ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/china-to-penalize-tiger-futu-in-cross-border-broker-crackdown)).
