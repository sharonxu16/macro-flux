> [!abstract] Overview
> Oil reversed lower after touching session highs near `$100` on US military strikes in Iran, as a de-escalation narrative reasserted itself with the first LNG tanker transit through Hormuz and reports of a US-Iran asset-freeze understanding, dragging Brent back near `$96`. That retreat pulled the dollar off its peaks against Asian FX, though the Warsh-driven higher-for-longer rates narrative kept the greenback broadly bid, with Treasuries rallying and the curve flattening further. The Korean won remained vulnerable as BofA warned that strong trade surpluses offer little cushion in a risk-off world, while Taiwan equities shrugged off geopolitical noise to overtake India as the world's fifth-largest stock market. The session's through-line was the grind higher in global bond yields—ECB's Schnabel demanding a June hike regardless of peace, gold falling as war-fired inflation fuels rate bets—leaving risk assets caught between a fragile peace premium that softens oil and a tightening impulse that lifts the dollar and weighs on Asia FX.
