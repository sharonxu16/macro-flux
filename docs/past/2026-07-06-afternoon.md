### 📌 China fires SLBM into Pacific, alarming neighbors and rattling regional security

**Fact**
A nuclear-powered People’s Liberation Army submarine fired a test missile into the Pacific on Monday, according to Chinese state media; the dummy warhead was fired at 12.01pm and “precisely landed in the designated waters,” sending a second-strike signal ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/military/article/3359541/nuclear-powered-pla-submarine-fires-test-missile-western-pacific)). ### 📌 Oil steadies at pre-war levels as Hormuz disruptions persist, Pakistan buys LNG

**Fact**
Oil prices steadied around levels last seen before the Iran conflict, as the OPEC+ output hike and partially recovering Hormuz flows kept a lid on crude even as the glut deepened ([Reuters](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiogFBVV95cUxPZkI2QThocHlWRUVKSHgtb2N1WmlUX2hDQ3MxZloycVItY3d4N2Nub2FTbWYzd1hPTlZXa2VPdEhfTGgxU2g3UlBrS0VvemRZTnI2SU9HZklMOEt4d2h1U1U2ZkpZT3dscE5SRzMtTHNqVXRGcy0yaWdXU3JxemdJRWNITEhVSkFSQnV0dnhKWlJWcF8xVWhNb0k4M1FaMDNtaHc)). Pakistan purchased more LNG as transit through the Strait of Hormuz failed to fully recover, signaling that the US-protected corridor has not normalized tanker movements ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-06/pakistan-buys-more-lng-as-flows-through-hormuz-fail-to-recover)). Iran may struggle to clear its substantial crude inventories even if sanctions are lifted, because limited onshore storage and weak demand complicate exports ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/06/iran-oil-supply-hormuz-china-energy.html)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — morning had Hormuz recovery; afternoon data shows recovery remains incomplete, with Pakistan’s emergency LNG purchase as proof of disruption.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Supply-risk premium lingers but is offset by the glut. Favors **Brent** range-bound with a downward bias; still **short oil** structurally.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: If a full convoy transits without incident in the next 24–48 hours, the disruption narrative fades. Confidence: Medium.

### 📌 South Korea launches historic 24-hour won trading

**Fact**
The South Korean won held steady as historic 24-hour trading began, marking a milestone in Seoul’s push to open its financial markets to global investors ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/asia/east-asia/article/3359506/south-korean-won-holds-steady-historic-24-hour-trading-begins)). The won eased `0.1%` to `1,531.40` per dollar after initially edging higher ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/asia/east-asia/article/3359506/south-korean-won-holds-steady-historic-24-hour-trading-begins)). Finance minister Koo Yun-cheol called it “the starting point for the won’s global leap,” with the round-the-clock trading centrepiece of an effort to improve foreign access and bolster the case for an MSCI developed-market upgrade ([WSJ](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMitAFBVV95cUxOVHBfQ0pYQV9Gc2M4cEZwM0Vzbm1PT2U3UGV2MndFdERwQWRqeHBqZWdaTWd6RGozX0o3T1YyWlgxdDFIcW56ZHpmMl8xM3EzdFN1TFpHdlVSZWRtcXZCZnNNVU9uM1VpVzVrMGdfbjVQT2Roemg0bFRCUXBTai1vLXFCaEJmWVBzRWcyeXM5QVBodnR0aUJWYXdudHl3dU5OMHlGLWl5ZUFlSHlzczdIWm8tNlg), [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/asia/east-asia/article/3359506/south-korean-won-holds-steady-historic-24-hour-trading-begins)).
