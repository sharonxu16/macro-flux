> [!abstract] Overview
> Oil surged past `$98/bbl` after Houthi missiles and drones struck two Saudi tankers in the Red Sea, tightening a multi‑front physical supply choke. Brent’s sprint toward `$100` ignited a global bond sell‑off, pushed German yields to a 15‑year high, and reinforced fears of a prolonged inflation‑fuelled rate‑hike cycle. The yen slumped to a fresh 40‑year low against the dollar, keeping BOJ intervention risk high, while South Korea’s Q2 GDP beat of `3.7%` y/y on AI chip exports catapulted the won to a 2½‑month high and lifted BOK tightening expectations. The common thread: oil‑driven inflation is tightening financial conditions and carving a sharp divide between AI‑beneficiary currencies and those exposed to energy‑import costs.
