### 📌 China Crackdown on Triple‑A Bond Ratings Intensifies

**Fact**
Chinese regulators are pressuring credit-rating agencies to limit triple-A designations for higher-interest borrowers, intensifying a long-running effort to curb the rampant use of top ratings in the nation’s `$20tn` corporate bond market ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/750c90d4-6c6f-464a-85c2-5ae86b085da8)). The move threatens to restrict credit access for firms that rely on triple-A ratings to attract funding, even as regulators aim to improve differentiation in a market where over `90%` of issues carry the highest grade.

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — formal regulatory push to limit top ratings represents a concrete tightening step beyond prior warnings.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Capital-flow channel risks wider Chinese corporate bond spreads and **weaker CNH** if credit repricing triggers forced deleveraging.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Any actual agency downgrades or a jump in Chinese onshore AA‑AAA spread; Confidence: Medium as enforcement timing remains uncertain.

### 📌 US Primary Dealers Hit Record Net Short Treasuries

**Fact**
For the first time in recent history, primary dealers have gone net short on debt they used to hold billions of dollars of, reflecting deep conviction that interest rates will stay elevated or rise further ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-11/banks-record-bond-short-sparks-quest-for-answers-credit-weekly)). The unprecedented short positioning underscores a market grappling with a world that’s good for the dollar and bad for bonds ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-12/traders-grapple-with-world-that-s-good-for-dollar-bad-for-bonds)).
