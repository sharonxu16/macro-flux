> [!abstract] Overview
> Oil’s collapse stalled and Brent edged off pre-war lows after Iran’s Revolutionary Guard warned that unauthorised Hormuz transits were “unacceptable and dangerous,” a direct intraday challenge to the tanker flow that had been dragging crude back to pre-conflict territory. The threat re-injected a risk premium just as Saudi Arabia prepared to restart loadings at its giant Ras Tanura terminal. In Japan, a government blueprint urging the BOJ to “fuel demand” clashed with the central bank’s own hawkish lean, injecting fresh doubts over the rate path and sending the yen lower. The cross‑market thread is that the disinflation tailwind from cheap oil now faces an Iranian pushback, while Japan’s policy confusion compounds an already volatile dollar‑led FX environment.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Iran IRGC Warns Against Unauthorised Hormuz Transits, Threatening Oil Supply Recovery

**Fact**
Saudi Arabia is set to restart loading crude at the giant Ras Tanura terminal inside the Persian Gulf, a landmark moment in the resumption of Middle Eastern supplies after the Iran war ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-25/saudis-set-to-restart-ras-tanura-oil-exports-as-gulf-flows-rise)). Brent oil erased all of its wartime gains after flows through the Strait of Hormuz ramped up following progress on a US‑Iran peace deal ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-24/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-june-25)). Iran’s elite Revolutionary Guard warned that it was “unacceptable and dangerous” for ships to transit the Strait of Hormuz without its approval, underscoring Tehran’s resolve to retain control over the waterway and to resist transits that bypass its authorization ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/25/iran-navy-shipping-recovery-strait-of-hormuz-unauthorized-routes-us-fragile-mou-.html)). The IRGC rejected alternative routes that were not approved by Iran ([Lloyds_List](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiqwFBVV95cUxQNndqZDZBa3M5X1pNZThIcmdiRThPUVpsa0hFMkxjZDFNUXEyVWltdmVyNVllb3NSTHpVSEF5b2NRcGVleF8wSHhmSzZIRFN2SmFqbHpUNFFmWVpVbW1DbUdfRW5xUnJxLWpFZk90Wks0NlRxU21xRmZBOVVNOFhCejk2M2VrUUxhNHVtQ1lqa3pIMFBMQWhLT3hKaGNvdUt5NlcxRWN3WU0xZ0U)). An oil tanker was reported to be navigating the strait, hugging the western coast, despite the IRGC’s threats ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/25/iran-warns-against-hormuz-crossings-without-authorisation)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Reversal — Iran’s explicit threat revives the Hormuz risk premium just as the reopening narrative had erased oil’s wartime gains.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk-premium channel supports **long Brent** and **long oil vol**; Asia FX that sold off on cheaper oil may stabilise if crude rebounds.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: If first sanctioned Saudi Ras Tanura loadings proceed without IRGC interference, the threat fades; Confidence: Medium due to cross-source confirmation of IRGC posture but thin evidence of actual disruption.
