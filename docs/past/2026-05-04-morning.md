## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Hormuz Escalation: "Project Freedom" launched, Iran warns of strikes, two vessels hit

**Fact**
The United States will launch an effort on Monday to "guide" stranded ships from the Iran-gripped Strait of Hormuz, President Donald Trump said, as two ships around the strait reported attacks. Trump gave few details about what could be a sweeping attempt to help hundreds of vessels and some 20,000 seafarers. Iran quickly denounced the move as a ceasefire violation [SCMP](URL). U.S. Central Command confirmed it will support the launch of Project Freedom in the Strait of Hormuz [US_CENTCOM](URL). A tanker was hit by unknown projectiles off UAE's Fujairah, UKMTO says [Reuters 1](URL). A bulk carrier was attacked by multiple small craft 11 nautical miles west of Sirik, Iran, according to UKMTO [Al Jazeera 1](URL). An Iran lawmaker said the Strait of Hormuz will never return to the status quo that existed before the US-Israel war on Tehran [Al Jazeera 2](URL). The Iranian military warned the US of attacks over the Hormuz mission [Al Jazeera 3](URL). Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said the US is "suffocating" Iran with economic and financial pressure [BBG 1](URL). OPEC+ agreed a third oil output quota hike since the Hormuz closure [Reuters 2](URL). Oil steadied as traders questioned the workability of Trump's plan, with a tanker reportedly hit in the waterway [BBG 2](URL).

### 📌 China Activates "Blocking Rules" for First Time Against US Sanctions on Oil Refiners

**Fact**
China has ordered companies throughout the country not to comply with US sanctions on five Chinese oil refiners accused of trading in Iranian fuel, a move observers say could mark a new stage in Beijing's pushback against American long-arm jurisdiction. The order is the first application of a measure designed to block "improper" foreign actions, and could be a potential headache for US sanctions enforcement, according to analysts [SCMP 1](URL). Beijing's decision to block Meta's $2 billion acquisition of Chinese AI start-up Manus was also described as a show of strength ahead of the Xi-Trump summit [SCMP 2](URL). Separately, China's exports of telecoms, computer and information services reached `808 billion yuan` ($118 billion) in 2025, up `13%` YoY, far outpacing traditional goods [SCMP 3](URL).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Base Case**: The "Blocking Rules" signal a new normal of selective financial decoupling, putting upward pressure on **USD/CNH** as capital flow risks rise.
> * **Tactical Trade**: **Long USD/CNH 3-month NDF**; **PIVOT to neutral** if US refrains from immediate secondary sanctions, suggesting diplomatic space ahead of the summit.

### 📌 Central Bank Divergence: RBA Hawkish Outlier, ECB Recession Fears, Fed Hemmed In

**Fact**
Australia's central bank is set to entrench its status as a hawkish outlier with a third straight interest-rate hike, diverging from peers that are mainly sitting tight to observe fallout from the US-Iran conflict [BBG 1](URL). The RBA is expected to hike to `4.35%` [BBG 2](URL). ECB policymaker Stournaras said euro zone recession concerns are "real and justified" [Reuters 1](URL). Minneapolis Fed President Kashkari said the Iran war limits the Fed's ability to provide rate guidance [Reuters 2](URL). Federal Reserve Vice Chair for Supervision Michael Barr warned that private credit could spark "psychological contagion" [BBG 3](URL).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Base Case**: RBA hike widens **AUD/USD** carry advantage short-term, but global risk-off from Hormuz caps upside; ECB recession and Fed pause reinforce **USD strength** vs EUR and JPY.
> * **Tactical Trade**: **Short EUR/AUD** on policy divergence; **PIVOT to flat** if Tuesday's RBA guidance sounds a markedly dovish note, signaling a peak.
