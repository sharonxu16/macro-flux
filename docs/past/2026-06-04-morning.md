### 📌 US Launches Broadest Tariff Wave Since Supreme Court Defeat, Citing Forced Labour

**Fact**
The Office of the US Trade Representative said China and 59 other economies failed to prohibit or adequately restrict US imports made with forced labour, subjecting them to punitive action ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3355882/china-eu-slam-proposed-us-tariffs-reject-forced-labour-allegations)). The proposal marks the White House’s first significant effort to resurrect sweeping tariffs since its defeat at the US Supreme Court ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/b2a48b50-7c7c-423a-b558-eee0821f3469)). The US has announced new tariffs of `10–12.5%` on dozens of countries accounting for almost all its imports ([BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cq6pe7nvldmo)). The administration simultaneously proposed a separate `25%` tariff on Brazilian goods ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/us/article/3355879/lula-thanks-china-beef-win-and-tells-us-after-tariffs-i-will-sell-someone-else)). Brazilian President Lula said the country “cannot accept treatment” after the new tariffs ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/3/lula-says-brazil-cannot-accept-treatment-after-new-us-tariffs-proposed)). Behind the scenes Trump is quietly rebuilding his tariff engine, preparing new avenues to impose duties after court setbacks ([CNN](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiYkFVX3lxTFBCUWFvY2pwbHRwUkNEdnlldVFrQk5SdFJ1bFhNQzY3dmVEaHFOalNVSzUyLURyVm9HaTZ5NHVrRzJkSm82amtYMUJZQjhhUkpNVzgxR2NLMEFkNWpScDQwWEJn)). EU trade chief Maros Sefcovic will meet China’s international trade envoy Li Chenggang in Paris on Thursday as both sides seek to defuse tensions that have pushed them to the brink of a trade war ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/article/3355859/eu-and-china-set-paris-talks-week-amid-mounting-trade-war-fears)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New — a qualitatively different tariff salvo emerges on the same day as diplomatic overtures, forcing markets to price a broader protectionist impulse rather than just the Iran‑supply risk.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk‑premium and capital‑flow channels favour **long USD**, **short CNH**, and **short EM Asia FX** via renewed tariff uncertainty and supply‑chain fragmentation.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Any firm retaliation announcement from EU or China at Thursday’s Paris meeting would confirm escalation; Confidence: High, given cross‑source government statements.
