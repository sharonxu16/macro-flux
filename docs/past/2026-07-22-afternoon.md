> [!abstract] Overview
> Brent surged past `$95` and Goldman Sachs warned of `$120` oil if the Strait of Hormuz remains disrupted, as shipping threats multiplied with the EU naval group raising its Red Sea threat level. The yen staged an intraday rebound after BOJ officials signaled openness to a faster rate-hike pace than economists expect, reversing some of the slide that had taken the currency past `163` to a 40-year trough. Gold vaulted above `$4,100` to a two-week high on combined Mideast risk and Fed repricing. Trump approved a Saudi nuclear deal while expanding tariffs to generic drugs, and the US threatened AI-related sanctions against China, layering trade-policy risk onto an already volatile energy complex. The day is compressing three macro impulses — physical oil supply disruption, Asian central-bank repricing, and tariff-front expansion — into a single risk-off flow favoring gold and dollar liquidity while punishing oil-importing EM currencies.
