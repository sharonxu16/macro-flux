> [!abstract] Overview
> The US-Iran conflict escalated further with a second day of American strikes and Iranian retaliation against Gulf allies, yet oil prices retreated from overnight highs as the Strait of Hormuz remained open and traffic steady, tempering supply-disruption fears. China’s June consumer inflation slowed to 1% while factory gate prices jumped to a near four-year high and turned negative month-on-month, signalling that upstream cost pressures are meeting tepid domestic demand. The cross-market thread is a divergence between contained oil risk premia and intensifying geopolitical and demand-side fragility, keeping dollar and gold bid but capping Asian FX upside.

### 📌 China CPI softens to 1%, PPI hits near four-year high but monthly momentum turns negative

**Fact**
China’s consumer inflation eased in June, while factory-gate prices surged to a near four-year high amid the Iran war turmoil ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/09/china-cpi-ppi-june-inflation-iran-war-.html)).
