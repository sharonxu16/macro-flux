> [!abstract] Overview
> The Xi-Trump summit concluded with warm optics but cold market reception as Chinese tech stocks sold off sharply, with the CSI 300 declining `1.1%` and US Trade Representative Greer reinforcing that Section 301 tariffs are here to stay, puncturing earlier hopes for comprehensive trade relief. The global bond rout intensified materially, with yields surging to multi-year highs from Japan to Germany as war-driven energy costs and fresh inflation data spooked investors, tightening financial conditions across major economies. The dollar rallied toward its best week since March on the hawkish repricing of Fed rate expectations under incoming Chair Warsh, while emerging-market equities posted their steepest drop in two months. In Asia, South Korea's Kospi cratered over `6%` from its record high as heavyweight tech names and Samsung union strike risks converged, deepening the rotation away from crowded AI-exposed markets. Oil prices held firm near elevated levels after Trump claimed China agreed to buy US crude, though skepticism over summit deliverables kept risk-asset relief in check. The cross-market thread is a tightening vice: sticky global yields and unresolved geopolitical risk are compressing equity valuations, eroding carry-trade logic, and tilting capital flows toward the dollar at the expense of Asia FX and emerging markets.
