> [!abstract] Overview

> Oil prices cratered and risk assets rallied after the first international vessel transited the Strait of Hormuz and Trump pledged the waterway’s reopening, crushing the energy risk premium that had dominated markets. The de‑escalation wave lifted Asian equities, slammed aluminum and grain futures, and sent benchmark bond yields lower, but lingering inflation warnings from ECB’s Nagel and Bloomberg Economics’ forecast that revived Chinese oil demand will rekindle price pressures kept the rally from running unimpeded. At the same session, Trump’s threat of 100% tariffs on French wine added a new tariff‑escalation tail risk that tempered European asset gains, while the BOJ’s `1%` hike widely expected tomorrow injects a separate monetary‑tightening cross‑current into Asia FX. The unifying thread is a fragile risk‑on pivot driven by Hormuz reopening, but fragmented by persistent inflation‑hawk commentary and the re‑emergence of trade war vol.
