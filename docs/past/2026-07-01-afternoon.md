> [!abstract] Overview
> Oil came under fresh pressure after Iran’s refusal to meet US envoys clouded the Doha peace process, while Iran claimed it was now selling crude at a 20% premium after 40 million barrels were stranded during the blockade. A firmer dollar, pinned by sticky US yields and fragile risk appetite, kept Asian currencies on the back foot.
