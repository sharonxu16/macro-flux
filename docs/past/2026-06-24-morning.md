> [!abstract] Overview
> A global technology-led selloff intensified, with South Korea’s KOSPI plunging nearly 10% after a regulatory warning on leveraged ETFs, amplifying concerns that the AI-driven equity rally has run too far and dragging emerging-market assets sharply lower. The dollar pushed to over one-year highs as hawkish Federal Reserve repricing continued, while the US issued sweeping sanction waivers on Iranian oil in a concrete step toward peace implementation that nonetheless saw crude prices edge lower. The cross-market thread is a decisive shift from risk-on exuberance to defensive positioning, favoring dollar strength and short EM exposures.

### 📌 US Issues Sweeping Iran Oil Sanctions Waivers

**Fact**
The United States has issued sweeping sanctions waivers covering all of Iran’s oil exports, as part of the broader US-Iran peace agreement, unlocking billions in revenue for Tehran ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/23/us-iran-oil-sanction-relief-strait-of-hormuz-peace-deal-.html)). The two-month waiver allows Iranian crude to flow to global markets ([Lloyd’s List](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMikgFBVV95cUxORVdHNjhvZGJieXJJSVRzWmdqUUdIdnlySXExQ3V5RTFGYUhjSnFJX3R0UGUwT2YyQnNodzdtU3VZSjBfb1ZrNjE2WWFfcDN2Ty12Nlhtb3hHbkE4Q2xXOE5GdERPa1ZULXQydl9FcEZVRTZmWlEyUjU4dXVGVEx5WDVQcXVVQUJMOURvTWp6Qm5xUQ)). International oil prices fell on Tuesday ([Xinhua](https://www.news.cn/fortune/20260624/e977d78d93a845caa908bac4d4ffda58/c.html)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration of US-Iran peace implementation, with a concrete and broad waiver replacing conditional language; oil markets instead focused on demand concerns.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Reduced geopolitical risk premium tilts risks toward **lower Brent** and **narrower USD/CNH** if Iranian supply expansion materializes.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: First confirmed cargoes of Iranian crude to US or European refineries under the waiver; Confidence: Medium, given the explicit two-month window and official action.

### 📌 China’s Trading Crackdown Seen Boosting Hong Kong’s Official Hub Role

**Fact**
China’s crackdown on cross-border securities trading could strengthen – rather than diminish – Hong Kong’s financial role, according to economists, as Beijing steers more capital through official channels and reinforces the city’s status as an offshore yuan hub. Diana Choyleva, founder and chief economist at Enodo Economics, said authorities were seeking to curb illegal capital outflows while pursuing “an increase in opening up the flow of capital, through Hong Kong via the connect schemes” ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3358091/chinas-trading-crackdown-seen-boosting-hong-kong-official-capital-hub)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Confirmation of capital-account tightening, but with a channeling effect that may formalize flows rather than halt them, reinforcing Hong Kong’s connect pipeline.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Capital-flow channel supports **stable HKD peg** and **CNH resilience** via official inflows, while short-term uncertainty weighs on luxury assets and shadow flows.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Any PBOC or SAFE announcement on expanding connect-scheme quotas; Confidence: Medium given the absence of official policy statement beyond the forum remarks.
