> [!abstract] Overview
> The Strait of Hormuz standoff escalated into direct US-Iran strikes and conflicting claims over the waterway’s status, driving Brent above `$79/bbl` and stoking rate-hike fears that sent US two-year yields to their highest since early 2025. A sharp AI rout hammered Korea, where SK Hynix crashed a record `15%` and dragged the won and broader EM benchmarks, as investors questioned the sustainability of the tech boom. A counter-narrative emerged in Chinese hard tech, with northbound stock-connect holdings hitting a record `3.13 trillion yuan` on a rotation into Beijing’s self-sufficiency champions. Oil-driven inflation anxiety and tech exhaustion fused into a broad risk-off tilt across Asia, punishing EM FX and rewarding the safety of the dollar and commodities.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Strait of Hormuz Standoff: New Strikes, Conflicting Claims

**Fact**
US forces launched fresh missile attacks against Iran in what has become a pattern of strikes and counterstrikes, with the two sides issuing conflicting declarations over whether the Strait of Hormuz is open to shipping ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-07-13/us-iran-trade-wave-of-attacks-video)). Global benchmark Brent rose above `$79` a barrel while West Texas Intermediate edged toward `$75` after US forces completed another wave of strikes against Iran ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-07-13/oil-jumps-as-us-conducts-wave-of-strikes-against-iran-video)). Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) targeted US military facilities in Kuwait, Bahrain and Oman after the latest US attacks ([Al Jazeera 1](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/13/new-iran-strikes-on-gulf-as-us-attacks-escalate-what-we-know)). The US and Iran exchanged a barrage of missile and drone airstrikes amid a dispute over the status of the Strait of Hormuz ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/us-iran-war-hormuz-oil-trump.html)). Oil prices jumped as the two countries trade attacks over the chokepoint ([Al Jazeera 2](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/13/oil-prices-jump-as-us-and-iran-trade-attacks-over-strait-of-hormuz)). Treasury two-year yields climbed to their highest level since early 2025 as oil gains fueled speculation the Federal Reserve will need to raise interest rates to combat inflation ([BBG 3](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/treasury-two-year-yield-rises-to-highest-since-2025-on-oil-gain)). Goldman Sachs said the Iran war is not enough to unseat inflation anchors and expects the Fed to keep rates on hold all year ([WallstreetCN 1](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiU0FVX3lxTE5VR0FxRWZyQjM3M1Q0eC1GZ2EzQk9fUjJkUVUyOTU5bUdscVROZ0o2ODhpTkdfVUh0R0lkN1A3c0xWN1VfdGtWYkJ2clh5dGRxdlZR)). India’s trade deficit widened in June, with uncertainty over the US-Iran conflict and shipping through the Strait of Hormuz continuing to cloud the outlook for global supply chains ([BBG 4](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/india-trade-deficit-widens-as-hormuz-uncertainty-clouds-outlook)). Abu Dhabi National Oil Co. is broadening options for long-term customers to take offshore oil from outside the Strait of Hormuz to circumvent the chokepoint ([BBG 5](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/uae-tweaks-offshore-oil-pricing-to-support-non-hormuz-options)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]

> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk-premium and inflation channel support **long Brent** and **short KRW/TWD**, while higher **US short-end yields** weigh on EM FX.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: First confirmed safe commercial convoy or a ceasefire agreement would reverse the oil spike; further strikes entrench the premium. Confidence: High.

### 📌 AI Chip Exhaustion: SK Hynix Record Drop Roils Korea, Won

**Fact**
South Korea’s AI-fueled stock rally came under renewed pressure Monday as SK Hynix Inc. tumbled by a record `15%`, underscoring growing investor concerns that the boom has become overstretched ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/sk-hynix-shares-drop-in-seoul-after-much-hyped-us-trading-debut)). Traders braced for won volatility after the blockbuster SK Hynix listing ([FT 1](https://www.ft.com/content/b433644d-caba-4962-8d60-f46fcd9716f9)). TSMC, SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics together account for `29%` of the MSCI Emerging Markets index, amplifying the impact of the AI rout on the benchmark ([FT 2](https://www.ft.com/content/734c23c8-73ed-4a4a-8150-49d12d891c30)). AI panic spread, with US pre-market memory chip stocks declining and SK Hynix plunging `15.4%` in its largest-ever drop ([WallstreetCN 2](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiU0FVX3lxTFB2eFhBYU52Y2ZQeHRsZnBqdVo2SnRPMG1wdkk4VHhPMXFoU2pZWk85U3B1blIwcFVhaDNCWllXVC16bVd5dkFwa25FWkRMOFl5TURv)). > [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New; AI overextension fears trigger a severe repricing in Korean tech, breaking the prior rally’s momentum.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Capital-flow and growth-expectation channel support **short KRW** and underweight **KOSPI/TSMC** until sentiment stabilizes.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: TSMC’s Q2 earnings outlook and any AI capex guidance from hyperscalers. Confidence: High.

### 📌 China Hard Tech Inflows Hit Record Amid Global AI Selloff

**Fact**
Overseas capital has poured into China’s hard technology champions at an unprecedented pace, driving northbound holdings under the mainland-Hong Kong Stock Connect scheme to a record high of `3.13 trillion yuan` (US$`461.65` billion) by the end of June ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/business/markets/article/3360310/chinese-hard-tech-giants-see-value-surge-first-half-global-investors-pour-capital)). The figures point to a broader repositioning of overseas portfolios towards industries linked to Beijing’s push for technological advancement and self-sufficiency, marking a departure from consumer and financial stocks that had traditionally dominated foreign investment ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/business/markets/article/3360310/chinese-hard-tech-giants-see-value-surge-first-half-global-investors-pour-capital)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Confirmation; structural rotation into China’s tech self-sufficiency theme reaches a new high.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Capital-flow channel supports **CNH** and Chinese A-share tech, offering a divergent hedge to the global AI rout.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: July stock-connect flow data and Beijing’s next policy signal on tech self-reliance. Confidence: Medium.
