> [!abstract] Overview
> The US and China formally agreed to establish trade and investment councils and roll back tariffs following the Trump-Xi summit, while Beijing signaled aircraft purchases and farm market access commitments. Oil supply fears intensified as the US Treasury allowed a sanctions waiver on Russian seaborne oil to lapse and Iran announced plans to publish a traffic management mechanism for the Strait of Hormuz. Global stockpiles of manufactured goods are being rushed on fears of an energy-supply crunch, and UBS warned global oil inventories could approach all-time lows by end-May if Hormuz remains closed. The bond selloff threatens to knock AI stock momentum off course, with incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh facing a split committee over whether to cut rates amid persistent inflation. The cross-market thread is a world simultaneously pricing a potential US-China trade detente and an escalating physical energy crisis, pulling bond yields higher and keeping FX markets on edge as Iran's next moves and the durability of the tariff rollback remain untested.
