> [!abstract] Overview
> Oil prices slid and the Hormuz risk premium retreated as Saudi Arabia restarted crude loadings at Ras Tanura after a nearly four-month halt and tanker traffic continued to flow despite a vessel strike, overwhelming the temporary UN evacuation pause. The deepening Asia tech rout intensified with South Korea’s KOSPI triggering a second trading halt this week as Apple’s memory-cost price hikes and Kioxia’s 12% slump laid bare the cost-push strain behind AI demand. The PBOC signalled a new liquidity management era, with a Bloomberg survey pointing to a 1.35% inaugural rate for its overnight reverse repo tool on Monday, marking a shift that could compress short-end rates and steady CNH funding. Cross-asset price action reflects a collision of restored physical oil supply, fragile tech sentiment, and a delicate Fed-ECB policy divergence framed by falling European inflation expectations.

### 📌 PBOC to Debut Overnight Reverse Repo at `1.35%` on Monday, Survey Shows

**Fact**
China’s central bank is expected to price its inaugural overnight reverse repo operation slightly below the seven-day rate when it debuts the tool on Monday, a Bloomberg survey shows; the median estimate is `1.35%` compared with the current seven-day rate of `1.5%`, implying the new tool will offer a lower funding cost and extend the yield curve to the very short end (`BBG`](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-26/pboc-may-set-new-overnight-operation-rate-at-1-35-survey-shows)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New — the introduction of an overnight facility marks a structural enhancement of the PBOC’s liquidity framework, allowing finer short-end control and potentially compressing the money-market curve.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Liquidity channel favors **lower CNH implied yields**, **narrower CNH-US rate spreads**, and a **stable CNH** against a hawkish dollar if the tool smooths funding volatility.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Actual announced rate and tenor on Monday; any deviation from `1.35%` or the absence of standing facility features would alter expectations; Confidence: High as the survey and official signaling are consistent.
