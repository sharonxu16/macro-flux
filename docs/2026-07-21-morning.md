> [!abstract] Overview
> Oil briefly surged above `$90/bbl` as the US-Iran war widened, with Houthi allies declaring a maritime embargo on Saudi Arabia and two tankers attacked, but retreated after mediators proposed a 10-day ceasefire. The cross-market thread is the simultaneous escalation of geopolitical risk premium in crude and protectionist trade friction, lifting the US dollar and keeping Asia FX on the defensive.

### 📌 China Ends Decade-Long Tax Breaks for EV Batteries and Solar Cells

**Fact**
China will levy a `2%` consumption tax on lithium-ion batteries from September 1 and on solar cells from April 1 next year, ending tax exemptions that helped fuel their rise ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3361207/chinas-ev-industry-rose-aid-tax-breaks-what-happens-when-they-end)). Analysts estimated the levy could add about `1,000` yuan (US`$147`) to the cost of producing an EV, a fresh squeeze on carmakers’ already razor-thin margins, as Beijing steps up efforts to curb overcapacity and domestic price wars ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3361207/chinas-ev-industry-rose-aid-tax-breaks-what-happens-when-they-end)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Acceleration — withdrawing a long-standing subsidy adds a deflationary impulse to China’s already softening consumption and auto demand.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Capital-flow channel supports **short CNH** and **short Chinese EV/tech equities**, with bearish spillover to lithium and cobalt.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: September 1 implementation — any signs of sharper-than-expected production cuts could accelerate the move; Confidence: Medium.
