### 📌 Hong Kong Expands RMB Liquidity Facility by 150%, Deepening Offshore CNH Market

**Fact**
Hong Kong’s updated yuan financing tool for banks, featuring a quota 150 per cent larger than before, was launched on Friday, giving a significant boost to the use of the Chinese currency in daily global business ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/business/banking-finance/article/3359981/how-rmb-business-facility-will-help-consolidate-hong-kongs-financial-role)). The RMB Business Facility quota was expanded to 500 billion yuan from Friday, with the tenor increased to two to three years, up from one ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/business/banking-finance/article/3359981/how-rmb-business-facility-will-help-consolidate-hong-kongs-financial-role)). The facility allows participating banks to borrow yuan at the relatively cheap Shanghai three-month interbank offered rate, which is about 2 percentage points lower than the US Federal Reserve rate ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/business/banking-finance/article/3359981/how-rmb-business-facility-will-help-consolidate-hong-kongs-financial-role)). It operates through the city’s currency swap line with the People’s Bank of China, channelling onshore yuan liquidity into Hong Kong’s offshore market ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/business/banking-finance/article/3359981/how-rmb-business-facility-will-help-consolidate-hong-kongs-financial-role)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: New — The facility expansion is a structural deepening of offshore CNH liquidity not covered in the prior macro state, not a simple confirmation of existing PBOC trends.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Liquidity and capital-flow channel; a deeper, cheaper CNH pool supports **offshore yuan usage** and tilts risks toward a **narrower CNH-CNY spread**.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Volume uptake data from HKMA in the first week of the expanded facility; Confidence: Medium based on single-source SCMP reporting with official HKMA sourcing.
