> [!abstract] Overview
> The afternoon session saw a clear risk-off tilt in Asia, led by a retreat in Hong Kong equities as HSBC missed profit estimates on a fraud-related charge and provisions for the Iran war, with the Hang Seng Index shedding over 300 points. Oil prices eased from earlier highs as a Maersk vessel successfully transited the Strait of Hormuz under US escort, but the ceasefire remains fragile after Iran struck an Emirati oil port and a South Korean-operated ship. South Korea rebuffed Trump’s call to join “Project Freedom,” while the EU sounded out industry on a new trade weapon targeting Chinese overcapacity. The cross-market thread: the dollar held firm on haven demand, Brent stayed above `$110`, and Asian FX—particularly the rupiah and rupee—came under renewed pressure.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 Hong Kong: HSBC Misses, HSI Slides on Risk-Off

**Fact**
Hong Kong’s benchmark Hang Seng Index fell as much as 1.6% in afternoon trading, on track for its biggest drop in three weeks, dragged by HSBC Holdings Plc after the lender missed first-quarter earnings estimates, booking a fraud-related charge and provisions for the Iran war ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-05-05/hsbc-misses-estimates-on-fraud-charge-economic-risks-video), [2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/live-blog/2026-05-05/ftse-100-live-iran-war-trump-oil-prices-pound-hsbc-bonds-gold-what-s-moving-uk-markets-right-now-markets-today-mos7ez9p)). HSBC reported a first-quarter pre-tax profit of $9.4 billion, marginally missing analysts' estimates, with higher expected credit losses ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/05/hsbc-q1-earnings-banking-finance.html)). The bank’s net profit was broadly flat at $6.94 billion, missing the $7.07 billion consensus, as “lower interest rates and provisions linked to the Middle East conflict offset solid growth in its wealth management business” ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/business/banking-finance/article/3352456/hsbc-profit-flat-middle-east-provisions-offset-wealth-growth)). HSBC’s CFO said there is still confidence in achieving a full-year RoTE of 17% or higher, but noted that the outlook for Hong Kong commercial real estate is “weak” due to “uncertain inflation and interest rate trends” ([HKEJ 1](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMipgJBVV95cUxPLVB6T2M4UkRaalRLbGN2S3RMWkZfcGxjbDV1S3ZzOGlLc1dwUkpwcDFYM2x6OW81cVVNVVUwZERXMmppSGh5aTJkS3BxU0cwZDVqcWRjR0FRVlhrOThGYXk0ZnVFdXpOMW5sMTF3QWJyWE1SZGQxM2M5aTNZMHZta2N2U1BMbU0xTVZWRDA1WGstdG40d3dSRk1RSWhJdF9QZmNUVmY0cUZPOWoycFZwTUR3d3htYzJiZE1IbTVjM1JWdDZyVlhmeThPUTJxdzgyTzhYS3B2cHVzM2hZcGpORlgtNlBRb25EamlzSXRfUDRwR0lQelpPdXNKd3diRkNkWHZPcmI5NjNEVlUxRWN6NW9DU2NIeThWdXZ6NU1lNDVOZE41VUE), [2](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMipwNBVV95cUxORkFlaXpwY3FKRnQyRTJ4OFZ4RjRIU25oaGtvVUFFYTlVbUlBcGs5dnZhR2c2MGI2VXo3S3JNSjZaTm1SclFBaUczbHh6Z1JmajdPVXFTOFVKNzBTNXNwbjF0WTRZWVBVMjBpaVFWZzBHYXc5WWw4NzBodjd0S1czb0t5SXg2LUdPZlBmc2ZoQ3pqR0xKYWRVS2NBUTJWNm1LVW5ldXRvckZnS09BbG8tbXFjTW92SjB1aXFPVkNlMkN5V1J3dU1qTHg3NThOd1E1c2UtcWNfb2szbjR4a2ZXNVBoVm81X1RGTm52ZGNYbTRTRmFSODE5LU5YVG5Ea181dkcwT1l0Wm1iTnctbjNYT0tvTTktT1dOc3o3ODRLNndSQ3FxczZrR2lsYlJfemowTDQyTGNkTVhCNU1UeUd5cVVIdGJWR0V0TGNDNXhzX3NUcXBTRU5wZnBhZEhzX2dtZ0ctblFXRVo5aEo0Z2ZaOXVOMGRoSnk5ZklwbHZBWXo1X2liZ3R5Wld1bmdVRnVGOTM3Q1lWaW9DMm0wOURYRVR2cW1INlE)).

### 📌 Strait of Hormuz: Fire on South Korean Ship, Seoul rebuffs Trump

**Fact**
South Korea will investigate a fire on one of its vessels in the Strait of Hormuz, with President Trump blaming the incident on an Iranian attack ([CNA](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiogFBVV95cUxNVi0waEFhaWZ0WlJXRXYwMi00clBqVmhPaFVjMHI2cnFWT1ZjYm1JWHVNODhiVjFweUxTTUdmZDhGeTAxdzFyZVlGNTZqemRoZnpOeGdBRi1MYXBnbEQ3S1JqdVg4LVljbmVNUFFWZjJ1YnR0WWh0S1ZTMEpUbzlyQkc4Mlh3WmRLczhLektGM2xqREpLLThvLXoyMk1KZkR4TVE)). Seoul has rebuffed Trump’s call to join “Project Freedom,” indicating that “a UN mandate and a global coalition are required before Seoul joins Trump’s mission to reopen the strait” ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/week-asia/politics/article/3352462/south-korea-rebuffs-trumps-project-freedom-call-after-hormuz-ship-fire)). A US-flagged Maersk commercial vessel transited the strait under US military protection ([BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cjwp432d0v5o)), which contributed to oil prices easing from their highs, though the IEA warned of a “dangerous escalation” ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/5/5/oil-prices-surge-as-violence-flares-in-strait-of-hormuz)).
> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Base Case**: Iran’s strikes on the UAE and a South Korean vessel harden the supply-risk premium, complicating the risk-on trade for Asian FX and supporting the case for elevated BOK hawkishness.
> * **Tactical Trade**: **Long USD/KRW**; **PIVOT to short USD/KRW** if South Korea announces it will join Project Freedom, signaling a de-escalation of bilateral risk.
