> [!abstract] Overview
> The US‑Iran war entered a new phase as CENTCOM confirmed an eighth consecutive night of airstrikes while Iran expanded counterattacks beyond Jordan to US‑aligned Gulf states including Kuwait and Bahrain, transforming what began as a retaliatory exchange into a widening regional conflict that is no longer confined to the Hormuz chokepoint. Compounding the energy supply shock, the Caspian Pipeline Consortium suspended Black Sea loadings after a drone strike on its tankers, opening a second major oil export disruption that threatens roughly `1.5 million bpd` of crude flows alongside the Strait of Hormuz. On a separate front, Russia launched its largest ballistic missile attack on Kyiv since the war began, exploiting Ukraine's acute shortage of Patriot air defense munitions and draining an already stressed global security backdrop. The simultaneous intensification of war on two fronts — each directly striking energy infrastructure — is tightening physical oil supply while reinforcing a broad risk‑off bid for the dollar against Asian currencies.
