> [!abstract] Overview
> The Bank of Korea delivered its first rate hike since 2023 and warned of more tightening, sparking a 7% plunge in the KOSPI that tripped circuit breakers and triggered an Asia-wide tech rout after regulators also moved to ban new listings of leveraged single‑stock ETFs tied to Samsung and SK Hynix. TSMC’s explosive 77% profit beat and a $100 billion Arizona investment were drowned out by the sell-off, highlighting how chip‑cycle euphoria is colliding with hawkish macro policy. Simultaneously, the Strait of Hormuz blockade deepened: US strikes hit Bandar Abbas, all Iraqi crude loadings were suspended, and the US strategic reserve fell to its lowest since 1983, keeping Brent elevated and reinforcing a stagflationary current that starves Asia of easy‑money fuel even as domestic inflation pushes central banks toward tighter settings.

South Korea will temporarily halt new listings of single‑stock leveraged exchange traded products to curb market volatility after a surge in popularity of funds tied to the two chip giants ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/south-korea-to-halt-new-listings-of-single-stock-leveraged-etfs)). Emerging‑market stocks resumed their slide as the Korean curbs fanned the sell‑off in chip heavyweights ([BBG 3](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/emerging-stocks-slump-as-korea-curbs-fan-selloff-in-chip-giants)).
