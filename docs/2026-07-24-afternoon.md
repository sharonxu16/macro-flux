> [!abstract] Overview
> The US imposed a sweeping new wave of tariffs on 60 trading partners, citing forced labour, drawing immediate rebukes across Asia and Europe as President Trump courts a wider trade war while also threatening a "massive attack" on Iran. ### 📌 China strikes back at EU with export controls, escalating economic retaliation

**Fact**
China added 14 European entities to its export control list, hitting back at a new round of EU sanctions on Russia that affected 14 firms from mainland China and Hong Kong ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/global-economy/article/3361732/china-adds-eu-entities-export-control-list)). China’s Ministry of Commerce announced the action immediately following the EU sanctions, signaling a tit-for-tat escalation in the economic conflict between Brussels and Beijing.

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Reversal from prior state, which focused on US-China dynamics. This signals a new EU-China economic front that compounds global trade fragmentation.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Risk-premium channel favors **EUR/USD** downside on China-exposed European industrials and raises the risk of broader supply-chain disruption in critical technologies.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: EU's response within 48 hours will determine if this becomes a prolonged tit-for-tat spiral; Confidence: Medium based on the specific, immediate nature of the retaliatory action.

### 📌 Oil falls back below $100 even as Houthi attacks heighten Red Sea risks

**Fact**
Brent crude traded near `$100` a barrel after Houthi attacks on tankers in the Red Sea opened a new front in the conflict, with prices falling back below the threshold during the session ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-23/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-july-24), [FT](https://www.ft.com/content/3fd494d1-ee74-4c0f-9cd6-34e2283961df)). The number of tanker crossings in the Strait of Hormuz dropped to the lowest levels in two months on Thursday ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/24/us-attacks-iran-as-houthis-allow-chinese-ships-to-pass-whats-the-latest)). A Greek tanker carrying Saudi crude exited the Red Sea with its transponder off, while another Chinese vessel and Russian cargoes headed toward the Bab el-Mandeb strait after Houthi attacks heightened risks ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-24/tanker-exits-red-sea-dark-as-another-china-ship-heads-to-strait)). Analysts noted that Houthi attacks for now are shaping who moves Saudi crude, not whether it moves ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/24/as-oil-soars-experts-watch-red-sea-tankers-for-clarity-on-houthi-blockade)). Cathay Pacific Airways will raise fuel surcharges by up to 41 per cent on most passenger flights from August, citing escalating tensions in the Middle East ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/hong-kong/transport/article/3361713/cathay-raise-passenger-fuel-surcharges-41-amid-middle-east-tensions)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Confirmation of the Red Sea chokepoint disruption, but the intraday pullback below `$100` signals no immediate acceleration in supply loss, as selective passage for Chinese tankers tempers a total blockade scenario.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Inflation and risk-premium channel favors **long Brent** on dips but suggests fading momentum for aggressive **short Asia FX** momentum tied solely to oil, with tariffs now the dominant FX driver.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: A repeat attack on a non-Chinese tanker transiting Bab el-Mandeb in the next 24 hours; Confidence: Medium due to the volatile and selective nature of Houthi targeting.
