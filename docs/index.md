### 📌 China Activates "Blocking Rules" Against US Oil Sanctions

**Fact** | Source: ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3352278/us-sanctioned-chinese-oil-refineries-now-china-really-pushing-back))

China has ordered companies nationwide not to comply with US sanctions on five Chinese oil refiners accused of trading in Iranian fuel ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3352278/us-sanctioned-chinese-oil-refineries-now-china-really-pushing-back)). The order is the first application of Beijing's "Blocking Rules," designed to block "improper" foreign actions. Analysts say this marks a new stage in Beijing's pushback against US long-arm jurisdiction.

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** — This is a significant escalation in the sanctions enforcement game. If Chinese refiners comply with Beijing's order and continue Iranian crude purchases, it undermines US pressure on Iran and complicates any Hormuz reopening deal. The PBOC and Chinese state banks may face secondary sanctions risk.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — Supports long crude relative to products if Chinese refining runs are sustained on Iranian feedstock. Bearish for tanker rates if the US escalates enforcement against Chinese shipping.

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** — The `188k bpd` hike is symbolic — it signals OPEC+ unity post-UAE but does little to replace the `~17M bpd` that normally transits Hormuz. The real supply response is coming from US shale exports, which are now the swing producer of last resort.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — Supports long US crude (WTI vs Brent) as US export capacity becomes the marginal barrel. The OPEC+ quota adjustment is too small to move the global balance.
