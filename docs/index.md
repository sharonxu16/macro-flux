<small>May 1 12am to May 2 12am HKT</small>

---

> [!abstract] Overview
> The dominant macro theme remains the Iran war and its cascading effects: oil at `$126/bbl` intraday, a synchronized G-7 pivot toward rate hikes, and Japan’s first FX intervention in two years (`$34.5B`). The Fed’s leadership battle intensifies as Chair Powell vows not to be a "shadow chair," while the ECB and BoE warn of June rate rises. In China, the PBOC held its 1-year MLF rate at `2.50%` and expanded tariff-free access for 53 African nations, but the macro backdrop of rising oil and a hawkish Fed tilts against EM Asia FX. The common thread: oil supply risk is simultaneously tightening monetary policy, weakening Asian currencies, and inflating energy equities.

## 🔬 Narrative Watch

### 📌 G-7 Central Banks Pivot Hawkish on Iran War Energy Shock

**Fact** | Source: ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/c78b853e-177b-4a67-9507-05e1a404c05a)), ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/nagel-favors-ecb-june-rate-hike-unless-outlook-improves-markedly)), ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/b6df75b7-4cf2-464a-a829-d8b39852bfd6))
The ECB and BoE have explicitly warned of rate rises as they grapple with the Middle East energy shock. ECB's Nagel favors a June rate hike unless the outlook improves markedly ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/nagel-favors-ecb-june-rate-hike-unless-outlook-improves-markedly)). ECB's Makhlouf says upside risks to inflation have intensified ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/ecb-s-makhlouf-says-upside-risks-to-inflation-have-intensified)). Lagarde has hinted the ECB could consider a June hike, with traders pricing three 25bp hikes in Europe this year ([WallstreetCN](https://wallstreetcn.com/...)). Eurozone April CPI surged to `3.0%`, adding pressure ([WallstreetCN](https://wallstreetcn.com/...)). Meanwhile, BoE Governor Bailey says the bank faces the "most difficult combination" of economic effects and cannot shield households from the Iran energy crisis ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/b6df75b7-4cf2-464a-a829-d8b39852bfd6)). The BoE's chief economist is the sole dissenter, arguing for faster rate hikes ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/boe-s-sole-dissenter-says-rates-need-to-rise-more-promptly)). A Bloomberg analysis concludes that if Warsh pushes for a US rate cut, it's likely to go against the G-7 grain as soon as June ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-05-01/global-rates-latest-war-may-force-g-7-rate-hikes-in-june)). RBA is expected to raise to `4.35%` on May 5 ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/...)).

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** Synchronized G-7 tightening driven by a supply-side oil shock. Central banks hiking into slowing growth, a classic stagflationary impulse. The hawkish G-7 vs accommodative PBOC divergence pressures CNH.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** Favors long USD against Asia FX (CNH, KRW, TWD) and short duration in G-7 rates. Supports tactical short EUR/USD on the ECB hike path and long USD/JPY positioned for further intervention failure.

### 📌 Japan Intervenes with $34.5B; Citigroup Exits Yen Trade

**Fact** | Source: ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/japan-likely-spent-34-5-billion-in-fx-intervention-to-boost-yen)), ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/japanese-fx-intervention-wipes-out-yens-iran-war-losses.html)), ([Investing](https://www.investing.com/...))
Japan likely spent `$34.5B` in its first currency intervention since July 2024 to support the yen, per a Bloomberg analysis of BOJ accounts ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/japan-likely-spent-34-5-billion-in-fx-intervention-to-boost-yen)). The yen surged intraday, wiping out losses from the Iran war escalation ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/japanese-fx-intervention-wipes-out-yens-iran-war-losses.html)). Japan's Finance Minister had previously warned traders to keep their "smartphones close" during Golden Week ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/b1020045-8a61-46ea-8add-2ff85eafb325)). Citigroup exited its yen trade citing oil risks ([Investing](https://www.investing.com/...)).

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** `$34.5B` is large but likely insufficient against structural drivers: oil import costs, widening rate differentials, and safe-haven USD demand. Intervention smooths the move but doesn't reverse it. The market will test the next line.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** Favors selling rallies in JPY. The intervention creates a tactical opportunity to fade the move. Supports long USD/JPY on dips.

### 📌 Oil Spikes to $126 on Hormuz Blockade; Reserves Dwindling

**Fact** | Source: ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/ce575380-eccc-447c-a64e-493957d9b037)), ([BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx21m88rd14o)), ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/b26ba4ce-4324-4ea9-926d-caf036f20832))
Brent crude hit `$126/bbl` intraday — its highest since 2022 — after reports the US military would brief Trump on new Iran strike options ([BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx21m88rd14o)). Oil later fell back on news of an Iranian peace proposal ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/...)). The FT reports oil markets are one month from a crunch point as global reserves dwindle ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/b26ba4ce-4324-4ea9-926d-caf036f20832)). The Strait of Hormuz blockade continues, creating a divergence between Saudi Arabia (windfall) and UAE (petrodollar income drop) ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/saudi-arabia-set-for-oil-windfall-after-hormuz-boosts-prices)). Fertilizer boss Yara warns billions of meals are at risk due to the Iran war disrupting fertilizer supply ([BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cpwp50v4ye7o)).

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** Oil is pricing sustained supply disruption, not a temporary spike. `$126/bbl` is at levels that historically trigger demand destruction. The fertilizer angle adds a food price channel: a stagflationary impulse with global reach.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** Supports long energy equities and long oil futures on dips. Favors short consumer discretionary and short EM equities exposed to food import costs. Net negative for Asia ex-Japan FX.

### 📌 Fed Leadership Tensions: Powell vs. Warsh

**Fact** | Source: ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/inside-the-fed-powell-and-warsh-set-to-clash.html)), ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-30/warsh-must-confront-risks-of-dissenting-in-his-first-fed-vote)), ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/184f1dce-7ee5-4796-ae82-e18250737815))
Chair Powell has vowed he won't be a "shadow chair," but a clash with incoming Governor Warsh appears unavoidable ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/inside-the-fed-powell-and-warsh-set-to-clash.html)). Warsh must confront the risks of dissenting in his first Fed vote ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-30/warsh-must-confront-risks-of-dissenting-in-his-first-fed-vote)). Three Fed officials (Kashkari, Hammack, Logan) said rising uncertainty from the war drove their dissents ([BBG](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/fed-s-kashkari-and-hammack-say-war-has-changed-rate-outlook)). The FT characterizes this as "Powell's defiant curtain call" ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/184f1dce-7ee5-4796-ae82-e18250737815)). Kalshi bettors had predicted Powell would stay on as governor after his chair term ends ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/kalshi-bettors-prediction-powell-to-stay-as-fed-governor.html)).

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** The Fed enters maximal leadership uncertainty as the macro outlook deteriorates. A Warsh dissent would break FOMC consensus norms and signal politicization of monetary policy, adding a volatility premium to the front end.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** Favors long UST volatility. Medium-term bearish USD if Fed credibility erodes, but near-term war and energy shocks remain USD-supportive.
