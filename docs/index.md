### 📌 China Deploys "Blocking Rules" Against US Sanctions on Oil Refineries

**Fact** | Source: ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3352278/us-sanctioned-chinese-oil-refineries-now-china-really-pushing-back))

China has ordered companies nationwide not to comply with US sanctions on five Chinese oil refiners accused of trading in Iranian fuel, marking the **first application** of its "Blocking Rules" designed to counter improper foreign actions. The order was issued in response to a series of US sanctions and could be a potential headache for US sanctions enforcement, according to analysts ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3352278/us-sanctioned-chinese-oil-refineries-now-china-really-pushing-back)). Separately, Beijing blocked Meta's `$2 billion` acquisition of Chinese-founded AI startup Manus last Monday, with analysts viewing this as a show of strength ahead of the Xi-Trump summit ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/opinion/china-opinion/article/3352135/chinas-manus-block-show-strength-ahead-xi-trump-summit)).

**[AI Reasoning] Macro Lens** — The Blocking Rules deployment escalates the long-arm jurisdiction conflict directly into operational territory. If Chinese refineries defy US sanctions, Iranian oil flows resume more easily, reducing the supply premium. If they comply despite Beijing's order, it signals Beijing's enforcement capacity is limited. This is the most significant test of US secondary sanctions enforcement since the Trump 1.0 era.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — Supports a modest Brent risk premium reduction if China's non-compliance is credible. Watch for PBOC USD/CNY fixing — a weaker fixing would signal Beijing is offsetting sanctions pressure on trade competitiveness.

**[AI Reasoning] Potential Market Impact** — The quota hike is too small to move Brent; the UAE exit and Saudi caution are the dominant signals.
