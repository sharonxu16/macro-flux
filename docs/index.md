> [!abstract] Overview
> Intraday price action was dominated by a violent two-way swing in crude and Asian currencies after Iran claimed to have hit a US warship with missiles in the Strait of Hormuz—a claim swiftly denied by Washington—while a tanker attack off Fujairah kept the physical supply risk firmly on the table. **Brent** spiked intraday before paring gains on the US denial, while **KRW** and **KOSPI** rallied to new records on AI optimism, decoupling from the geopolitical noise as the BOK signalled an eventual rate hike. China's unprecedented activation of "Blocking Rules" against US sanctions on oil refiners is now the dominant macro overhang for **CNH** and the broader decoupling narrative. Across markets, tech earnings momentum is grinding Asian equities ex-Japan to all-time highs, while the ECB confirmed a June rate hike is "all but inevitable," cementing a hawkish global central bank pivot.
