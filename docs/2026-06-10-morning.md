> [!abstract] Overview
> The twin forces of a potential Iran ceasefire and renewed US strikes on Tehran drove gold below `$4,200` and oil lower, even as geopolitical volatility persists. The Pentagon’s formal reinstatement of Alibaba, Baidu, and BYD to its military blacklist re-ignites US-China tech decoupling fears, threatening to upend the KOSPI’s prior `8%` surge and extend the Hang Seng’s fifth straight decline. China’s May CPI held at `1.2%` versus `1.3%` expected, reinforcing the dual-economy divergence, while BOJ hike expectations and Korea’s FX bank scrutiny add to Asia FX tensions. The shared thread across markets is the repricing of geopolitical risk premia from Iran to trade wars, tilting flows away from Asia tech and toward safe havens.
