### 📌 China’s May Credit Data Confirms Weak Domestic Demand

**Fact**
New yuan loans in May stood at `5200` billion yuan, well below expectations, while aggregate social financing came in at `2.03` trillion yuan and M2 money supply grew `8.6%` year‑on‑year ([WallstreetCN 1](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiU0FVX3lxTE94YUFBVGdBNWt6MzlFNlliVHhwSHl6ZVhxQW90cWtZOWFKaWJtOUZiZUtuYTBMMzBKeG9VRUwyYlJNVEt2RW16bm50bDFRa3JPaFFF)). A publication affiliated with the People’s Bank of China urged investors to “view the slowdown in loan growth objectively and rationally,” arguing that the economy is shifting away from credit‑intensive industries ([WallstreetCN 2](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiU0FVX3lxTE91S3BQQTkxckhFdGJiVkxlbDNNYXhpazdpZEVLYktpZmo2RDhmSW1RR3BfSmc3U3h2bEYyNTcxZnRqcmhYVnIzVjYwMmNTYjVyOUxJ)). The data underscore that the private sector remains reluctant to borrow despite successive policy‑easing measures.

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Confirmation of the persistent China demand‑weakness story; the loan figure reinforces the view that structural headwinds are overwhelming marginal stimulus.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Sustained growth under‑performance caps **CNH** upside and keeps **Asia FX** bid, while supporting **long CNH bonds** on further easing expectations.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: Next PBOC MLF operation — a rate cut would confirm an easing pivot and accelerate CNH depreciation; Confidence: High based on repeated weak data and official commentary.

### 📌 US Rate‑Cut Expectations Pushed into 2027, ECB Stays Hawkish

**Fact**
A Bloomberg survey of economists shows that most now expect the Federal Reserve to hold rates steady until the middle of next year, pushing the first rate cut further into 2027 ([BBG 1](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-12/economists-push-rate-cut-expectations-into-2027-survey)). In Europe, Bundesbank President Joachim Nagel said the ECB “stands ready to raise rates again” and will keep all options open ([FT](https://www.ft.com/content/ff7f0ce7-7202-4a34-b924-1ce1b1d742f4)), while Governing Council member Peter Kazimir stated that inflation’s broadening means rates must be lifted further ([BBG 2](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-12/ecb-s-kazimir-says-rates-must-be-lifted-more-to-tackle-inflation)). The ECB’s hike was the first among G7 central banks since the Iran war began ([BBG 3](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-06-12/ecb-stands-out-with-first-g7-rate-hike-since-start-of-iran-war)).

> [!info] [AI Reasoning]
> * **Narrative change**: Confirmation of the hawkish ECB theme and a new signal that US rate‑cut expectations are extending further, reinforcing higher‑for‑longer globally.
> * **Transmission / Market Read**: Widening rate differentials favor **long EUR/USD** and **long USD/Asia FX** against low‑yielding currencies; **short gold** as real yields stay elevated.
> * **Watchpoint / Confidence**: June FOMC meeting and ECB July decision — any dovish dissent would unwind the EUR rally; Confidence: High, with multiple ECB speakers and a consensus survey backing the view.
