> [!abstract] Overview
> Iran war tensions escalated sharply as the White House denied any draft peace deal and President Trump rejected easing sanctions, while the US carried out new strikes inside Iran and a commercial vessel was hit by a likely Iranian missile in the Strait of Hormuz. Oil jumped, global bonds retreated, and Fed officials warned that the energy spike will force further tightening, placing Asian FX and equities under fresh risk-off pressure. The cross-market thread is an intensifying energy-supply shock that tightens global financial conditions, while China’s trade diplomacy offers a tentative, localized circuit breaker.
