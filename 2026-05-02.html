Separately, Trump signed an executive order expanding Cuba sanctions with global reach, authorizing penalties against foreign companies and financial institutions that do business with the island (SCMP). The Pentagon announced withdrawal of 5,000 troops from Germany (Reuters) as transatlantic relations deteriorate further. Germany responded by planning to add 75,000 soldiers to its military (SCMP). The IfW Kiel Institute estimates the auto tariff hike could cost Germany nearly $18 billion in output (Reuters).

This is classic Trump negotiation: maximize pressure before a bilateral. The EU auto tariff is a direct blow to Germany's core export sector, compounding the Iran war's energy cost shock. The US troop withdrawal from Germany signals a structural decoupling of NATO that will force European defense spending higher — fiscally expansionary for Europe but a headwind for bunds.

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Long European defense equities (Germany's 75K troop expansion is just the start). Short European auto sector on direct tariff exposure and worsening demand outlook from higher fuel costs. The EUR/USD faces headwinds from both trade escalation and the ECB's emerging hawkish tilt — a stagflationary mix that favors short EUR vs. USD on dips, not at current levels.

Separately, incoming Chair Kevin Warsh faces an immediate dilemma: "Stare down White House demands for immediate rate cuts, or stare down fellow policymakers who remain skeptical" (BBG). Powell has vowed he "won't be a shadow chair" but a clash with Warsh is "tough to avoid" (CNBC). The G-7 is now penciling in possible rate hikes as soon as June (BBG).

The Warsh transition creates a power vacuum: Warsh must assert authority over a committee that just publicly rejected the easing bias, while the White House demands cuts. The market is pricing a 2H26 cut that the data and the oil shock argue against. This is a classic credibility crisis — the Fed's word is losing signaling value.

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — The USD benefits from Fed uncertainty (rate cuts delayed = dollar support). 2Y UST yields should reprice higher as the market prices out the cut. Short 2Y UST is the cleanest expression of this theme. The steepener (long 2s30s) also works if the Fed is forced to hike short rates while long-end inflation expectations remain anchored.

The market is pricing a benign resolution that is not materializing. Each week of blockade drains strategic reserves and forces demand destruction. The US is uniquely exposed: Trump's Iran war has left America with the sharpest fuel shock in the G7 (FT), directly feeding midterm election vulnerability (MarketWatch).

[AI Reasoning] Trade Implications — Long crude (Brent/WTI) with a target of $125/bbl is the consensus trade but the risk is asymmetric to the upside if Hormuz remains closed. Long oil equities (Exxon, Chevron) as they will benefit from higher prices even as they resist Trump's pressure to drill more (FT). Short USD/INR on India's energy vulnerability. Long US natural gas as the alternative fuel for power generation.
